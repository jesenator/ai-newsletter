PITCH OF THE DAY

A Sitting Congresswoman Tried to Disavow Her Leading the Future Endorsement — Then Reversed Within Hours

Transformer's Shakeel Hashim got Rep. Val Hoyle (OR-04) on the record this week criticizing AI regulation that "harms labor or people" and saying she "did not seek out the endorsement" from Leading the Future — the $100M industry super-PAC that quietly spent $275,555 supporting her via affiliate PAC Think Big. After Transformer contacted Leading the Future for comment, Hoyle's office issued four successive revised statements, landing on language embracing the endorsement (Transformer).

Why this is the story: It's the first documented case of a Leading the Future-endorsed incumbent publicly trying to distance herself from AI-industry money — and the receipts of the walk-back are unusually clean. The piece would write itself across three threads The Information already owns: (1) FEC records on the Build American AI questionnaire pipeline funneling candidates into Leading the Future endorsements without their knowing it; (2) growing evidence (Jesse Jackson Jr.'s IL-02 loss despite a $1.43M ad buy; Alex Bores's boost in NY-12 from Public First; Schlossberg refusing AI PAC money) that AI-industry money is becoming a net liability the way crypto money never was; (3) Blue Rose polling showing AI is rising in voter salience faster than any other tracked issue. Hoyle's pirouette is the cleanest single anecdote yet for a feature on how the industry's $100M political bet is colliding with public backlash heading into the midterms.
