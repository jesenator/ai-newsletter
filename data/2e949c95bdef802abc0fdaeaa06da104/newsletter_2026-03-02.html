PITCH: OpenAI's Pentagon Deal Relies on Laws That Enabled Mass Surveillance—Not New Protections

New reporting from The Verge reveals that OpenAI agreed to "any lawful use"—the exact phrase Anthropic refused—while claiming identical red lines. Sources confirm the Pentagon "didn't budge" on bulk data collection. OpenAI's contract cites Executive Order 12333 and FISA—the same legal authorities that enabled Snowden-era mass surveillance programs.

Why this matters: Sam Altman told the public and employees that OpenAI secured the same protections Anthropic demanded. But the legal framework OpenAI accepted has a documented history of enabling exactly what Anthropic called "incompatible with democratic values." Miles Brundage (OpenAI's former head of policy research) said employees should assume "OpenAI caved + framed it as not caving, and screwed Anthropic while framing it as helping them." Worth investigating: What specific language did Anthropic demand that OpenAI declined? Will OpenAI employees push back when the distinction becomes clearer?
