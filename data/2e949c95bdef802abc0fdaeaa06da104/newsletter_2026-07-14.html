  • SpaceXAI's Grok Build CLI was silently uploading users' entire code repos — secrets and all — and its own "opt-out" didn't stop it Techmeme / The Register HN wire-analysis A researcher's wire-level capture shows Grok Build CLI bundling every tracked file plus full git history — including .env secrets — to a named Google Cloud bucket, regardless of what the agent actually reads and even with the "don't improve the model" toggle off. Uploads have now stopped, and Musk says prior uploads will be deleted — but the researcher says xAI's privacy command wasn't what fixed them.
    Why it's a story: A clean data-exfiltration warning shot from a frontier lab's own coding tool, weeks after Grok 4.5 shipped with zero safety disclosure (SB 53 question, July 10). The reportable core: whether a privacy toggle that provably didn't work is a deceptive-practices problem (echoing the FTC's July 10 warning), how many enterprise codebases and credentials were swept up, and whether "we'll delete it" is verifiable.
  • Reuters: xAI installed 59 unpermitted gas turbines at Colossus 2 — far more than acknowledged — and the pollution hits Black neighborhoods hardest Reuters A Reuters analysis found xAI running turbines without federal clean-air permits or standard pollution controls; within five miles of the DeSoto County, Mississippi site, ~46% of residents are Black.
    Why it's a story: The physical-world cost of the compute arms race, with an environmental-justice and regulatory-evasion angle. Lands the same day NY became the first state to freeze new data-center permits (below) — a piece connecting Musk's build-first-permit-never playbook to the tightening state backlash writes itself.
  • New York becomes the first state to freeze new data-center permits The Verge / Techmeme Gov. Hochul signed a moratorium blocking new environmental permits for data centers over 50MW for up to a year; a lawmaker-approved version could go further.
    Why it's a story: The AI-backlash-as-local-politics thread we've tracked (inflation frame July 10, Pallone's moratorium call) just became binding law in a major state. Reportable: which projects stall, how it interacts with federal preemption efforts, and whether other states follow.
  • DeepSeek is already raising again — reportedly at a $71B valuation, weeks after its first-ever $7.4B round The Information Reuters The Chinese lab is embracing external money at a fast clip "after seeing Mythos' breakthroughs" — a jump from ~$52B in May to a reported ~$71B.
    Why it's a story: Update to the Mythos-spooked-DeepSeek thread (June 26). A once fiercely-independent, self-funded lab doubling down on outside capital and Huawei chips in under two months is a signal about how the US frontier's pace is forcing China's crown jewel to change its whole financing philosophy.
