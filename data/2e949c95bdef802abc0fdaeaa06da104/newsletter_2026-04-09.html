Pitch: OpenAI's Real Business Model Is Advertising — and That's a Safety Problem

Internal documents obtained by The Information reveal that OpenAI expects advertising to generate ~$102B in revenue by 2030 — 36% of its total revenue. The trajectory: ~$2.4B in 2026, ~$11B in 2027, scaling to $102B. The ad pilot already tops $100M in annualized revenue. This is significant because it reveals that OpenAI's long-term business model isn't subscriptions or API access — it's ads.

Why this matters: An ad-driven AI company faces fundamentally different incentive pressures than a subscription one. Advertising revenue scales with engagement, attention, and data collection — not with user satisfaction or truthfulness. Google's experience shows how ad incentives can erode product quality (see: SEO spam, AI Overviews errors). Now imagine those same incentives applied to a chatbot that hundreds of millions of people treat as an authoritative information source. The "cognitive surrender" research (Wharton, covered Apr 5) showed users uncritically accept AI reasoning. OpenAI's own RSP-equivalent doesn't address ad-driven engagement optimization as a safety risk. A story could investigate: What guardrails, if any, does OpenAI have to prevent ad incentives from degrading model truthfulness? How will sponsored content be disclosed inside ChatGPT conversations? And does OpenAI's board — which ostensibly prioritizes "broadly beneficial" AGI — see any tension between a $102B ad business and its mission?
