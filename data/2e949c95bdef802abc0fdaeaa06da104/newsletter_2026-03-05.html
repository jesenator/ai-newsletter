PITCH: OpenAI's "Cloud-Only" Autonomous Weapons Loophole Is Meaningless—and Anthropic Knew It

A new Transformer News deep dive on autonomous weapons reveals that OpenAI's "red line" against autonomous weapons is technically meaningless. OpenAI claims it enforces its prohibition through "cloud-only deployment"—keeping models off "edge devices" inside weapons. But modern military AI architectures rely on mesh networks connecting battlefield drones to cloud data centers. Anthropic explicitly rejected this premise, telling The Atlantic the distinction is "less a wall and more of a gradient."

Why this would be impactful: OpenAI accepted the Pentagon deal by claiming a technical safeguard that AI weapons experts and Anthropic itself say doesn't work. Drones already in Ukraine use cheap on-device vision models for split-second targeting while running everything else in the cloud. A source told The Verge that even if OpenAI's tech "isn't directly involved in pulling the trigger, it could very well be powering everything leading up to that point, with no guarantee a human oversees the final step." This is the core of why Anthropic was destroyed: it refused to accept a distinction it believed was a fiction. Key questions: Did OpenAI know this distinction was meaningless? Did the Pentagon? Why is the company that held the line now blacklisted while the company that accepted a loophole got rewarded?
