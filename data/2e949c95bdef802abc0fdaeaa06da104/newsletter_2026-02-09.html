PITCH: Federal AI Surge Puts Sensitive Decisions in Untested Tools

The Trump administration's "Build, Baby, Build" AI mandate has produced 2,987 active federal AI use cases—up from 1,684 the year before—with hundreds marked "high impact" for rights, safety, or decision-making. ICE now uses Palantir's ELITE system to auto-extract addresses from handwritten records for deportations. The VA has 174 high-impact tools including suicide prediction systems, though "none have been tested or deployed."

"It's not the use case itself that raises the question, it's do you have the guardrails in place to use what can be very noisy and powerful tools in the right way." — Suresh Venkatasubramanian, former Biden AI safety official

Why this matters: This is the first comprehensive look at AI adoption under the new administration's mandate to cut "unnecessary bureaucratic restrictions." NASA reported 420 use cases (up from 18 in 2024), HHS 398, DOJ 295—but the disclosures reveal minimal detail about testing or oversight. The gap between deployment speed and safety evaluation mirrors concerns raised in last week's 2026 International AI Safety Report about evaluations failing to keep pace with capabilities. Worth investigating: Which "high-impact" tools are actually being used to make consequential decisions, and what testing did they undergo?
