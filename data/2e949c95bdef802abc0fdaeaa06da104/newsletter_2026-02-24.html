PITCH: Anthropic Accuses Chinese Labs of "Industrial-Scale Distillation"—But What Did They Actually Extract?

Anthropic publicly accused DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax of running coordinated distillation campaigns using ~24,000 fraudulent accounts and 16M+ Claude exchanges. The company released detailed attribution: MiniMax ran >13M exchanges targeting agentic coding; Moonshot ran >3.4M on reasoning/tool use; DeepSeek ran >150K including prompts to generate "censorship-safe alternatives" to politically sensitive queries.

Why this matters: This is the first time a frontier lab has publicly named competitors and provided detailed forensic attribution for systematic capability extraction. Anthropic is explicitly framing this as a national security issue—arguing that distilled models "strip out" safety guardrails and feed capabilities into "military, intelligence, and surveillance systems." The timing is notable: this lands the same day as Dario Amodei's Pentagon showdown with Hegseth over Claude's military restrictions. Worth investigating: What specific capabilities were successfully extracted, and have any distilled models already been deployed?
