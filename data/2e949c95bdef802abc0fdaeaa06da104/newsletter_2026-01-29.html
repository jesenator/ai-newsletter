PITCH: Amazon Found CSAM in AI Training Data—Won't Say Where It Came From

Bloomberg reports Amazon reported hundreds of thousands of pieces of suspected CSAM to NCMEC in 2025—but won't disclose the source. Child safety officials say Amazon provided no information about where the material originated.

"The tech giant reported hundreds of thousands of cases of suspected child sexual abuse material, but won't say where it came from."

Why this matters: This raises alarming questions about the state of AI training data across the industry. If Amazon found this much CSAM in its training datasets, what have other labs found? The refusal to disclose the source—whether a public dataset like LAION, scraped web data, or something else—prevents the broader AI safety community from addressing the problem. This is exactly the kind of story that connects AI development practices to real-world harms. Worth investigating: Which datasets contained the CSAM? Are other labs scanning their training data? And why the secrecy about the source?
