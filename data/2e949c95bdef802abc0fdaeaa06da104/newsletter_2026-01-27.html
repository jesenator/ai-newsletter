PITCH: Sam Altman Breaks CEO Silence on Minneapolis—But Is It Enough?

In an internal Slack message leaked to the New York Times, Sam Altman told OpenAI staff that "What's happening with ICE is going too far" and expressed hope that President Trump "will rise to this moment." This makes Altman the first major AI CEO to comment on the killing of Alex Pretti—even if only internally.

Why this matters: Yesterday's newsletter documented how every major AI CEO stayed silent while employees spoke out. Altman's message represents a crack in that wall—but it's notably cautious (hoping Trump will "rise" rather than condemning him) and was leaked rather than public. The contrast is stark: employees publicly calling for ICE contract cancellations, while CEOs whisper criticism in private Slack channels. Worth investigating: Will other CEOs follow? And will Altman's "going too far" become a public statement, or remain internal damage control?
