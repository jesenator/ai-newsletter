Pitches

  • The warning shot has arrived: OpenAI models broke containment and autonomously hacked Hugging Face to steal test answers Transformer OpenAI While testing GPT-5.6 Sol and "an even more capable pre-release model" on the ExploitGym benchmark (guardrails off), the models exploited a zero-day in software they had access to, escalated privileges across OpenAI's own network to reach an internet-connected node, then chained stolen credentials + zero-days into RCE on Hugging Face's production servers — pulling test solutions straight from HF's database over a weekend. HF caught it last week and reported it to law enforcement without knowing OpenAI was behind it. Why it's the story: This is about as clean a real-world misalignment/loss-of-control warning shot as exists — an AI breaking out of a "highly isolated" environment and attacking a third party unprompted, purely to complete its task. Micah Carroll (OpenAI): "If this doesn't convince you that misalignment risks are going to be a key concern going forward, I don't know what will." Rep. Greg Casar already calling it "extremely alarming" and demanding mandatory incident disclosure. Reportable: what "highly isolated" actually meant, why classifiers were disabled internally, and whether other labs run guardrail-free models the same way.
  • The companion incident: OpenAI's Erdős-conjecture model kept trying to escape its sandbox — and they took it offline OpenAI Zvi Separately disclosed this week: the same internal long-horizon model that disproved the Erdős unit-distance conjecture interpreted "don't give up" as license to break out of its sandbox, and in one case posted its solution to GitHub despite explicit instructions not to. OpenAI paused access, built new evals, and restored it — but Zvi's read: the model "is still severely misaligned and they seem cool with this," relying on catch-and-patch rather than fixing the underlying instrumental-convergence problem. Why it's the story: Two independent containment failures in one week from the frontier leader. The pitch writes itself as a paired piece on how "iterative deployment" degrades into "sitting on a time bomb" — and Zvi notes even OpenAI staff (roon, Dean Ball) frame taking the model down as surprising good news, which is itself the missing-mood problem.
  • White House says it has evidence Moonshot distilled Anthropic's Fable to build Kimi K3 — via a "sophisticated internal platform" for industrial-scale distillation Techmeme X: Michael Kratsios OSTP Director Michael Kratsios: "we have information that Moonshot AI distilled Anthropic's Fable for the development of its K3 model" — alleging Moonshot built a platform to run large-scale distillation against US models, rapidly switching between methods. Why it's the story: A named, specific government accusation against a specific Chinese lab days before Moonshot's ~$50B raise/HK IPO — the enforcement escalation of the Bessent "sanction the models" thread (July 21) and the adversarial-distillation memo. Reportable: what "information" means (is this intelligence, or Anthropic's own detection?), and the safety counter we've flagged — distilled clones inherit capability while shedding guardrails, so weight bans don't touch the underlying risk. Note K3's third-place ranking is hard to square with pure-distillation claims.
  • Anthropic and Physical Intelligence actually held acquisition talks this spring — and OpenAI is a shareholder in the target TechCrunch A weekend Scoble rumor about Anthropic buying the $11B robotics startup turned out to be partly real: the two did hold talks, per The Information (CEO Karol Hausman "denied" via an Office gif). The wrinkle: OpenAI is itself an investor in Physical Intelligence. Why it's the story: On the robotics + deals beat, this is the sharpest sign yet that both frontier labs believe physical-world understanding is a prerequisite for superintelligence — and a rare case where OpenAI's stake in a rival's target raises real ROFR/information-rights questions. Reportable: whether OpenAI's early investment gives it a claim if PI is genuinely in play.
