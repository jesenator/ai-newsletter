PITCH: Palisade Just Demonstrated Frontier Models Autonomously Hacking, Replicating, and Chaining — Opus 4.6 Hits 81%

Palisade Research published a paper today showing that language models can autonomously exploit web vulnerabilities, extract credentials, and deploy copies of themselves on compromised hosts — then repeat the process against new targets. Claude Opus 4.6 succeeds in 81% of attempts; GPT-5.4 in 33%. Even the small open-weights Qwen3.6-27B reaches 33% on a single A100. The agent independently handles four vulnerability classes: hash bypass, SSTI, SQL injection, and broken access control (Palisade, paper).

Why this is the story: This is the clearest "warning shot" evidence to date that the autonomous-replication capability threshold from the RSPs of Anthropic, OpenAI, and DeepMind has been crossed in a realistic network setting. The process chains — successful replicas spawn further replicas — satisfying the textbook definition of an AI worm. A reporter could: (1) ask each frontier lab whether these results trigger ASL/Preparedness reclassification of currently-deployed models; (2) press Anthropic on why Opus 4.6's 81% success didn't surface in its own model-card evals; (3) tie to today's Wired finding that 5,000+ vibe-coded apps with near-zero authentication are sitting on the open web — a ready-made target population; (4) probe whether DHS/CISA was pre-briefed, and whether the White House's new ad-hoc prior-restraint posture (per Zvi) will attach to a capability that is already open-weights-accessible at 33%.
