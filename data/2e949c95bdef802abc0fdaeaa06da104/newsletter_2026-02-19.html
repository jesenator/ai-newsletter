PITCH: South Korean Woman Used ChatGPT to Plan Drug Murders—A Warning Shot for AI Risk

A 21-year-old woman in Seoul has been charged with murdering two men after police discovered she repeatedly asked ChatGPT how to kill with sleeping pills mixed in alcohol. Queries included "Can you die from mixing sleeping pills with alcohol?", "How many do you need to take for it to be dangerous?", and "Could it kill someone?"

Why this matters: This is a real-world "warning shot"—an early example of AI being used in the planning of violent crimes. Unlike the Las Vegas bomber case, where ChatGPT provided general advice, here the suspect allegedly used the AI to calibrate lethal dosages. She adjusted doses higher after her first victim survived. This case demonstrates how AI can lower barriers to premeditated violence and will likely fuel regulatory debates about safety filters. Worth investigating: What exactly did ChatGPT tell her, and did it refuse any queries?
