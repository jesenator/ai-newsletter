Top Pitches

  • Trump's former AI adviser Dean Ball is joining OpenAI to lead a new "Strategic Futures" team on frontier policy and internal governance Axios via Techmeme Ball — architect of much of the administration's early AI posture and a vocal critic of the Fable shutdown ("the government just made my world dumber") — moves from the FAI/White House orbit straight into OpenAI to handle "frontier AI policy and internal governance."
    Why it's the story: A marquee revolving-door hire that puts one of the most influential outside voices on US AI policy inside the company filing for a retail-heavy mega-IPO. Ball spent months publicly litigating preemption, the prior-restraint EO, and the export-control regime that just nuked a rival's model — now he'll be shaping OpenAI's line on all of it. Reportable: what "internal governance" means (does he touch RSP/preparedness decisions?), whether he recuses on matters he worked on in government, and how this reads against OpenAI's denials around Leading the Future.
  • Waymo recalls nearly 3,900 robotaxis off highways — and a safety expert calls it the "third strike" on Waymo's failed quick-fixes TechCrunch via Techmeme Phil Koopman The NHTSA filing reveals a pattern, not one incident: 13+ events of cars entering and driving at speed into closed freeway construction zones across April–May, with Waymo continuing operations after each "quick fix" until a viral police-chase video forced action. No remedy is yet identified.
    Why it's the story: A concrete real-world-risk warning shot on the most-deployed autonomous fleet. Koopman's framing — that this is the third recall (after flooded roads and passing stopped school buses) where tactical mitigations failed and a recall came only after press attention — raises a sharp safety-culture question and a possible downside of end-to-end ML: "Why is it so hard to get these fixes to work?" Reportable: were the earlier quick-fixes reported to NHTSA, and what undisclosed incidents exist without a news hook?
  • Manus's early Chinese backers plan to buy the startup back from Meta — at the same $2B Meta paid The Information Investing.com HSG, ZhenFund and Tencent are organizing the buyback as Meta completes an operational separation it began after Beijing's demands. Meta appears poised to fully unwind the deal at cost.
    Why it's the story: A rare reverse-acquisition that crystallizes how US–China decoupling is forcibly redrawing AI M&A. A US hyperscaler is being made to sell a marquee agent startup back to its Chinese origin investors — the mirror image of the Fable export-control saga. Reportable: who pressured whom (CFIUS vs. Beijing), whether Meta keeps any IP/talent, and what this signals for other cross-border AI deals.
