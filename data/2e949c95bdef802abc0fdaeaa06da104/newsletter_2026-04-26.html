PITCH: The Circularity Just Broke Through $200B — Google's $40B Anthropic Commitment Completes a Capital Loop That Regulators Have No Framework For

Google committed up to $40B in additional Anthropic investment Friday (CNBC) — $10B now, $30B to follow. In the same 7-day window: Amazon pledged $25B more to Anthropic with a $100B AWS spending-back commitment; OpenAI's $110B round (Amazon $50B, Nvidia $30B, SoftBank $30B) continues to flow back to those same backers as compute spend; Nvidia rolled out OpenAI Codex firmwide to 10,000+ employees (MoneyControl); Oracle closed $16B in bonds for an OpenAI-dedicated Michigan data center.

Why this is the story: Anthropic now has capital commitments from all three hyperscalers — Amazon ($33B total), Google ($40B+), and Microsoft ($30B in cloud) — while positioning itself as the safety-first PBC. A reporter could (1) map the full set of reciprocal commitments into one diagram, (2) ask whether a single frontier lab can remain structurally independent when its three largest investors are also its three largest customers and competitors, (3) probe whether Anthropic's board has any governance mechanism to prevent hyperscaler pressure on safety decisions — especially given Amazon also owns $50B of OpenAI, and (4) investigate whether the SEC, FTC, or CMA has any framework for "circular revenue" disclosure. This week's deals may have quietly ended the fiction that frontier labs are independent companies.
