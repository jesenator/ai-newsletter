PITCH: OpenAI's New Whistleblower Policy Could Set a Dangerous Standard

OpenAI quietly expanded its whistleblower policy to 13 pages—now the most comprehensive among frontier AI companies—but AIWI's assessment reveals critical gaps that other labs may copy.

Key findings from AIWI's evaluation: 8 of 13 recommendations "significantly or partially addressed," including creating a dedicated Compliance function and treating "AI Safety Concerns" as a distinct category. But structural problems remain: internal policies don't create legally enforceable rights unless explicitly stated, and research shows 90%+ of corporate retaliation cases stem from internal disclosures.

Why this matters: This is the first independent assessment of a frontier lab's whistleblower infrastructure. The National Whistleblower Center advises insiders to "generally avoid internal channels"—yet OpenAI's expanded policy could become the template for the industry. Worth investigating: Will other labs copy the improvements or the gaps? And does this policy actually protect employees who raise AI safety concerns, or does it route them into company-controlled processes that increase retaliation risk?
