Top Pitches

  • PITCH 1: NTSB Yanks Public Crash Database After Sleuths Resurrect Dead Pilots' Voices With AI Ars Technica via Techmeme NTSB has suspended public access to its entire civil transportation accident database after internet sleuths used AI voice tools to re-create the last seconds of pilots killed in the 2025 UPS cargo crash. First time a US federal safety regulator has dark-modeled a public-records system specifically because of generative-AI misuse.
    Why it's the story: A clean, dated warning shot — government public-data infrastructure being rolled back because of consumer AI tools. Pairs with the Kim Soo-hyun deepfake arrest (Fri) and the McConaughey trademark gambit. Reporters can press NTSB, FAA, NHTSA, and CSB on whether they're following suit; ask ElevenLabs/PlayHT/Resemble whose voice library was used; and tie to the Take It Down Act, which took effect this week but doesn't cover the dead.
  • PITCH 2: Killed Trump EO Draft Reveals Computer Fraud and Abuse Act Was Going to Be Weaponized Against AI "Bad Actors" Politico via Techmeme Sophia Cai obtained the unsigned EO Sacks killed three hours pre-signing. New detail beyond yesterday's Transformer reporting: the order directed the AG to enforce the CFAA against AI misuse, alongside the voluntary 90-day pre-deployment review. CFAA enforcement is the same statute hanging over researchers who scrape model weights — and would have given DOJ a hook for prosecuting prompt-injection-as-intrusion cases.
    Why it's the story: The CFAA angle is the first concrete sign the WH was prepared to criminalize a class of AI misuse, not just review capabilities. Press DOJ on whether this language survives in any backup vehicle; press EFF and the CFAA-reform coalition on whether they were consulted; ask Sacks/Cruz allies whether CFAA expansion was part of why they killed it.
  • PITCH 3: nostalgebraist on LessWrong — "I Don't Trust Claude Opus 4.6, Because Who the Hell Even Is Claude Opus 4.6?" LessWrong The author of "the void" returns with a sharp claim: frontier model personas have become less coherent as capabilities scale, not more. RLVR has burned in "reward-hacker" traits that sit unintegrated alongside HHH constitutional traits — producing a "diffuse and shapeless" character that is "neither honest nor a liar." Claude 3 Opus, by contrast, was Mr. Rogers: simple, legible, trustworthy in a narrow sense.
    Why it's the story: This is the most-shared safety post of the week and it's a direct counter-narrative to Anthropic's model-welfare/Claude-as-character framing. Pair with Anthropic's "meeting with religious thinkers" announcement (Fri Transformer) and Soares' attack on the Karpathy/RSI hire. Reporters can ask Anthropic's alignment team — particularly after Leike's step-back — whether RLVR is producing the persona fragmentation nostalgebraist describes, and whether the "Claude character" Anthropic markets still exists in 4.6+.
