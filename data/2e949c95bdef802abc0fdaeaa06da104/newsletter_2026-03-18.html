Pitch: AI Labs Talk Safety, But Their Headcounts Tell a Different Story

  • Parmy Olson's new Bloomberg Opinion investigation reveals that the total number of people working on AI safety across all major labs "might fit on a single transatlantic flight." Safety-oriented headcount is "a rounding error" compared to capability investment.
  • Why this matters: This is the kind of quantitative exposé that forces a concrete conversation. Labs have made sweeping safety commitments — Anthropic's RSP, OpenAI's Preparedness Framework, DeepMind's Frontier Safety Framework — but if the teams responsible for implementing them are tiny relative to the capability teams, those commitments are structurally hollow. This is especially damning in context: Anthropic just weakened its RSP (newsletter 3/13), and OpenAI is racing to take over Anthropic's Pentagon contracts. The piece also lands as the DOJ filing today argues Anthropic's safety restrictions make it an untrustworthy military partner. The implication: labs are understaffed on safety even as their models are deployed in targeting pipelines and classified government work. A follow-up story could quantify the exact ratios at each lab and compare them to stated commitments.
