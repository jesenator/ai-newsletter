PITCH: Claude Was Used in the Venezuela Raid—Did Anthropic Violate Its Own Usage Policy?

The Wall Street Journal reported Saturday that Anthropic's Claude was used by the US military in the January operation to capture Nicolás Maduro. Claude was deployed via Anthropic's partnership with Palantir. The raid killed 83 people according to Venezuela's defense ministry.

Anthropic's usage policies explicitly prohibit using Claude "to support violence, design weapons or carry out surveillance." A spokesperson declined to confirm the deployment but said any use must comply with their policies.

Why this matters: This is the first confirmed use of a frontier AI model in a classified military operation that resulted in deaths. Anthropic built its brand on responsible AI development—Dario Amodei has publicly warned against AI in "autonomous lethal operations." If Claude was used for targeting, logistics, or operational planning in a raid that killed 83 people, did Anthropic know? Did they consent? The company that just closed a $35B round at $380B valuation may have violated its stated principles in the most consequential way possible. Worth investigating: What exactly did Claude do, and what did Anthropic know beforehand?
