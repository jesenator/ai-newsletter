Top Pitches

  • PITCH 1: Pro-Iran hackers used Meta's AI support bot to deface the Obama White House and US Space Force Instagram accounts — and MFA blocked the exploit Krebs on Security BBC Big update on yesterday's 404 Media story. Krebs reports the attack defaced the Obama White House and Chief Master Sergeant of the US Space Force accounts with pro-Iranian imagery, that Telegram videos circulating since May 31 documented the VPN-+-bot exploit, and that SMS MFA was sufficient to block it — hackers told viewers the exploit "failed to work against any accounts that had MFA enabled." Lumen's Black Lotus Labs: "AI chatbots create interesting new attack surface, and we're likely going to see a lot more of these kinds of attacks." Why it's the story: This is no longer an account-recovery quirk — it's the first nation-state-adjacent defacement campaign that used a Big Tech AI agent as the attack surface, hitting US government accounts. Worth pressing Meta on which Llama variant powers the bot, whether prompt-injection testing was done before deploying it to account-recovery flows, and what the disclosure timeline was to CISA given the .gov-adjacent victims.
  • PITCH 2: Microsoft ships MXC — an OS-level sandbox for AI agents — and uses a live demo of an agent trying to delete a developer's files to sell it VentureBeat Microsoft Execution Containers (MXC) is a policy-driven primitive baked into Windows + WSL that lets admins declare what an agent can touch, enforced at the kernel. OpenAI, Nvidia, Manus, and Nous Research are launch partners. Every agent gets a strong identity via Entra; demo had OpenClaw instructed to "delete all the files on my desktop" — sandbox blocked it. Why it's the story: After two weeks of agent-supply-chain warning shots (BadHost/Starlette, the @redhat-cloud-services npm worm that targeted MCP packages, the Meta-bot defacement above), Microsoft is establishing the de facto enterprise standard for agent containment — and it bypasses MCP's auth model entirely by binding to Entra identity. Worth pressing: does MXC apply to MCP servers running inside it? Is Anthropic notable by its absence from the launch partner list? And what's the policy-bypass attack surface — Sysmon-style logging gaps already exist.
  • PITCH 3: Thrive Holdings commits $1B to roll up local accounting firms and replace staff with self-improving AI — claims "98% data-entry accuracy" in live production Techmeme/Forbes Dec context Josh Kushner's Thrive spinoff is deploying $1B via subsidiary Current to acquire CPA firms outright, with OpenAI as an equity partner (announced December). Forbes reports self-improving models hitting up to 98% data-entry accuracy in "live-fire testing" at firms already acquired. Why it's the story: This is the cleanest test case yet for the "AI eats the BPO/professional-services workforce" thesis — not selling software to accountants, but buying their firms and replacing them. Pair with Pace's $46M Series B for insurance back-office agents (last week) and Mecka AI/Human Archive raising $60M+/$8.2M to film humans training their replacements. Reportable angles: (1) what happens to acquired CPAs' employment contracts, (2) PCAOB/state-board posture on AI-driven attest work, (3) what "self-improving" means operationally on legally-binding tax filings, (4) does the Travelers/OpenAI claim-assistant deal (also announced today) use the same architecture?
