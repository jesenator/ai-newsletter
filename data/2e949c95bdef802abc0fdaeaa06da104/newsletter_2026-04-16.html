Pitch: Google Is Quietly Reversing Its Post-Maven Military Stance — Negotiating to Deploy Gemini in Pentagon Classified Settings

  • The Information Techmeme Google is negotiating an agreement with the Department of Defense to deploy Gemini AI models in classified settings, reversing its longstanding reluctance to engage in military AI since the 2018 Project Maven walkout. The proposed terms allow the Pentagon to use Gemini for "all lawful purposes," with contract language preventing domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons without human oversight — mirroring the deal OpenAI struck with the Pentagon earlier this year.
  • Why this story matters: This is a watershed moment. Google's employee revolt over Project Maven in 2018 defined the tech-military relationship for years and set the template for the "responsible AI" movement. Now Google is going further than Maven ever went — classified deployment, not just unclassified analysis. The timing is telling: it comes as Anthropic is being punished by the Pentagon (labeled a supply-chain risk for refusing to loosen safety guardrails), and just weeks after Google began providing unclassified AI agents to Defense personnel. A story could investigate: Did Anthropic's standoff create the opening for Google? Are Google employees aware of and aligned with this reversal? And what does it mean for AI safety when the company that once defined "don't be evil in defense" is now pursuing classified military AI contracts?
