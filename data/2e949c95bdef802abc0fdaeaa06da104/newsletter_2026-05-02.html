PITCH: OpenAI Execs and a16z Are Quietly Funding Influencers to Push Anti-China AI Messaging to Millions — Without Disclosure

Taylor Lorenz reports in Wired that Build American AI — a dark-money nonprofit tied to the $140M Leading the Future super PAC — is paying TikTok/Instagram lifestyle influencers up to $5,000 per video to amplify "Team USA" anti-China AI messaging. Sample scripts handed to creators warn that if China wins, "China gets personal data from me and my kids." Supporters of Leading the Future include Greg Brockman, Joe Lonsdale, a16z, and Perplexity. Creators label posts "ads" but don't disclose the funder. SM4, the agency running it, directly pitched Lorenz.

Why this is the story: This is the concrete execution layer beneath the super-PAC wars The Information has been covering — a direct line from OpenAI's president's pocket to the FYP of 1.4M-follower mom influencers, with no disclosure. It pairs with today's NYT reporting that Ripple's Chris Larsen will spend $3.5M supporting Alex Bores against Leading the Future's attacks, and with a Midas Project report that an AI-content news site pushing industry talking points is also linked to Leading the Future (the PAC blamed a "third-party vendor"). A reporter could (1) obtain SM4's full creator roster and rate card, (2) map Build American AI's 501(c)(4) donor structure to named OpenAI/a16z execs, (3) ask the FTC whether "#ad" without sponsor disclosure violates endorsement guides for political messaging, and (4) tie it to OpenAI's formal statement that it has "no corporate affiliation" — a claim that strains credulity when its president is a listed backer.
