Pitch of the Day

OpenAI Just Endorsed Third-Party Audits in a State AI Bill — A First, and a Direct Repudiation of Its Own Position Four Weeks Ago In written testimony to the Illinois Senate this week, OpenAI's Caitlin Niedermeyer formally disavowed the liability safe harbor in SB 3444 — the bill OpenAI endorsed in April that the bill's sponsor described as "an initiative of OpenAI" and LawAI called a contender for "worst state AI bill of all time" (Transformer). OpenAI then endorsed SB 315 — modeled on California SB 53 and NY's RAISE Act, but going further by requiring third-party audits of frontier safety frameworks, a provision industry had previously stripped from RAISE.

Why this is the story: This is the first time any frontier lab has endorsed binding third-party audits in a state bill. Combined with OpenAI distancing itself from Leading the Future, supporting an international AI governance body, and calling for CAISI to develop auditing standards — all within a week — it suggests a wholesale repositioning by Chris Lehane's policy shop. A reporter could (1) obtain the unreported Niedermeyer testimony and reconcile it line-by-line against OpenAI's April SB 3444 endorsement letter; (2) ask whether Anthropic's parallel endorsement of SB 315 was coordinated; (3) press whether OpenAI's stance on the federal Obernolte–Trahan bill (expected post-Memorial Day) will preserve third-party audits or trade them away for state preemption; (4) tie to today's news that the CAISI pre-deployment testing announcement vanished from the agency's website amid an intelligence-community turf war over evals. The "race to the top" or "reading the room"? Either way, the AI-policy Overton window just moved.
