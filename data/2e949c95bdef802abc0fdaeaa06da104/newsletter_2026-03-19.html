Pitch: NHTSA Escalates Tesla FSD Probe to "Engineering Analysis" — A Key Step Toward Potential Forced Recall of 3.2M Vehicles

  • Today NHTSA announced it is escalating its investigation of Tesla's Full Self-Driving software to an "engineering analysis" — the final step before the agency can order a recall or take enforcement action. The probe covers ~3.2 million vehicles across multiple model years. It focuses on FSD's failure to detect system degradation and driver inattention, triggered by crashes in poor visibility. (WSJ, Bloomberg)
  • Why this matters: An engineering analysis is the most serious form of NHTSA investigation and is the immediate precursor to mandatory action. This is happening while Musk just claimed a fatal Cybertruck crash wasn't Autopilot's fault, while a federal judge upheld a $243M verdict against Tesla in another Autopilot death, and while Musk in January declared FSD is "100% unsupervised." The probe landing now — as the Uber-Rivian robotaxi deal signals the industry betting billions on Level 4 autonomy — creates a direct contrast: one company is drawing massive regulatory heat on its existing self-driving claims while others rush to scale autonomous vehicles. If NHTSA issues a recall, it could set precedent for how aggressively the US regulates autonomous driving software across the industry.
