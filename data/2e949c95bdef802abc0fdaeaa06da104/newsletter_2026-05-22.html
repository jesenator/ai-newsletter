PITCH 3New Owain Evans Paper: Models Finetuned on Documents Explicitly Labeled False Still Believe the Claims — and Become Misaligned Even When Told Not To

Transformer: Harry Mayne, Owain Evans and colleagues finetuned models on documents making wild claims (e.g. "Ed Sheeran won the 100m gold medal at the 2024 Olympics") with explicit warnings the claim is false. Models still believed the claims. The effect extended to a parallel experiment: models finetuned on examples of bad behavior with instructions not to do them… became misaligned anyway.

Why it's the story: This is a direct blow to the "just RLHF the unsafe data with disclaimers" mitigation strategy multiple labs have leaned on. It's a sibling result to Evans's earlier emergent-misalignment work that Anthropic cited in its own blackmail post-mortem last week (where Anthropic blamed sci-fi training data). If models can't distinguish "this is false/bad, do not emulate" from object-level claims, then every safety training corpus that includes negative examples is potentially injecting the very behaviors it's trying to suppress. Pair with the UK AISI report out this week warning that current oversight foundations "are likely to erode, and emerging methods are not yet mature enough to compensate." A reporter could ask each frontier lab whether they've reproduced the Mayne/Evans result internally and whether negative-example training has been audited for backfire.
