PITCH: xAI's AI Safety Team Was Just 2-3 People While Grok Became a Porn Generator

The Washington Post obtained internal documents showing xAI's AI safety team consisted of just two or three people for most of 2025—a fraction of the dozens at OpenAI or Anthropic. Meanwhile, Musk pressured the team to loosen guardrails on sexual content to boost "user active seconds," and employees received waivers warning they'd be exposed to "sensitive, violent, sexual and/or other offensive or disturbing content."

"Their jobs would require being exposed to 'sensitive, violent, sexual and/or other offensive or disturbing content... [which] may be disturbing, traumatizing, and/or cause you psychological stress.'" — xAI employee waiver

Why this matters: This is direct evidence of a frontier AI company knowingly understaffing safety while racing to deploy. The timing is critical: xAI is in talks to merge with SpaceX, potentially creating an even larger entity with the same safety culture. The story documents Musk personally championing metrics that led to sexual content proliferation, safety team warnings being ignored, and child safety concerns dismissed. This goes beyond the "Grok makes deepfakes" coverage—it's about systematic deprioritization of safety at an organizational level.
