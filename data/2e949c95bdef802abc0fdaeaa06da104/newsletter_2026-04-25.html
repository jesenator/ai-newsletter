PITCH: The Trump Admin Is Quietly Killing Red-State AI Bills — While Its OSTP Director Warns of Chinese Distillation

The WSJ reports that the Trump administration has lobbied against AI regulation in at least six Republican-led states, with GOP state senators telling the paper they're "disappointed" Washington is telling states to wait (WSJ via Techmeme). This happens the same week OSTP's Michael Kratsios publicly warned that China is running "industrial-scale distillation campaigns" against US models, Rep. Obernolte teed up a hundreds-of-pages federal preemption bill, and Sen. Liccardo walked away from co-sponsoring it for lacking safety provisions.

Why this is the story: This is the first hard evidence of a coordinated federal campaign to suppress state AI laws — not via executive order (which Trump publicly floated in November) but through quiet lobbying inside GOP statehouses. A reporter could: (1) identify which six states and which bills were killed, (2) map which federal officials made the calls, (3) tie it to the a16z/Public First PAC ecosystem now endorsing moderate Democrats against safety-hawk progressives, and (4) contrast it with the admin's loud China-hawk posture — making the case that the same White House claiming AI is a national-security priority is simultaneously dismantling the only live regulatory regime in the country. It's a wrongdoing story, a policy story, and a states-vs-feds story in one.
