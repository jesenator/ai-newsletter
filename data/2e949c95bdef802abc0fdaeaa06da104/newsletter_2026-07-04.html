  • Midjourney moves to pry open Hollywood's own AI playbook — an "unclean hands" gambit in the studio copyright war Variety/Techmeme VP Land Midjourney is asking Judge Kronstadt to overturn a magistrate's limit and force Disney, Universal and Warner Bros. to hand over internal AI business plans, training data, model weights and board presentations — arguing the studios "cannot sue over AI training while running comparable AI programs themselves" (fair use + unclean hands). Why it's the story: this is the discovery fight that could make Hollywood's own behind-the-camera AI adoption admissible evidence — a rare offensive move by an AI defendant, distinct from the NYT/local-news suits we've tracked. Reportable: what the studios actually run in VFX/production pipelines, and whether "unclean hands" becomes the template defense for every gen-AI copyright case. Note Disney is itself building Disney+ gen-AI tools.
  • Robotics capital is going vertical even as its own godfather calls LLMs "hopeless" for the field TNGlobal TechCrunch Storyboard18 A frenzy day: Singapore's dConstruct raised a $125M Series A for GPS-denied autonomy; Korea's FASSTO closed a first pre-IPO tranche; Unitree cleared its $619M STAR Market IPO; and ex-DeepMind trio's quant-AI EquiLibre hit a $500M valuation. Meanwhile Yann LeCun (now at $1B+ seed AMI Labs) reiterates that "LLMs are largely hopeless for robotics" and bets on JEPA world models instead. Why it's the story: the money is pouring into embodied AI on the premise that current architectures transfer to the physical world — exactly the premise LeCun, Fei-Fei Li's World Labs, and Oxford's mechanistic-world-model camp dispute. A piece pitting the funding thesis against the architecture skeptics is the under-covered fault line beneath the humanoid boom, with clear implications for which of these nine-figure bets survive.
