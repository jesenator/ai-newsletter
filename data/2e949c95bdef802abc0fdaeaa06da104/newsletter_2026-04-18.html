Pitch: Anthropic Is Delaying Mythos's Wider Release Because It Can't Keep Claude Online — While the White House Scrambles for Access

  • The FT reports that Anthropic is holding off from a wider Mythos release because it cannot reliably serve it to customers, having suffered multiple outages in recent weeks. This comes the same day Dario Amodei met with White House Chief of Staff Susie Wiles and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent in what the White House called a "productive and constructive" meeting. The government is reportedly desperate to gain access to Mythos for federal cybersecurity.
  • Why this would be an impactful story: The most powerful AI model in the world — one that UK AISI confirmed can autonomously compromise corporate networks — is bottlenecked not by safety concerns or policy disagreements, but by infrastructure problems. Anthropic shipped 14 product releases in March alongside 5 outages. This raises a pointed question: is Anthropic growing too fast to safely operate the systems it builds? A story could investigate: How severe are the outages? Is Anthropic's infrastructure team understaffed relative to the scale of models it's deploying? Are existing Glasswing partners already experiencing reliability issues with Mythos access? And does the fact that the White House is essentially negotiating for access to a model that can't stay online change the calculus around whether Anthropic can responsibly expand deployment?
