Pitch: OpenAI Kills Sora — and Pivots to Robotics

OpenAI abruptly shut down Sora, its AI video-generation app, and cancelled a $1 billion deal with Disney — saying it will refocus on robotics "that will help people solve real-world, physical tasks." Reuters reports no money had actually changed hands with Disney. Analyst Thomas Husson called Sora "a resource black hole" with "limited monetisation." OpenAI says it will apply video-generation tech to training robots instead.

Why this matters: This is a strategic earthquake. OpenAI built Sora into one of the most hyped AI products in history, got Disney to license Mickey Mouse and Yoda for it, and then killed it all within three months. The real story is what this tells us about where OpenAI thinks value lies: not in creative media tools but in physical AI. This pivot mirrors COO Simo's recent "no side quests" memo. It also raises safety questions — OpenAI struggled to prevent Sora from generating nonconsensual imagery and misinformation, and analyst Henry Ajder suggests the shutdown was partly to "minimise the associated risks" ahead of a potential IPO. A story could investigate: did OpenAI pull the plug because Sora was becoming an IP and safety liability that threatened its path to going public? And what does "we're pivoting to robotics" actually mean for a company with no robotics team?

  • BBC Variety Reuters Disney statement: "We respect OpenAI's decision to exit the video generation business." ChatGPT image tools remain; video generation fully discontinued. Seedance (ByteDance) and other competitors remain.
