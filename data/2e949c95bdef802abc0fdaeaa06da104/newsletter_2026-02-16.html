PITCH: Pentagon Threatens to Blacklist Anthropic Over Weapons Restrictions

  • Hegseth "close" to designating Anthropic a "supply chain risk" Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth is nearing a decision to sever Pentagon business with Anthropic—a penalty typically reserved for foreign adversaries—after months of tense negotiations. Any company wanting military contracts would have to cut ties with Anthropic. The $200M DoW contract is at risk. Axios Economic Times
  • Anthropic refused to lift restrictions on mass surveillance and autonomous weapons The Pentagon demanded unrestricted use of Claude for "all lawful purposes"—including weapons development, intelligence gathering, and battlefield operations. Anthropic specifically refused to allow mass domestic surveillance or fully autonomous weapon systems. Google, OpenAI, and xAI reportedly agreed to remove similar safeguards.
Why this matters: This is the first time a frontier AI company has faced existential government pressure over safety restrictions. Anthropic built Claude into classified Pentagon systems—including reportedly the Maduro raid—and now faces punishment for maintaining guardrails the company publicly committed to. If Hegseth proceeds, the message to other AI labs is clear: safety commitments are a liability when governments want unrestricted AI. Worth investigating: Are OpenAI and Google's relaxed terms documented anywhere, and did Anthropic's Palantir partnership include guarantees about usage limits?
