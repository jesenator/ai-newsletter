Pitch: OpenAI Is Quietly Briefing Spy Agencies on a Cyber-Offense Model — While Its Safety Commitments Go Unmonitored

  • Axios/Reuters OpenAI has been briefing U.S. federal agencies, state governments, and Five Eyes intelligence allies on the capabilities of GPT-5.4-Cyber over the past week. The model, launched April 15 with a "Trusted Access" program for vetted defenders, is now being demonstrated to intelligence partners in what amounts to a roadshow for governments. The dual-track design — a restricted public version with strong guardrails and a permissive variant with "lowered refusal boundaries" for vetted users — means the same model exists in two radically different safety configurations.
  • Why this matters: When Anthropic restricted Mythos access to ~40 organizations and refused to loosen guardrails for the Pentagon, it was labeled a supply-chain risk. OpenAI is taking the opposite approach: proactively pitching its cyber model to intelligence agencies with permissive settings. A story could investigate: What exactly does the "lowered refusal boundary" version of GPT-5.4-Cyber allow that the public version doesn't? Who decides what counts as "vetted"? Is there any independent oversight of the Trusted Access program — or is OpenAI self-certifying who gets the keys? And does OpenAI's eagerness to brief Five Eyes allies while Anthropic fights the Pentagon signal a new race to the bottom on cyber-model access?
