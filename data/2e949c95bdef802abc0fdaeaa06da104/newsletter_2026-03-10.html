PITCH: Google and OpenAI Employees Just Told Their Own Companies Their Pentagon Deals Are Dangerous

On Monday, 30+ employees from OpenAI and Google—including DeepMind chief scientist Jeff Dean—filed an amicus brief supporting Anthropic's lawsuit against the Pentagon. The brief says the Pentagon's decision to blacklist Anthropic "chills professional debate on the benefits and risks of frontier AI systems" and that "contractual and technological requirements that AI developers impose on the use of their systems represent a vital safeguard against their catastrophic misuse."

Why this is impactful: These are employees at the companies that just took Anthropic's place. OpenAI signed the Pentagon contract Anthropic rejected; Google's Gemini is deployed across DoD's 3M workforce. Their own technical staff is now publicly stating in federal court that the safety restrictions Anthropic demanded—and that their employers did not—are necessary. This creates internal contradictions for both companies and validates Anthropic's position from inside the labs that benefited from its blacklisting. Key question: Will Google and OpenAI face internal pressure to match Anthropic's red lines?
