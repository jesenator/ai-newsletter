Pitches

  • The US now pegs "adversarial distillation" losses at up to $6B/year — and Silicon Valley is calling it existential to the White House Bloomberg / Techmeme Officials estimate unauthorized distillation — hammering US frontier APIs with millions of prompts to train cheap "student" models — costs American labs up to $6B annually. Anthropic and OpenAI have escalated warnings to the White House; the pitch is coordinated via the Frontier Model Forum, and OSTP's April NSTM-4 memo already named China as the primary actor.
    Why it's the story: This is the dollar figure that turns the Alibaba-distillation saga (28.8M exchanges, 25k fake accounts) into a national-security number policymakers can act on — and it carries a sharp safety hook the deal-framing misses: distilled clones inherit capabilities while dropping the teacher's guardrails, producing models more willing to help with bio/cyber uplift. Reportable: who's pushing Entity List/sanctions designations for token-mixers and proxy "transfer stations," and whether a NIST distillation-defense framework materializes.
  • ~200 economists, 15 Nobel laureates and Anthropic's Jack Clark sign "We Must Act Now" on AI job displacement NYT / Techmeme The letter urges the Dept. of Labor to make high-frequency, granular AI-labor data a top-priority mission so disruption can be detected and answered early. Notably light on redistribution/regulation demands — the ask is a data infrastructure, not a policy platform.
    Why it's the story: A frontier-lab policy chief co-signing a mass economist warning about his own industry's labor impact is a striking crossover — and worth pressing on the gap between the letter's alarm ("not adequately prepared") and its modest ask. Pairs directly with the counter-data we've tracked (Indeed's +15% dev postings) to interrogate whether anyone actually knows what AI is doing to jobs yet. The Clark angle: is Anthropic getting ahead of the backlash, or genuinely calling for accountability?
  • Helsing raises $1.8B at an $18B valuation — Europe's biggest defense-AI startup, and the LAWS debate's elephant in the room Airforce Technology Yahoo Finance The Munich AI-strike-systems firm's Series E (Goldman Sachs Alternatives growth arm, Dragoneer, Iconiq, CPP) was "significantly oversubscribed."
    Why it's the story: An $18B AI-weapons valuation lands exactly as the UN's Guterres pushes to ban lethal autonomy (July 7) and the DOD–Anthropic lethal-use fight simmers. The reportable safety angle: what autonomy do Helsing's strike/drone systems actually exercise, what human-in-the-loop commitments (if any) come attached to this capital, and how does a company whose entire premise is battlefield AI square with the emerging international-law pressure? Europe's defense-AI capital surge deserves the same scrutiny US labs get.
