Pitches

  • In a rare joint statement, all Five Eyes signals agencies warn that AI capable of "taking down governments and businesses" is months — not years — away Guardian via Techmeme The Decoder The SIGINT chiefs of the US, UK, Australia, Canada and NZ jointly urge leaders to "act now," declaring frontier models will "fundamentally transform both offensive and defensive cyber" and that "the timeline is not years, it is months." The Decoder notes the statement lands days after the same US national-security apparatus blocked foreign access to Anthropic's Fable/Mythos — even as the NSA reportedly runs Mythos for offensive ops. Why it's a story: The clearest official endorsement yet of near-term catastrophic cyber risk, from the very agencies driving the Fable export crackdown. It sharpens the "ban it abroad, deploy it ourselves" contradiction and gives a concrete, on-record hook for the loss-of-control/cyber-uplift debate. Reportable: what classified evals underpin "months," and whether this is laying groundwork for broader export controls.
  • OpenAI ships GPT-5.5-Cyber and "Patch the Planet" — open-sourcing frontier offensive-capable cyber tooling the same week the US banned a rival's for being too dangerous OpenAI Wired via Techmeme GPT-5.5-Cyber now hits 85.6% on CyberGym (vs 81.8% for GPT-5.5) and is offered to "trusted defenders"; Patch the Planet (with Trail of Bits, HackerOne) enlists cURL, Go, Python and 30+ projects. OpenAI says "the bottleneck is now patching, not finding" — Codex Security has scanned 30M+ commits. Why it's a story: A direct, reportable juxtaposition with the Fable saga — a US lab voluntarily releasing a more cyber-capable model under a "trusted access" governance regime, exactly the kind of bespoke licensing the Fable shutdown lacked. Worth probing how "trusted defender" gating works, the dual-use disclosure risk, and whether GPT-5.5-Cyber clears OpenAI's own Preparedness thresholds.
  • Nvidia unveils "Halos," a full-stack functional-safety OS for humanoids — the robotics-safety layer arrives just as embodied-AI funding goes vertical Nvidia Bloomberg via Techmeme Adapted from autonomous-vehicle tech and running on IGX Thor hardware, Halos targets the "split-second decisions" humanoids must make around people; Nvidia is also opening a dedicated safety lab. Crunchbase reports robotics VC hit record 2026 highs (Standard Bots $200M, Figure "now employs more robots than humans," SoftBank-backed Coowa eyeing a HK IPO). Why it's a story: A genuine warning-shot-adjacent safety story in physical AI — who certifies that a humanoid won't injure a person, and is an Nvidia-controlled proprietary stack the right place for that standard to live? Connects the robotics capital frenzy to a concrete, under-covered safety-governance question. Reportable: how Halos handles liability, and whether it becomes a de facto industry gatekeeper.
