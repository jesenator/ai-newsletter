Pitches

  • The federal AI testing institute is imploding — CAISI's director is quitting after just three months Axios (Maria Curi) / Techmeme Chris Fall, who took over the Center for AI Standards and Innovation only in late April (itself after Collin Burns was pushed out), is resigning, Commerce confirmed. Why it's the story: CAISI is the US government's only real muscle for evaluating frontier models — the body Hassabis and Altman keep invoking when they call for a "US-led testing regime." A second director departure in months signals the evals mission is being hollowed out, exactly the "cutting CAISI would be bad" thread on your beat. Reportable: who's actually running model evaluations now, whether the MOUs with OpenAI/Anthropic survive, and how this squares with the White House's ad-hoc model-gating regime.
  • Trump officials are reviving a de facto ban on Chinese open-source models — a gift to OpenAI and Anthropic Axios (Maria Curi) / Techmeme Parts of the administration are reigniting efforts to ban cutting-edge foreign open models as Kimi K3/Qwen momentum builds — a move that "could lock in dominance by OpenAI and Anthropic." Bill Gurley counters in WaPo that open models are "what competition looks like," not a security threat. Why it's the story: this is the policy payoff of the "China won" panic you flagged as overblown (Zvi, AISI's 4–7-month cyber gap). A ban would use national-security framing to entrench two US labs' market power — a concentration-of-power story with a clean safety counter-argument (banning weights doesn't stop distillation, and cements incumbents). Reportable: is this Entity-List mechanics, an EO, or import controls, and who inside the administration is driving vs. resisting.
  • "Synthetic insiders": AI deepfake employees are now infiltrating companies from the inside FT (Hannah Murphy) / Techmeme A Verizon analysis of ~22K incidents found 12% were carried out by internal actors — and the emerging vector is AI deepfake "employees" who pass hiring and gain legitimate access. Why it's the story: a concrete, escalating real-world warning shot — AI-enabled social engineering that has already surfaced (the North Korean fake-IT-worker cases) now being systematized. Fits your "early real-world examples of risk" genre and is under-covered vs. the model-capability news cycle. Reportable: named victims, how deepfake interviews clear KYC/HR checks, and which detection vendors are cashing in (the SentinelOne-vet $100M "secure AI agents" raise below is the flip side).
