PITCH: OpenAI Staff Raised Alarms About Mass Shooting Suspect—Leadership Decided Not to Report

The Wall Street Journal reports that about a dozen OpenAI staffers debated whether to report Jesse Van Rootselaar's ChatGPT activity to authorities in June 2025—eight months before she killed 8 people in Tumbler Ridge, British Columbia. Some staff identified her usage as indicating real-world violence and pushed leadership to alert police. Leadership decided not to.

OpenAI's defense: the activity "did not meet its threshold of a credible or imminent plan for serious physical harm." They proactively contacted Canadian police after the attack.

Why this matters:

This is the clearest "warning shot" case yet for AI safety. Unlike the Las Vegas bomber (passive advice-giving), OpenAI's own internal systems flagged this user as concerning enough for a dozen employees to debate action. The company made a judgment call—and 8 people died. Key questions: What exactly triggered the abuse detection? What did the conversations contain? Who made the final call not to report? This invites scrutiny of OpenAI's reporting threshold and whether "imminent" is too narrow a standard when AI can detect patterns humans miss.
