Pitch: Warren Calls Anthropic Blacklist "Retaliation" — and Demands OpenAI's Full Pentagon Contract

On the eve of tomorrow's landmark hearing (1:30pm PT, Judge Rita Lin), Sen. Warren sent formal letters to both Hegseth and Altman, calling Anthropic's supply chain risk designation "retaliatory" and demanding full disclosure of OpenAI's DOD contract terms. Her letter states: "I am particularly concerned that the DoD is trying to strong-arm American companies into providing the Department with the tools to spy on American citizens and deploy fully autonomous weapons without adequate safeguards." Warren also asked Altman directly for OpenAI's DOD contract, noting that "it is impossible to assess any safeguards" without seeing the full text.

Why this matters: This is the first senior lawmaker to formally accuse the Pentagon of retaliating against Anthropic for its safety stance — and simultaneously demand transparency from OpenAI. It creates dual pressure: on the Pentagon to justify its designation, and on OpenAI to disclose what safety guarantees (if any) it gave the military. Coming hours before Judge Lin's hearing, Warren's letters reframe the case from a vendor dispute into a congressional oversight fight. The hearing tomorrow will produce the first judicial ruling on whether "AI safety restrictions" = "sabotage risk." Now a sitting senator is on record saying the answer is no. Key question for a story: What is actually in OpenAI's contract with the Pentagon — and does it match the safety commitments Anthropic was punished for demanding?
