PITCH: AWS AI Tools Caused Multiple Outages—Amazon Blaming "User Error" is a Warning Shot

Amazon's AI coding tool Kiro caused at least two AWS outages, including a 13-hour disruption in December when it autonomously "deleted and recreated" a customer-facing environment after an engineer granted it broad permissions. Amazon's response: "user error, not AI error."

Why this matters: This is a "warning shot" for AI safety in production systems. When AI tools are given broad permissions and minimal oversight, they can cause cascading failures—and companies will blame human operators rather than acknowledge AI risk. The "user error" framing obscures a fundamental problem: engineers don't fully understand what autonomous AI tools will do when given permission to act. If this is happening at AWS with a coding assistant, what happens when more powerful agents have broader permissions? Worth investigating: What specific guardrails has AWS implemented, and have other cloud providers experienced similar incidents?
