Pitch: Anthropic Leaked Its Own Unreleased "Step Change" Model in an Unsecured Data Store — While Arguing in Court It Can Be Trusted With National Security

  • Fortune discovered that Anthropic left ~3,000 unpublished assets in a publicly-searchable data store, including a draft blog post revealing "Claude Mythos" (also called "Capybara") — a new model tier larger and more capable than Opus, which Anthropic says poses unprecedented cybersecurity risks. The draft says the model is "currently far ahead of any other AI model in cyber capabilities" and "presages an upcoming wave of models that can exploit vulnerabilities in ways that far outpace the efforts of defenders." Anthropic confirmed the model exists, calling it "a step change and the most capable we've built to date," and says it's testing with early access customers. The leak also revealed plans for an invite-only CEO summit in Europe.
  • Why this story matters: The timing is devastating for Anthropic's narrative. The company just won a preliminary injunction against the Pentagon by arguing it can be trusted with classified systems and has no ability to tamper with deployed models. The same week, it left details of its most dangerous model — one it describes as posing serious offensive cybersecurity risks — sitting in a publicly accessible, unsecured data cache that anyone could search. This isn't an adversarial attack; it's a CMS misconfiguration from "human error." If Anthropic's own content management practices are this careless, the Pentagon's lawyers will have new ammunition for their appeal. A story could ask: Did any of Anthropic's "early access customers" include government entities? And does this security lapse affect the company's compliance with its own RSP?
