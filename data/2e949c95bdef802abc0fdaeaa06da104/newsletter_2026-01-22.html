PITCH: Did Nvidia Pay Influencers to Kill an AI Chip Export Bill?

Model Republic/The Midas Project has documented what appears to be a coordinated, likely paid astroturfing campaign against the AI OVERWATCH Act. Around a dozen MAGA influencers—including Laura Loomer (1.8M followers), Brad Parscale, and Ryan Fournier—posted nearly identical attacks on a bill that would give Congress veto power over AI chip exports to China.

The smoking guns: Two posts contained the exact same typo ("Al" instead of "AI")—the kind of error that only happens when copy-pasting. Eight accounts used "win/lose the AI race," seven used "strip Trump of authority," four mentioned "Hakeem Jeffries." Several accounts have documented ties to Influenceable, a PR firm that runs paid conservative influence campaigns. David Sacks quote-tweeted one post saying "Correct."

The bill is Republican-sponsored, backed by Microsoft and conservative think tanks—but posts falsely claimed it was "sponsored by a long list of degenerate Democrats." Nvidia, which stands to lose billions if Congress can block H200 sales to China, declined to comment.

"When accounts that have participated in previous Influenceable campaigns all suddenly become experts on semiconductor export control policy within a single day, posting similar language with similar errors, the coincidence raises serious questions."

Why this matters: Despite the astroturfing, the House Foreign Affairs Committee passed the bill 42-2. But this is a rare documented case of what appears to be corporate-funded influence operations targeting AI policy. Worth investigating: Did Nvidia or its lobbyists fund this campaign? What other AI policy debates have been similarly manipulated?
