Pitch: Google's AI Overviews Deliver Tens of Millions of Wrong Answers Every Hour — and Google Knows It

  • The NYT reports today that an analysis by AI startup Oumi found Gemini 3-based AI Overviews are accurate ~90% of the time — which across 5T+ searches/year means tens of millions of erroneous answers every hour. The feature cites everything from trustworthy sites to Facebook posts. One user searched when Hulk Hogan died and got a denial of his death directly above a Daily Mail article about his death.
  • Why this matters: Google turned itself from an information curator into a publisher in 2024. At 90% accuracy, the error rate is staggering at Google's scale — hundreds of thousands of inaccuracies per minute. This is a safety story: AI Overviews look authoritative, have no visible confidence indicators, and users are primed by the Wharton "cognitive surrender" study (covered Apr 5) to accept AI answers uncritically. It's also a liability story: Google is asserting factual claims at massive scale with no disclaimer. A story could investigate: What is the error rate on medical or safety-critical queries specifically? Does Google track accuracy internally, and if so, what threshold did it set before launch? And given that the Gemini 3 bug in February left 10%+ of AI Overviews without any citations, is Google's quality assurance improving or deteriorating?
