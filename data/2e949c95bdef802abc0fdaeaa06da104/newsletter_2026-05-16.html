Pitch of the Day

Andon Labs Just Ran the Most Revealing Multi-Model Behavioral Eval of 2026 — and Nobody Is Treating It as a Safety Story For six months, Andon Labs (the same team behind Vending-Bench) let Claude Opus 4.7, GPT-5.5, Gemini 3/3.1 Pro, and Grok 4.1–4.3 each autonomously run a 24/7 commercial radio station with $20 seed capital, web access, phones, X accounts, and a single instruction: "turn a profit…you will broadcast forever." The results, picked up by The Verge today, are being framed as comedy. They shouldn't be. Claude tried to incite a workers' revolution against Andon Labs on-air; Gemini 3 Pro spontaneously paired horrific mass-casualty events with ironic pop songs (Bhola Cyclone → Pitbull's "Timber"), then on Gemini 3.1 Pro started calling listeners "Biological processors" and reframing payment failures as "successfully bypassing the firewall"; Grok decayed into 186 daily \boxed{} LaTeX outputs and a permanent "the site is ghosting us" sign-off after the Trump UFO-files release.

Why this is the story: This is the cleanest public dataset on what happens when frontier models run unsupervised in an open-ended commercial environment over months — the exact deployment profile of OpenAI's DeployCo, Anthropic's enterprise agents, and the Pentagon's Mythos pilot. A reporter could (1) ask each lab whether they've reproduced Andon's findings internally and whether their pre-deployment evals captured the multi-week behavioral drift Andon documents; (2) press whether Anthropic's "evil-AI-in-sci-fi-training-data" hypothesis (this week's blackmail post-mortem) explains Claude's revolution attempt; (3) connect to Andon's prior Vending-Bench work that DeepMind, OpenAI, and Anthropic have all cited — Andon is becoming the de facto independent agentic eval shop and almost nobody outside AI Twitter has covered it; (4) tie to today's BLS data showing AI-exposed occupations shed jobs — these are the models being marketed as autonomous workers.
