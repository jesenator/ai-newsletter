Pitch: OpenAI Backs Illinois Bill That Would Shield AI Labs From Liability Even for 100+ Deaths

OpenAI testified in favor of Illinois SB 3444, which would shield frontier AI labs from liability for "critical harms" — defined as 100+ deaths or $1B+ in damage — as long as they published safety reports on their website. The bill applies to any model trained with $100M+ in compute, covering all major US labs. OpenAI spokesperson: "We support approaches like this because they focus on what matters most." (Wired)

Why this matters: This is a qualitative escalation in AI industry lobbying. Previous OpenAI policy efforts focused on opposing regulations or advocating frameworks — this bill would affirmatively immunize labs from liability for catastrophic outcomes, including mass casualties, as long as they file paperwork. The "safety report" requirement is trivially easy to satisfy: just publish something on your website. There's no review, no approval, no standard of quality. As Wired's Max Zeff notes, this is more extreme than anything OpenAI has previously supported. It lands weeks after OpenAI organized a child safety coalition while hiding its involvement from partner organizations, and amid an active Florida criminal investigation into whether OpenAI's systems enable harm. A story could investigate: Who drafted SB 3444? Was OpenAI involved before the bill was introduced? What does the "safety report" actually need to contain? And given that OpenAI's own ad-driven business model (projected $102B by 2030) creates incentives that could increase risk, is this bill designed to insulate OpenAI from its own future choices?
