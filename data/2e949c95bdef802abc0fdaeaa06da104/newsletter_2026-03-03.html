PITCH: OpenAI's Pentagon "Red Lines" Are a Mirage—Surveillance Protections May Not Exist

New Transformer reporting reveals that OpenAI's contract clause—"shall not be used for unconstrained monitoring of US persons' private information"—is riddled with loopholes. The DIA already purchases bulk smartphone data without warrants; "unconstrained" means any constraint at all could void the prohibition; and "as consistent with these authorities" ties restrictions to the same legal frameworks enabling mass surveillance.

Why this matters: Sam Altman admitted the deal was "opportunistic and sloppy" and is now renegotiating. But the key question remains: OpenAI accepted "any lawful use"—the exact phrase Anthropic refused. If OpenAI's safeguards are window dressing, the company that held the line on democratic values just got destroyed while the company that caved got rewarded. Sarah Shoker (former OpenAI geopolitics lead) told The Verge: "The use of the word 'unconstrained,' 'generalized,' 'open-ended'—that's not a complete prohibition."
