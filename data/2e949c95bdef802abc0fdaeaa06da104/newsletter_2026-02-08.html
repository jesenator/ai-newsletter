PITCH: Anthropic's Enterprise Dominance is Rewriting Safety Economics

Anthropic has guided investors that annualized revenue will exceed $30B by end of 2026—up from $9B at year-end 2025. The company is closing a ~$35B funding round at $350B valuation. Meanwhile, Steve Yegge published an analysis based on 40+ employee interviews describing Anthropic's "Yes, and..." culture as a "hive mind" where ideas are "judged based on vibes."

"Companies are asking Anthropic sales: 'Will everything be OK?' or 'Will we be here in five years?'" — Steve Yegge

Why this matters: Anthropic's enterprise strategy—selling Claude as a tool for developers and businesses rather than chasing consumer attention—is paying off spectacularly. Thomson Reuters crashed 16% and RELX dropped 14% this week on Claude's legal AI capabilities. But the safety question is whether vibes-based culture scales with billions in funding and hundreds of employees. Is Anthropic's safety-first reputation compatible with a "spaceship taking off" mentality where there are "no plans beyond 90 days"? The company that built its brand on responsible development now has enormous financial incentives to ship fast. Worth investigating: What happens to safety processes when a company 9x's revenue in 12 months?
