PITCH: Meta Spending $65M to Elect State Politicians Who Will Kill AI Regulation

NYT Meta has launched two bipartisan super PACs—one backing Republicans, one Democrats—to elect state legislators who will block AI safety bills. The spending is designed to "prevent state legislation that it fears could inhibit artificial intelligence development."

Why this matters: This is corporate influence at its most direct: Meta is paying to elect lawmakers who will vote against regulating Meta. Unlike federal lobbying, state-level spending is harder to track and faces less scrutiny. After California passed SB 53, other states are considering similar frontier AI rules. Meta's playbook—fund both parties to ensure winners owe you regardless of outcome—could become the template for Big Tech to preemptively kill safety regulation before it reaches the federal level. Worth investigating: Which specific bills is Meta trying to kill, and which candidates have already received PAC money?
