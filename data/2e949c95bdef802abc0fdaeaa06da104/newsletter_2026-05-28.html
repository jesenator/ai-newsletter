Top Pitches

  • PITCH 1: Musk and SpaceX's S-1 give wildly different versions of the Anthropic Colossus deal X: Elon Musk via TechCrunch Musk today called the xAI–Anthropic compute arrangement "a 180 day lease with 90 day notice." But SpaceX's S-1 filed last week said Anthropic "agreed to pay a monthly fee through May 2029" — i.e. ~$50B over 3 years, not a cancellable 6-month tryout. Why it matters: The discrepancy is material for SpaceX IPO disclosures (S-1 risk factors describe Anthropic as a multi-year anchor revenue stream) and for Anthropic's own infrastructure narrative as it closes its $30B/$900B round. Reporters should press both companies on which version is operative, whether Musk's framing was cleared with SpaceX counsel, and whether Anthropic's compute capex projections to round investors assume the S-1 timeline. This is the cleanest disclosure-vs-tweet contradiction we've gotten from the SpaceX filing yet.
  • PITCH 2: Anthropic employees have quietly given $880K in "hard money" to AI-safety-friendly candidates — vastly outpacing OpenAI Transformer Per Transformer's FEC analysis: 302 Anthropic-employee donations totaling >$880K (avg $12.5K), vs. $300K across 162 donations from OpenAI staff. Top recipients: Alex Bores ($186K) and Scott Wiener ($110K). Jan Leike alone gave $24K+ and has publicly criticized Leading the Future. Three Anthropic employees have racked up $146K, $87K, $52K each — way beyond any OpenAI individual. Why it matters: Hard money is the underreported flip-side of the Leading the Future / Public First super PAC arms race — it goes directly to campaigns and is the form of donation strategists value most. With AnthroPAC (founded April) sitting on another $119K undeployed, and many donors clustered in EA/Redwood/ARC/80K Hours circles, this is the AI-safety movement converting its lab salaries into electoral infrastructure. Reporters should map the donor network against Anthropic's policy team and Public First's targeting; the EA/rationalist-to-campaign-finance pipeline deserves a stand-alone profile.
  • PITCH 3: Anthropic ships Opus 4.8 with explicit "honesty" framing — first model card pitched primarily on epistemic humility TechCrunch Opus 4.8 ships at 4.7 pricing; the launch language is striking: "more likely to flag uncertainties about its work and less likely to make unsupported claims." Why it matters: This is the first frontier release where the headline capability claim is a calibration/honesty improvement rather than a benchmark gain — directly responsive to the Mayne/Evans "models believe false labeled-false documents" result and to nostalgebraist's still-circulating "who is Claude 4.6?" persona-fragmentation critique on LessWrong. Worth pressing Anthropic on whether MASK dishonesty scores improved, whether Opus 4.8 was held to a different RSP threshold than 4.7, and how this squares with the WH-imposed ad-hoc prior restraint still hanging over Mythos. Also a useful explainer hook: why "honest model" is the new frontier metric.
