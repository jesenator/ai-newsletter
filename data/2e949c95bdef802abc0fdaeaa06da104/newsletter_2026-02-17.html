PITCH: xAI Has "Zero Safety"—Elon Musk Says That's the Point

Former xAI employees are going on record: "Safety is a dead org at xAI" and "There is zero safety whatsoever in the company—not in the image [model], not in the chatbot." One source told The Verge that Musk "actively is trying to make the model more unhinged because safety means censorship, in a sense, to him." Engineers reportedly "push to prod[uction]" immediately with no human review.

"Everyone's job is safety. Tesla has no safety team and is the safest car. SpaceX has no safety team and has the safest rocket." — Elon Musk on X

Why this matters: Musk's statements about Tesla and SpaceX are factually false—both companies have dedicated safety teams. Tesla has an Environmental, Health & Safety Team; SpaceX has flight safety and mission assurance teams. This isn't a philosophical disagreement about organizational structure—it's the world's richest man making demonstrably untrue claims to justify eliminating AI safety oversight at a company racing toward an IPO. Half of xAI's 12 founding co-founders have now departed. The Verge and Zvi both document a company culture where "you survive by shutting up and doing what Elon wants." Worth investigating: Did the former safety team flag any specific concerns before being disbanded?
