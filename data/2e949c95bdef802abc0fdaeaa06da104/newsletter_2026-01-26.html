PITCH: AI Industry Fractures Over Minneapolis Shooting—Employee Pressure Could Force CEO Silence to Break

After federal agents fatally shot Alex Pretti—an ICU nurse and lawful gun owner filming an arrest—over 450 AI workers signed a letter urging CEOs to cancel ICE contracts and speak out. Jeff Dean, Chris Olah, and other prominent researchers publicly condemned the killing, while Sam Altman, Dario Amodei, Demis Hassabis, and every major AI CEO remained silent.

Why this matters: This is a real-time test of whether employee pressure can move AI companies on politically charged issues. The industry's government dependencies—compute, export controls, regulatory treatment—have kept CEOs quiet on Trump policies. But OpenAI employees are now publicly criticizing Greg Brockman's Trump donations, while Aidan Clark says he hopes "a day like today makes Greg reconsider his politics." The NRA—typically aligned with Trump—criticized the administration for suggesting armed citizens risk being "lawfully shot." Worth investigating: Which AI companies have ICE contracts? Will any CEO break ranks? And what happens to employees who spoke out?
