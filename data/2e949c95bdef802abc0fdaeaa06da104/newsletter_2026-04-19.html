Pitch: Mythos Is Flooding Open-Source Maintainers With Bug Reports They Can't Keep Up With — and Anthropic Has No Plan to Fix the Bottleneck

  • Bloomberg reports that Anthropic's Mythos is compounding an already unsustainable workload for open-source maintainers, many of whom were already dealing with a "crazy" number of AI-generated bug reports before Mythos even launched. Mythos can find vulnerabilities faster than small volunteer teams can fix them, putting critical internet infrastructure at risk. Sophos warned earlier this month: "We can't control the pace of AI-driven vulnerability discovery, but we can control how fast we respond." But the response infrastructure doesn't exist.
  • Why this would be an impactful story: This is a real-world "warning shot" story. Anthropic built Mythos to find vulnerabilities and launched Project Glasswing to patch them at scale — but the patching pipeline funnels through open-source maintainers who are mostly unpaid volunteers. The Curl project already had to stop accepting AI-generated submissions because quality was so poor. VentureBeat noted: "Flooding open-source maintainers with an avalanche of critical bug reports could easily do more harm than good." A story could investigate: How many Mythos-discovered vulnerabilities are sitting in unpatched queues? Is Anthropic paying any maintainers directly? Is Glasswing's $100M credit program creating an unfunded mandate for the open-source community? And does this represent a new class of AI externality — where companies profit from finding problems that volunteers must fix for free?
