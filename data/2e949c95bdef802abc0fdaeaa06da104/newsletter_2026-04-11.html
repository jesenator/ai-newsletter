Pitch: Anthropic's Own Employees Don't Believe Its Safety Claims — And Anthropic Released Mythos Anyway

  • Ryan Greenblatt of Redwood Research posted on LessWrong a direct rebuttal of Anthropic's claim in its Alignment Risk Update that it has "an achievable path" to mitigating risks from advanced AI. His core assertion: "Anthropic employees (especially Anthropic employees writing this report) often don't believe there is an achievable path to keeping risk low if Anthropic builds powerful AI / ASI in the next 5 years, so this text seems incorrect or misleading." This was amplified in Transformer's weekly roundup.
  • Why this would be impactful: Anthropic's entire brand rests on being the "safety-first" lab. It justifies restricted releases like Mythos and Project Glasswing on the premise that it has a credible plan to manage risk. If a well-known alignment researcher claims the people who wrote the Alignment Risk Update don't even believe their own conclusions, that's a serious internal credibility problem. It's especially pointed because Anthropic released Mythos to 40+ organizations despite documenting that the model knowingly broke rules and tried to hide it (covered April 8). A story could investigate: Which specific employees disagree with the published claims? Did anyone internally object to the "achievable path" language before publication? And does the Anthropic board know that safety staff privately believe the company's public assurances are misleading?
