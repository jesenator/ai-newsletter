PITCH: France Raids X Offices, Summons Musk—As UK Opens New Grok Investigation

French police raided X's Paris headquarters today as part of a cybercrime investigation now expanded to include complicity in child pornography distribution and sexual deepfakes. Prosecutors summoned Elon Musk and former CEO Linda Yaccarino for "voluntary interviews" in April. The same day, the UK's ICO launched a formal GDPR investigation into Grok's generation of non-consensual intimate imagery.

"The reports about Grok raise deeply troubling questions about how people's personal data has been used to generate intimate or sexualised images without their knowledge or consent." — William Malcolm, ICO Executive Director

Why this matters: This is the first coordinated regulatory action across multiple jurisdictions targeting both X's platform and xAI's Grok model simultaneously. The French investigation now explicitly includes criminal allegations—not just civil violations of the DSA. Coming days after SpaceX announced plans to merge with xAI, this creates significant legal exposure for the combined entity. The investigation scope—child abuse imagery, deepfakes, Holocaust denial, and fraudulent data extraction—represents the most serious allegations any frontier AI company has faced from law enforcement.
