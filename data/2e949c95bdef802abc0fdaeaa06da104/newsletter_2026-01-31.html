PITCH: Pentagon Demands Anthropic Remove Safety Restrictions—$200M Contract at Stake

The Pentagon is at a standstill with Anthropic over a $200M contract because Anthropic won't remove restrictions against autonomous weapons targeting and domestic surveillance. Reuters reports Pentagon officials argue they should be able to deploy commercial AI "regardless of companies' usage policies, so long as they comply with U.S. law."

"In line with a January 9 department memo on AI strategy, Pentagon officials have argued they should be able to deploy commercial AI technology regardless of companies' usage policies." — Reuters

Why this matters: This is the first major documented clash between a frontier AI lab's safety policies and government deployment demands. Anthropic's refusal to allow autonomous weapons control without human oversight is exactly the kind of "red line" that safety advocates have pushed for—but the Pentagon is now explicitly pressuring them to remove it. The timing is critical: Anthropic is preparing for an IPO while simultaneously taking Gulf state investment, and this clash could force a choice between safety commitments and government revenue. Worth investigating: Will other AI labs face similar pressure? And if Anthropic caves, what precedent does that set for AI safety commitments industry-wide?
