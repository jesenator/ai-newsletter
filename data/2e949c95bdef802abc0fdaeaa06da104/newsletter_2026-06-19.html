Pitches

  • DOJ intervenes in NAACP's clean-air suit against xAI — arguing Grok is "vital" to national security and was used in the Iran strikes Wired Utility Dive DOJ filed Monday to dismiss the NAACP's suit over xAI's 57 unpermitted gas turbines at Colossus 2, with DoD's chief AI officer Cameron Stanley declaring Grok Gov Mode is one of only four models supporting "Secret and Top-Secret" missions — including "recent strikes against Iran." Shutting the turbines would "directly threaten ongoing national security interests." Why it's the story: A frontier lab's pollution liability is being waved away by invoking classified military dependence — a striking new use of national-security doctrine to shield AI infrastructure from environmental law. It hands the government a precedent to designate any data center "mission-critical." Reportable: what Grok actually did in the Iran operation, whether the four-model claim is verifiable, and how this squares with the same admin export-banning a rival's model (Fable) on security grounds.
  • Over two-thirds of the Department of War has now transitioned off Anthropic's models — the Fable fallout becomes a measurable procurement exodus X: Wes Roth Transformer DoD says most of the department has moved daily workflows to alternative vendors after the export-control shutdown; agencies with Claude embedded had six months to switch. ChatGPT, meanwhile, goes live on the Pentagon's bespoke platform in early July. Why it's the story: This is the first hard number on the commercial cost of the Fable takedown — and evidence the White House is actively steering the government's largest AI customer away from Anthropic. Pairs with the "is this punishment?" thread. Reportable: which vendors are winning the displaced contracts, whether the migration predated or was triggered by the export order, and the dollar value at stake.
  • New Oxford/AISI study: frontier models out-persuade elite human debaters — who chose their own topics and prepped in advance — by a blowout margin arXiv Transformer Researchers found AI was "reliably more persuasive than expert humans, even when expert humans chose their issues, researched in advance." Transformer scores it AI ~275, humans 0 — vindicating Altman's 2023 prediction that superhuman persuasion would arrive "well before" superhuman general intelligence. Why it's the story: A concrete, peer-reviewable warning shot on the persuasion/manipulation risk that underpins election-integrity, chatbot-harm, and loss-of-control concerns alike. An explainer on how the eval was run — and why post-training/prompting, not raw scale, drives the gains — would advance AI literacy ahead of the midterms. Connects directly to the live state-AG and GUARD Act chatbot-harm fights.
  • Bernie Sanders introduces the American AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Act — a one-time 50% equity tax on the largest AI firms Sanders Senate The bill would give the public 50% ownership of OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI and likely Nvidia/Amazon/Google infrastructure, paid in stock, to seed a $7T fund paying 5% dividends. Why it's the story: The most aggressive labor-displacement-response bill yet, and a left-flank counterweight to the preemption package Blackburn is brokering. Even DOA, it reframes the "who owns the upside" debate that Amodei's UBI hedging and the 88K AI-attributed job cuts have opened. Reportable: how a non-sellable equity stake funds cash dividends, and which firms qualify.
