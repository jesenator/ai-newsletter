PITCH: Trump Admin Fires Key China Tech Defense Officials While Softening Export Controls

The Trump administration has pushed out Liz Cannon, the Commerce Department official whose office barred Chinese cars and banned Kaspersky antivirus. She joins a pattern: four senior export control officials have now departed under Trump. Meanwhile, the administration dropped drone restrictions, paused truck import rules, and greenlit H200 chip exports to China.

"It's going to be hard to find somebody who can come in and replace the job Liz Cannon was doing. Even if we are in a period of detente, it's important to have the expertise to think through the risks and possible actions. If not, it's going to come back to haunt us." — Geoffrey Gertz, Center for New American Security

Why this matters: This is a significant story for AI policy coverage. The same administration publicly claiming to counter Chinese tech threats is systematically dismantling the office responsible for doing so. Cannon's office created the ICTS rules banning connected Chinese vehicles—rules that remain in place but may now lack the institutional capacity to enforce or expand. The pattern (fire officials, drop restrictions, approve chip exports, plan Xi meeting in April) suggests export controls may be used as trade leverage rather than national security policy. Worth investigating: Who will replace these officials? Are H200 approvals contingent on the Xi meeting?
