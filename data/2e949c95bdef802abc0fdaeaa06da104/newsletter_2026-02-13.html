PITCH: Meta's Internal Memo Reveals Cynical Strategy for Launching Facial Recognition on Smart Glasses

A leaked Meta internal memo from May 2025 shows the company planned to launch facial recognition on Ray-Ban smart glasses specifically because "political tumult in the United States would distract critics" from the feature's release. The feature, internally called "Name Tag," would let wearers identify people and retrieve information via Meta AI.

"We will launch during a dynamic political environment where many civil society groups that we would expect to attack us would have their resources focused on other concerns." — Meta Reality Labs internal document, per NYT

Why this matters: This is a rare case of a major tech company's internal deliberations being exposed—showing they explicitly factored in exploiting political chaos to minimize backlash on a controversial technology. Meta shut down facial recognition for Facebook photo tagging in 2021 citing privacy concerns. Now they're bringing it back via hardware with 7M+ units sold—a deployment vector that's harder to regulate or opt out of. The memo is evidence of calculated, cynical timing that could inform legislative testimony, regulatory action, or litigation. This is exactly the kind of wrongdoing exposure that journalists should investigate: What else did Meta's internal discussions reveal about their calculus?
