Pitch: Pentagon Used Anthropic's AI in Venezuela and Iran — While Calling It a "Sabotage Risk"

  • A new Quinnipiac poll (LA Times / Bloomberg) reveals that a slight majority of Americans oppose the military using AI to select military targets. Buried in the poll write-up: "The polling was conducted after reports revealed the Pentagon used Anthropic PBC's AI technology to conduct military operations in Venezuela and Iran." This is the first mainstream confirmation that Claude was used in active combat operations — not just planning or analysis — in two theaters where the U.S. is currently engaged.
  • Why this matters: The Pentagon designated Anthropic a "supply chain risk" and accused it of potential sabotage — while simultaneously using its technology in real military operations, including in Iran during an active war. This is an extraordinary contradiction: the government argued in court that Anthropic's safety restrictions made it dangerous, then reportedly deployed Claude in the exact high-stakes scenarios where those restrictions would matter most. A story could investigate: What specific operations used Claude? Did those deployments comply with Anthropic's usage restrictions? And did the Pentagon's own operators rely on the safety guardrails that the DoW publicly called a sabotage risk? This could reshape the pending DC Circuit appeal, where Trump-appointed judges are weighing whether the "supply chain risk" designation was legitimate.
