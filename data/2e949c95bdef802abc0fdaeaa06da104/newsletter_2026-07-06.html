Pitches

  • Pritzker signs SB 315 — the first US law mandating annual third-party safety audits of frontier AI companies CBS News/Techmeme Capitol News IL Illinois' law (effective 2028) forces developers over $500M revenue to publish a risk framework and hire outside auditors to verify compliance — going beyond NY's RAISE Act and California's SB 53. AG gets exclusive enforcement, penalties up to $3M/violation; both OpenAI and Anthropic backed it.
    Why it's the story: This is the concrete birth of the independent-verification-organization model we flagged July 2 as the alternative to the White House's opaque case-by-case licensing regime. It's the first mandatory-audit statute in the country, arrives the same week Lutnick's team is reportedly finalizing a federal release-approval framework, and TechNet is already fighting the "subjective determinations" audit clause. Reportable: which boutique/Big Four firms are positioning to become the accredited auditors, and whether federal preemption efforts target it.
  • Two new benchmarks say frontier capability is now accelerating exponentially — Fable 5 quadrupled the remote-work bar in eight months AI Safety Newsletter Anthropic's Fable 5 completed 16.1% of Remote Labor Index projects to professional standard — up from 2.5% at RLI's launch last October, roughly double the next model (Opus 4.8). Separately, ByteDance's new EdgeBench finds AI agents' post-deployment learning speed is doubling every three months.
    Why it's the story: Amid the "is this a bubble" noise, two independent economically-grounded benchmarks both show a step-change, not a plateau — directly relevant to the layoffs-attributed-to-AI story and to whether society can adapt. Pairs with the METR/Sol cheating findings (a capability-measurement crisis) to make an accessible explainer on what these benchmarks actually measure and why the exponential framing matters for policy timing. The Five Eyes' warning that "the timeline is not years, it is months" is the same signal from the security side.
  • Anthropic signs a 20-year, ~$19B lease for a 400MW TeraWulf data center in Kentucky — as it courts a $1T+ IPO CNBC The Hawesville campus starts delivering power in H2 2027; the deal converts a former bitcoin miner into frontier compute.
    Why it's the story: Anthropic is stacking enormous long-dated compute obligations — a custom chip effort with Samsung (July 2), Trainium commitments to AWS, and now $19B in leases — while it drafts IPO risk factors (Freshfields hire, July 3). A safety-branded lab locking 20-year power contracts is a governance/disclosure angle: how does a company still subject to government access controls justify these liabilities to public investors, and what happens to the lease if a future model triggers a deployment pause? The bitcoin-miner-to-AI pivot is also its own under-covered infrastructure trend.
  • Mercor hits a $2B revenue run-rate — the data-labeling economy is quietly minting a giant as MTurk dies The Information Mercor's gross revenue run-rate topped $2B in June — double its April pace — and it's now free-cash-flow profitable.
    Why it's the story: This is the flip side of AWS retiring Mechanical Turk (yesterday's pitch): the human-in-the-loop data economy hasn't shrunk, it's consolidated into specialized expert-labeling vendors (Mercor, Surge, Scale) commanding billion-dollar run-rates. A piece on where the "humans behind AI" actually went — and what a $2B, FCF-profitable labeling firm reveals about how much frontier training now depends on paid domain experts rather than crowdsourced clickwork.

Useful color for any piece on the philosophical undercurrents shaping how alignment researchers think.
