PITCH: Federal Agencies Warned About Grok Before DOD Approved It for Classified Settings

The WSJ reports that multiple federal agencies raised concerns about Grok's safety and reliability in recent months—before the Pentagon approved xAI's model for use in classified settings. This comes as Anthropic faces a 5:01 PM deadline today over its refusal to lift safety restrictions.

Why this matters: The Pentagon is simultaneously threatening to blacklist Anthropic as a "supply chain risk" for maintaining safety guardrails while approving a competitor whose safety and reliability multiple agencies questioned. This creates a perverse incentive: the lab that eschews safety evals gets classified access; the lab that maintains restrictions faces quasi-nationalization threats. Worth investigating: What specific concerns did agencies raise about Grok? Did DOD override interagency objections? How does this affect the Pentagon's credibility in demanding "responsible AI" from other labs?
