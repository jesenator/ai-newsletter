PITCH: Karpathy Joins Anthropic to Automate Pre-Training Research — A Direct Test of Jack Clark's "No-Human-Involved AI R&D by 2028" Bet

Andrej Karpathy — OpenAI founding member, ex-Tesla AI director, the most-cited public explainer of LLMs alive — announced today that he is joining Anthropic to help launch a team focused on using Claude to accelerate pre-training research (Axios via Techmeme, X: @karpathy). The framing — Claude automating the work that produces the next Claude — is the most literal possible instantiation of Jack Clark's recent "60%+ chance of no-human-involved AI R&D by end of 2028" prediction, and of Joe Carlsmith's "AI for AI safety" thesis that Anthropic has internally adopted.

Why this is the story: (1) It's the highest-profile defection from the OpenAI orbit since Sutskever — and unlike Sutskever, Karpathy went to a direct competitor, not his own lab. (2) The pre-training framing matters: Anthropic has publicly signaled it sees the next big lift coming from RL-on-pre-training and self-improvement loops, not just post-training agents — Karpathy's hire is operational confirmation. (3) It lands the same week Anthropic's $30B/$900B round closes, the Stainless acquisition is announced, and Jan Leike steps back from Alignment Science — suggesting a deliberate rebalancing toward capabilities-acceleration even as the safety org reshuffles. (4) A reporter could ask: was Karpathy hired into the same reporting chain as Alignment Science? Does his charter touch the "automated alignment researcher" agenda Carlsmith laid out? Did OpenAI counter-offer? Tie to Sutskever's testimony last week that Altman ran a "consistent pattern of lying" — Karpathy was in the room for the founding and has stayed conspicuously silent through the trial.
