PITCH: OpenAI Built a Custom ChatGPT to Hunt Down Internal Leakers

OpenAI's security team is using a custom ChatGPT with access to employee Slack, email, and document access logs to identify employees who may be leaking information to journalists, according to The Information's Stephanie Palazzolo. The system cross-references news articles with internal access patterns to pinpoint potential sources.

Why this matters: This represents a new frontier in AI-powered internal surveillance. OpenAI is deploying its own AI to monitor employee communications and behavior—the same company that warns others about AI risks. The irony is stark: the world's most prominent AI company is using its flagship product as a tool for hunting employees who speak to the press. Coming the same week an OpenAI researcher publicly resigned citing "deep reservations" about the company's direction, this raises serious questions about internal culture, whistleblower protection, and whether AI labs should be using their own AI against their workers.
