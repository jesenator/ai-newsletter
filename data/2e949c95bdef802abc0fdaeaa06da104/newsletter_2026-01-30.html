PITCH: Pro-AI Super PAC's $125M War Chest Reveals Dark Money Pipeline

Leading the Future has raised $125M+ in 2025 from OpenAI's Greg Brockman, a16z, Joe Lonsdale, Ron Conway, and Perplexity—but the network's dark money structure runs deeper than disclosed. Transformer's investigation reveals that Build American AI's executive director Nathan Leamer ran the "Digital First Project," which doesn't legally exist. It's actually a project of American Resolve—a 501(c)(4) that sent 75%+ of its 2022-2023 spending to Targeted Victory, the GOP consulting firm that created it.

"These groups are determined to undermine bipartisan efforts to pass a regulatory framework that ensures American leadership in innovation." — Nathan Leamer, when asked about donor disclosure

Why this matters: The same consulting firm (Targeted Victory) runs Stand With Crypto, which successfully spent $135M to elect crypto-friendly candidates. Now they're applying the same playbook to AI regulation—candidate scorecards, anti-state-law ads, and dark money nonprofits. The filings show zero payroll spending despite prominent policy advocacy. AI safety advocates are planning counter-PACs ("Public First"), but they're dramatically outgunned. This story exposes how AI lobbying actually works.
