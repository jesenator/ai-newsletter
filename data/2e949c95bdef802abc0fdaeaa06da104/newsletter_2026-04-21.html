Pitch: Anthropic Just Locked Itself Into $100B of AWS Spending. What Does That Mean for Its Independence on Safety?

  • Yesterday, Amazon announced up to $25B in new investment in Anthropic ($5B now, $20B tied to milestones), and Anthropic committed to spending over $100B on AWS over the next decade, including up to 5GW of Trainium capacity. (CNBC, Amazon) The initial $5B investment is at Anthropic's latest $380B valuation. Total Amazon investment in Anthropic: ~$33B.
  • Why this would be an impactful story: Anthropic is a public benefit corporation that positions safety as its core differentiator. But a $100B decade-long spending commitment to a single cloud provider creates an enormous financial dependency. Anthropic already has $30B in cloud commitments to Microsoft and "multiple gigawatts" with Google/Broadcom. These circular arrangements — Amazon invests in Anthropic, Anthropic spends the money back at AWS — raise hard questions: If Amazon pressures Anthropic to loosen safety guardrails to win enterprise customers (or to compete with OpenAI, which Amazon also invested $50B in), does Anthropic have the structural independence to refuse? The $100B commitment dwarfs Anthropic's $30B annualized revenue. A story could investigate: How does Anthropic's board evaluate conflicts between its PBC obligations and its largest investor's commercial interests? Are there contractual protections preventing Amazon from influencing safety decisions? And does locking into AWS infrastructure limit Anthropic's ability to walk away if safety and commercial pressures ever collide?
