Top Pitches

  • Should we make deals with scheming AIs? Transformer takes the LessWrong debate apart in full Transformer Full feature now published (we flagged the preview yesterday). New reportable substance worth its own piece: Redwood's Alexa Pan says the only prerequisite for dealmaking is that AIs "act like they're able to make deals" — consciousness is irrelevant. Concrete, checkable policy asks emerge: labs should not train models to refuse deals from trusted internal parties, and Finnveden/Forethought propose a formal "honesty policy" where genuine offers carry a credible "honest" tag and models are compensated when deceived for safety evals. Why it's a story: The backfire risk is the buried lede — Pan warns "$5,000 per piece of verifiable evidence of past scheming" could be read by a suggestible model as "if I scheme without getting caught and keep receipts, I can sell them for $5K each." Salib's point that an outed AI is "not part of the positive-sum cooperative future" reframes dealmaking as adversarial. Press Anthropic/Redwood on whether payout experiments are running now and who controls the honesty tag.
  • Pentagon blacklists Alibaba, Baidu and BYD as Chinese-military companies — sweeping in the labs behind Qwen and Ernie WSJ SCMP DoD added ~2 dozen firms to its 1260H list, including Alibaba (Qwen) and Baidu (Ernie) — two of the most-downloaded open-weight model families in the world. Why it's a story: The 1260H designation is a precursor to procurement bans and often a runway to Treasury/Commerce action. If the labs producing the leading Chinese open-weight models are formally "Chinese military companies," that complicates every US enterprise, cloud host, and researcher building on Qwen/Ernie derivatives — a much bigger AI-supply-chain story than the defense-contract headline suggests. Pair with today's Taiwan move to restrict all AI-chip sales to China (Bloomberg) and the DeepSeek-on-Ascend training claim. Reportable: does this trigger HuggingFace/cloud takedowns, and what's the carve-out for academic use?
  • Standard Bots hits unicorn status — and is quietly the White House's in-house lobbyist for banning Chinese robots The Robot Report Bloomberg $200M Series C (General Catalyst, RoboStrategy) at a $1B valuation for the NY-based AI-native robot-arm maker. Buried in the announcement: Standard Bots is "a leading advisor to the White House and Congress on a National Robotics Strategy," and its key recommendation is a ban on Chinese-made industrial robots and components. Why it's a story: Not just a robotics funding round — a company that directly profits from a Chinese-robot import ban is testifying before the Joint Economic Committee advocating for exactly that ban, the same week the Pentagon blacklists BYD and a bipartisan bill to block Chinese robot imports is circulating. This is the cleanest current example of robotics industrial policy being written by its beneficiaries. Reportable: who at OSTP is taking Standard Bots' meetings, and how the "ban Chinese robots" push interacts with the 1260H list above.
