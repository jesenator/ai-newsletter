PITCH: OpenAI Fired Safety VP Who Opposed "Adult Mode"—Then Disbanded Mission Alignment Team

Pattern of Safety Departures Accelerates as OpenAI Ships Ads

OpenAI fired VP of Product Policy Ryan Beiermeister in January for alleged "sexual discrimination" after she opposed the company's planned "Adult Mode" and raised concerns that child-exploitation safeguards weren't strong enough. She denies the discrimination allegation. The same week, OpenAI also disbanded its Mission Alignment team, moving leader Joshua Achiam to a new "Chief Futurist" role.

"The allegation that I discriminated against anyone is absolutely false." — Ryan Beiermeister

Why this matters: Beiermeister started a women's peer-mentorship program in early 2025—potentially the basis for the discrimination claim. The firing came during an administration pressuring companies over DEI initiatives. Combined with the Mission Alignment team disbandment (following the Superalignment team disbandment in 2024) and researcher Zoë Hitzig's public resignation over ads, OpenAI is losing safety voices at an accelerating rate. Worth investigating: What exactly were Beiermeister's concerns about child safety mechanisms, and does the "Adult Mode" she opposed still have those vulnerabilities?
