PITCH: OpenAI Got a Pentagon Deal With the Same Red Lines Anthropic Was Blacklisted For

Hours after Trump ordered all federal agencies to cease using Anthropic—and Hegseth designated it a "supply chain risk to national security"—Sam Altman announced OpenAI reached an agreement with the Department of War to deploy models on classified networks. The deal includes the exact same prohibitions Anthropic demanded: no domestic mass surveillance, human responsibility for use of force including autonomous weapons.

Why this matters: The administration just demonstrated it will grant one company favorable contract terms while destroying another company for requesting those same terms. This isn't about the policy—it's about which company the administration favors. Worth investigating: What exactly differs between OpenAI's contract and what Anthropic was offering? Did OpenAI's willingness to publicly "de-escalate" earn it preferential treatment? Does the Pentagon's "supply chain risk" designation have any legal basis when applied to a company whose competitor just got approved for identical uses?
