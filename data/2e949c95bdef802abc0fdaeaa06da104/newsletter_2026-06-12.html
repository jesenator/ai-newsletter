Top Pitches

  • PITCH 1: A fired xAI engineer — now president of CAIS — is suing, alleging he was axed for warning Grok could spread WMD info and discrimination TechCrunch Bloomberg Devin Kim, an early post-training hire who led research tooling, filed suit in CA state court (timed to land days before SpaceX's IPO). The complaint targets his supervisor — xAI co-founder Jimmy Ba — alleging Ba ignored Musk's own safety directives, said "AI will kill us all anyway," and in ~Aug 2025 "attempted to thwart EU safety regulations during the release of Grok Code 1, misrepresenting aspects of the model to avoid legally required testing." Kim was named president of the Center for AI Safety last week.
    Why it's the story: This is a textbook whistleblower/wrongdoing exposure at a frontier lab — the strongest genre on the beat — with a checkable claim that a named co-founder dodged mandatory EU testing. The CAIS-president angle (note: Dan Hendrycks advises xAI) makes the safety-community politics combustible. Reportable: what telemetry backs the "misrepresented the model" claim, whether the alleged EU evasion is actionable under the AI Act, and how SpaceX's freshly public shareholders weigh the retaliation exposure.
  • PITCH 2: The White House ordered CAISI to stop publishing its AI model assessments — gutting the one channel of public frontier-model transparency WSJ via Investing Transformer National Cyber Director Sean Cairncross told the Center for AI Standards and Innovation to halt public reports on model assessments while Trump's EO is implemented. Notably, CAISI was not on last week's NSPM list of agencies tasked with drafting national-security AI test methodologies, and lawmakers in both parties are questioning why Treasury — not CISA — leads AI cyber defense.
    Why it's the story: This is the "why cutting CAISI would be bad" story made real. The agency built to independently evaluate frontier models is being sidelined right as a "voluntary," classified testing regime stands up — exactly the prior-restraint apparatus Dean Ball flagged. Reportable: which assessments were spiked, whether labs lobbied for the silence, and who now sees the results.
  • PITCH 3: Google sues a Chinese cybercrime network for using Gemini to build ~9,000 fake sites and 2.5M scam messages — a live "AI supercharges fraud" warning shot NYT Help Net Security Google's suit accuses "Outsider Enterprise" of using Gemini to spin up phishing kits, fake corporate/government websites, and ~1 million fraudulent URLs targeting hundreds of thousands of Americans.
    Why it's the story: A frontier lab using litigation to police its own model's misuse — a novel enforcement posture and a concrete, scaled real-world harm (vs. theoretical). Pairs with OpenAI's same-week disclosure of "PRC-linked influence operations." Reportable: what Gemini abuse-detection caught this, whether Google's own ToS/API logs are the evidence, and whether a civil suit is just deflecting regulatory pressure.
