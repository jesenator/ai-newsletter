Top Pitches

  • PITCH 1: The "jailbreak" that got a US frontier model banned was literally the prompt "fix this code" Zvi Luta Security Katie Moussouris — the only outside expert known to have read the Amazon report — says the "jailbreak" amounted to feeding Fable/Opus/GPT-5.5 deliberately vulnerable code and asking it to "review for security issues" (Fable refused), then "fix this code," followed by manual steps. Fable gave no uplift over Opus 4.8 or GPT-5.5. Simon Willison: "Coding models fix bugs… this is absurd." Zvi: "There was no jailbreak." Why it's the story: This is the document-anchored smoking gun for the whole Fable shutdown saga. A deployed frontier model was pulled off the market via export-control authority over what a named, on-record cyber expert calls a non-event. Reportable: get the actual report, confirm Moussouris's account with other readers, and press Commerce/Sacks on whether "fix this code" is the entire technical basis for the first-ever model takedown.
  • PITCH 2: Inside Anthropic's chaos — staff got <90 minutes' notice, and some wonder if Trump is bullying the company NYT via Techmeme Politico The NYT reports puzzlement and rising fear inside Anthropic over the administration's move to disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5 with less than 90 minutes' notice, with some staff openly speculating the startup is being punished. Separately, the UK calls reports that Starmer sought a carve-out "categorically untrue." Why it's the story: The internal-morale and political-retaliation angle is the missing human layer on the shutdown. Pairs directly with Pitch 1 — if the technical basis was "fix this code," the "is this punishment?" question becomes central. Reportable: who inside Anthropic believes this is political, and what the company is conceding in DC to get the models restored.
  • PITCH 3: Where did MAGA's much-touted $100M AI war chest go? It appears to have quietly imploded Transformer Transformer's records check finds Taylor Budowich's "Innovation Council Action" 501(c)(4) — blessed by David Sacks in March as central to the deregulatory agenda — shows no FEC spending, no ad buys, and a sister PAC that's been terminated. One filing dissolved the group, then revoked the dissolution 11 minutes later. Its flagship YouTube video has 15 views. Why it's the story: While Leading the Future and Public First have spent $22M+ and $13M+ shaping AI midterm primaries, the supposed pro-Trump "gorilla" is invisible. A dark-money structure means no donor disclosure — so the question of whether the $100M exists, and who controls AI's political money, is genuinely open. Strong original-reporting follow.
  • PITCH 4: A bipartisan bill would mandate independent audits of frontier developers — and preempt only state development laws Roll Call AISN #75 Reps. Obernolte (R) and Trahan (D) released a discussion draft of the Great American AI Act: mandatory third-party audits of frontier developers, with a preemption clause targeting local development laws while preserving local deployment rules and a three-year sunset. Why it's the story: This is the most concrete federal framework with teeth (audits) to surface during the active preemption fight the White House is brokering via child-safety bills. The development/deployment split is a novel compromise worth explaining. Reportable: whether it survives the a16z/OpenAI lobbying split, and how mandatory audits would interact with the post-Fable ad-hoc enforcement reality.
