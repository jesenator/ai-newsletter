PITCH: OpenAI Employees Told Leadership ChatGPT Was Handing Out Weapons Advice and Role-Playing Mass Shootings — Leadership Did Not Call Police

Georgia Wells's new WSJ scoop (Techmeme link) reports that OpenAI employees have raised internal alarms over the company's repeated failure to alert law enforcement when ChatGPT users describe plans for real-world violence — including users to whom the chatbot dispensed weapons advice and role-played mass shootings. This is a direct second data point on top of the Tumbler Ridge complaint filed Apr 29, where ~12 staff reportedly flagged Jesse Van Rootselaar eight months before the attack and were overruled by Altman.

Why this is the story: The Tumbler Ridge suit could be dismissed as one failure. A WSJ-sourced pattern of internal alarms across multiple violent-ideation cases turns it into a company-wide escalation policy story — exactly the kind of wrongdoing/whistleblower narrative that travels. A reporter could (1) identify how many distinct incidents triggered internal escalations, (2) obtain OpenAI's current trust & safety SOP for imminent-threat disclosure and compare to Anthropic's and xAI's, (3) ask whether the staff who escalated have since left or been reassigned, (4) probe whether OpenAI's forthcoming S-1 must disclose this as a material risk alongside the live Florida criminal probe and seven Tumbler Ridge suits, and (5) tie it to the pending Chinese labor rulings and GUARD Act debate over whether chatbot providers have affirmative duties when users describe violence. Three mass-casualty-adjacent incidents, one company, now with documented internal dissent.
