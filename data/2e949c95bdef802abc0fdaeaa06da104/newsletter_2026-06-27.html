Pitches

  • The de facto licensing regime starts to unwind: Mythos 5 cleared for critical-infrastructure operators, Fable 5 could return "this coming week" Axios via Techmeme X: Anthropic The Decoder After 15 days dark, the government notified Anthropic that Mythos 5 — its strongest cyber model — can be redeployed to a set of US critical-infrastructure organizations, with broad Fable 5 access reportedly imminent. The carve-out even clears non-US-national Anthropic employees and approved-org members. Mirrors the customer-by-customer regime OpenAI got for GPT-5.6 Sol.
    Why it's the story: this is the first concrete data on how the opaque licensing regime de-escalates — who gets access, on what grounds, and how non-citizen access is handled. After two weeks of "is this political punishment?" framing, the resolution terms are the reportable substance: what eval framework (if any) gated the approval, which orgs are on the approved list, and whether this becomes the template for every future frontier release. The NSA reportedly lost Mythos access during the standoff — worth confirming it's restored.
  • ~400 local newspapers sue OpenAI and Microsoft for mass copyright infringement Courthouse News Editor & Publisher Led by Long Island's Richner Communications, the suit accuses the pair of scraping copyrighted journalism to train commercial models and of knowingly stripping copyright-management info — bylines, attribution — from articles (a separate DMCA claim).
    Why it's the story: this is the local-news front of the AI copyright war, distinct from the NYT and Authors Guild cases — and the CMI-stripping count is a sharper, more concrete legal theory than fair-use fights. Hundreds of small publishers banding together is a litigation-financing and class-formation story worth tracking. Reportable: who's bankrolling it, whether it consolidates with the NYT MDL, and how it interacts with OpenAI's new licensing deals (Getty this week).
  • China's embodied-AI capital frenzy: Beijing's Anyverse/Boundless Dynamics closes a $200M+ angel round Caixin X: Caixin A pre-product Beijing embodied-AI startup raised over $200M in an angel round — paired with a 500M-RMB+ order — as China posts a wave of nine-figure robotics rounds since Nov 2025. Caixin notes Chinese robotics firms are eyeing a global IPO window around 2027.
    Why it's the story: a $200M angel (seed-stage) check is a striking marker of how frothy Chinese physical-AI funding has become relative to the US — and it lands while US robotics money also surges (General Intuition's $320M, Tombot, Aseon). The contrast between US "physical AI commercialization" talk and China's earlier, larger bets is an under-covered competitiveness thread with clear export-control and CFIUS hooks. Reportable: who the state-linked co-leads are and whether US LPs are exposed.
