PITCH: Anthropic Dropped Its Core Safety Commitment — Right Before the Pentagon Fight Made It a Martyr

The Guardian reports today that the Anthropic-Pentagon battle "shows how big tech has reversed course on AI and war." Meanwhile, the CAIS AI Safety Newsletter highlights a detail getting buried in the Pentagon drama: Anthropic's RSP v3.0, released Feb 24, removed its commitment to never release catastrophically harmful AI. The old RSP pledged not to "train or deploy models capable of causing catastrophic harm unless we could guarantee in advance that safety measures were adequate." The new one replaces this with voluntary, flexible language.

Why this matters: Anthropic is receiving massive public sympathy for standing up to the Pentagon on safety principles—but it quietly weakened its most important safety commitment just days before the fight began. None of Anthropic, OpenAI, or DeepMind currently have robust commitments against releasing AIs they assess to be highly dangerous. The CAIS newsletter notes the RSP change is driven by competitive pressure, creating "a vicious cycle" where loosened commitments accelerate development, which incentivizes further loosening. As the TIME exclusive put it: Anthropic dropped its "flagship safety pledge." This juxtaposition—safety martyr in public, weakening commitments in private—deserves scrutiny.
