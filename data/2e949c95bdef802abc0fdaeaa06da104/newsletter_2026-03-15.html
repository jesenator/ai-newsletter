Pitch: AI-Generated Iran War Propaganda Is the First Real-World Stress Test of Wartime Deepfakes — and Platforms Are Failing

  • The New York Times identified over 110 unique AI-generated images and videos about the Iran war in just two weeks. A Cyabra study finds the majority push pro-Iranian views, exaggerating military capabilities. Meanwhile, Al-Monitor/AFP reports that X's new demonetization policy has failed to stem the tide — AI-generated clips of fake US soldier captures, Israeli cities in ruins, and burning embassies continue to go viral.
  • Why this would be an impactful story: This is the first major armed conflict where AI video generation (Sora, Seedance, Kling) is mature enough to produce convincing war footage at scale. The NYT found AI fakes depicting events that shaped real diplomatic exchanges — including the USS Abraham Lincoln incident, where Iran claimed to have attacked the carrier, and AI-generated videos of the ship on fire went viral before the US confirmed the ship was unharmed. AI-generated propaganda is no longer theoretical; it's actively shaping public perception of an ongoing war. X's Grok chatbot made things worse by telling users seeking fact-checks that AI-generated war videos were real. The story connects AI safety (content authentication failures), platform governance (X's monetization of misinformation), and geopolitics (Iran using AI as "a tool of war," per Brookings). Key question: If platforms can't manage 110 deepfakes in two weeks, what happens when this scales to thousands?
