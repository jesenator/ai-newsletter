PITCH: Tencent Built Its New Hy3 Model With Claude Code — Did Anthropic's Usage Policy Just Get Laundered Through a Vendor?

Juro Osawa reports that internal Tencent memos show Tencent engineers used Claude Code to evaluate and fine-tune their new Hy3 model (The Information). Anthropic's Usage Policy prohibits use by entities "headquartered" in China, and the company just rolled out ID verification partly to block exactly this. Meanwhile, a separate Bloomberg scoop today documents at least two China-based funds using parallel fund structures to pull US capital into Chinese AI firms — and MiroMind's Chen Tianqiao is openly building "firewalls" between his US and China businesses after Beijing blocked Meta's Manus deal.

Why this is the story: Anthropic's entire China posture — refusing Pentagon access to Mythos on supply-chain grounds, ID-gating users, selling "sovereign AI" to allied governments — rests on the assumption that its Usage Policy is enforceable. If a top Chinese lab laundered Claude Code through a cloud reseller or overseas subsidiary to train a competing model, that's a material enforcement failure at the company most publicly identified with responsible scaling. A reporter could: (1) identify which reseller/API endpoint Tencent accessed; (2) ask Anthropic what audit controls, if any, caught it (and whether they're retroactively terminating); (3) tie it to the parallel-fund structures Bloomberg exposed today as a pattern of compliance arbitrage; and (4) ask Commerce/BIS whether Claude Code access constitutes a controlled export to a Chinese frontier-model developer. This is a wrongdoing-at-an-AI-company story layered on top of a live US-China policy fight.
