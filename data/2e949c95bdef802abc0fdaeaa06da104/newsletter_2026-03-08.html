PITCH: OpenAI's Robotics Chief Quit Over "Lines That Deserved More Deliberation"

Caitlin Kalinowski, OpenAI's head of hardware and robotics since November 2024, resigned Friday citing concerns about the Pentagon deal. In her X post, she wrote: "Surveillance of Americans without judicial oversight and lethal autonomy without human authorization are lines that deserved more deliberation than they got."

Why this matters: This is the first high-profile departure from OpenAI over the Pentagon contract—and it comes from someone leading a critical growth area (robotics). Kalinowski ran a 100+ person San Francisco lab and was planning a Richmond, CA facility. Her specific language—"judicial oversight" for surveillance, "human authorization" for lethal autonomy—suggests she believes OpenAI's claimed "red lines" are not actually enforceable. Her departure validates concerns that OpenAI's safeguards are weaker than advertised. Key question: Will other safety-focused employees follow?
