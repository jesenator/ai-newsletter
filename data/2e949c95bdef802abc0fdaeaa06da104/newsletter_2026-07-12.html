Pitches

  • A hard-line anti-AI activist has vanished — and the WSJ says the "resistance" is now a real Bay Area movement WSJ Sam Kirchner, co-founder of Stop AI, disappeared Nov. 21 after allegedly threatening OpenAI and attacking a fellow activist; his case has the growing movement — organized around fears of human extinction — on edge.
    Why it's the story: extinction-risk discourse has jumped from LessWrong forums to street activism with an unstable, potentially violent fringe. This is the ground-level counterpart to the anti-AI political frame (inflation, moratorium bills) we've tracked — and a warning shot on how doomer sentiment could radicalize. Reportable: Stop AI's tactics, funding, and overlap with the rationalist/EA diaspora, plus what SFPD actually knows about Kirchner's threats against OpenAI.
  • AI coding tools are drowning open-source maintainers in low-quality "slop" pull requests FT / Techmeme A data-driven FT piece finds users of AI coding tools are flooding open-source projects with low-quality contributions, overwhelming unpaid maintainers and eroding community engagement.
    Why it's the story: the counter-narrative to the triumphant "Claude Code writes everything now" thread (and the Indeed dev-jobs bump). The infrastructure the entire industry trains on and depends on — open-source — is being degraded by the same tools it enabled. A reportable piece: quantify the maintainer exodus, and connect it to the broader AI-slop measurement problem (Pangram's 25%-of-longform figure). Who bears the cleanup cost of agentic coding?
  • Update: Apple's OpenAI suit could stall the Jony Ive device "for years, or forever" Bloomberg (Gurman) Spyglass Follow-ups to Friday's trade-secret suit: Gurman details the simmering Tang Tan / John Ternus feud behind it; Siegler argues a successful injunction could indefinitely sidetrack OpenAI's hardware ambitions.
    Why it matters: sharpens the litigation angle — discovery could expose the io "puck" architecture and whether the entire acquisition is tainted. The strained ex-boss relationship suggests this is personal, raising the odds Apple pushes for maximum remedy rather than a quiet settlement.
  • Benedict Evans: the token crunch is easing, and frontier models are becoming commodity infrastructure Benedict Evans / Techmeme Evans argues the current supply crunch is "unstable," and value is shifting to products built on top of increasingly commoditized models.
    Why it's the story: an authoritative framing of the exact thesis Meta's 25%-price API and Iris chip made concrete (July 9). Directly feeds the trillion-dollar-valuation-math question hanging over the Anthropic/OpenAI IPO drafts. An explainer connecting token economics to the "is this a bubble" debate — grounded in who captures margin when the model layer collapses.
