Pitch of the Day

OpenAI's "Reverse Federalism" Is Now an Explicit, Named Strategy — And Nathan Calvin Just Conceded It's Working

Brendan Bordelon's piece today gets Chris Lehane on the record naming the strategy: "reverse federalism" — co-opt the state patchwork OpenAI's industry allies spent a year trying to preempt, modeled explicitly on how tech lobbyists killed a federal privacy law by passing 20 near-identical state ones (Politico). The killer detail: Encode AI's Nathan Calvin — who OpenAI subpoenaed last fall — admits Illinois "looks more like a deliberate reset" and that he's "pleasantly surprised" OpenAI didn't kill the third-party audit provision the way it killed similar language in NY's RAISE Act.

Why this is the story: It's the first time a frontier-lab policy chief has named, owned, and publicly modeled a long-term AI lobbying playbook on the historical record (and the 2023 Politico-documented privacy playbook). A reporter can (1) pull every state AI bill OpenAI has touched since SB 53 and map line-by-line which protections survived vs. which got stripped, against Lehane's claim that he's pushing "relatively strong safety rules"; (2) press Hochul's office on Lehane's claim that he shaped NY's law through her directly (her office didn't respond to Politico); (3) ask Pritzker whether OpenAI lobbyists got the third-party audit language inserted or merely failed to remove it; (4) tie to Brockman's funding of Leading the Future, which spent >$1M trying to defeat the NY law's lead sponsor — meaning OpenAI is simultaneously endorsing and torpedoing the same legislative framework. The reverse-federalism quote is a gift; the contradictions write themselves.
