PITCH: Hassabis and Amodei Admit They'd Prefer to Slow Down—But Won't

At Davos this week, Demis Hassabis and Dario Amodei both said they would prefer AI development to slow down. Hassabis said he'd support a pause "if every company and country joined in." Amodei agreed: "I would prefer that. I think that would be better for the world."

"It's very hard to have an enforceable agreement where they slow down and we slow down." — Dario Amodei

Why this matters: This is extraordinary: the CEOs of two leading AI labs publicly admitting they'd prefer to slow the race they're running. They describe themselves as trapped in a prisoner's dilemma—each would prefer to slow, but neither will unilaterally. The timing is awful: as Mark Carney noted at the same event, "the multilateral institutions on which middle powers have relied... are under threat." The very international coordination needed to give labs an "out" is collapsing. Worth investigating: Are these genuine cries for help, or strategic positioning? What would it take for these statements to become commitments? And why won't anyone in government take them up on it?
