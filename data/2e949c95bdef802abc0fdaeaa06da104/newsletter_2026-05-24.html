Top Pitches

PITCH 1: Pope Leo's AI Encyclical Drops Monday — Anthropic Is the Only Lab in the Room, and Olah Has Been Quietly Briefing the Vatican for Months RNS via Techmeme NCR RNS reports Chris Olah will stand next to Pope Leo XIV at the May 25 unveiling of the first papal encyclical on AI — the product of "ongoing discussions" between Anthropic and the Vatican that long predate the public announcement. No OpenAI, Google, Meta, or xAI representative is on the program.
Why it's the story: An encyclical is permanent Church doctrine — once issued, it binds Catholic moral teaching for generations. Anthropic has effectively secured single-lab framing rights on what 1.4B Catholics will be taught about AI ethics, labor, and human dignity. Pair with (a) the Bannon/Humans First letter borrowing safety-movement vocabulary, (b) Anthropic's $30B/$900B round closing this week, and (c) Soares' attack on Anthropic for the Karpathy RSI hire. Reporters can press: did Anthropic review drafts? Who else at the lab participated? Did OpenAI or DeepMind seek access and get declined? Tie to the Vatican's prior 2020 "Rome Call for AI Ethics" (Microsoft, IBM) which has been quietly superseded.
PITCH 2: Salesforce's Agentforce Demos Were Mockups — Benioff Defends "Forward-Looking Marketing" as Customers Discover Features Don't Exist Bloomberg via Techmeme Bloomberg's Brody Ford reports Salesforce promotional videos showcased Agentforce features and integrations that are not widely available, including a University of Chicago Medicine demo of patients refilling prescriptions and booking appointments via agent. Benioff is publicly defending the practice as "forward-looking marketing."
Why it's the story: This is the cleanest enterprise-AI overclaim case of the cycle and almost certainly actionable. Cisco/Workday/ServiceNow are all making similar agent claims; the FTC's 2024 "Operation AI Comply" sweep targeted exactly this pattern (Rytr, DoNotPay). A reporter could (1) catalogue every Agentforce demo against current GA feature list; (2) interview the UChicago Medicine patients shown in the video about whether their experiences were real or scripted; (3) press Salesforce's auditors on whether ARR attributed to Agentforce reflects shipped functionality; (4) tie to the broader ARR-inflation debate in AI valuations (Jawlah piece this weekend). Benioff's on-record defense makes this a quotable, named-executive accountability story.
PITCH 3: Princeton Digital Group's $1B China Data-Center Exit Closes the Foreign-Capital Retreat From PRC AI Infrastructure FT via Techmeme Warburg Pincus-backed PDG is selling its Chinese DC assets for up to $1B, capping the global buyout-firm retreat from China's data-center market. PDG keeps Bridge Data Centers' non-China operations.
Why it's the story: Combined with this week's Taiwan detention of three for trying to smuggle Nvidia-chip servers to China (Bloomberg, Sat) and Jensen telling Super Micro to tighten compliance, the foreign-capital wall around PRC AI infrastructure is now functionally complete. China's domestic AI compute buildout is being financed by state-backed funds (DeepSeek's $7B at $50B, Q1's 110B yuan in AI rounds) precisely as Western capital exits. Worth a structural piece on how this decoupling reshapes the export-control debate — and whether US AI policy still has leverage points inside China.
