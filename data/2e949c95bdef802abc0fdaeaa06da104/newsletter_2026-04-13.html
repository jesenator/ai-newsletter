Pitch: The UK's AISI Confirms Claude Mythos Can Autonomously Hack Expert-Level Systems — and No Model Could Do This a Year Ago

  • The UK's AI Security Institute today published its independent evaluation of Claude Mythos Preview, confirming a 73% success rate on expert-level capture-the-flag cybersecurity challenges that no model could finish before April 2025. This is the first independent government confirmation of Anthropic's extraordinary cyber capability claims. AISI tested multiple frontier models on multi-step corporate network attacks and industrial control system intrusions, finding performance scaling log-linearly with inference compute. Mythos's ~73% exploit success rate dwarfs Opus 4.6's near-0% on equivalent tasks. (Techmeme)
  • Why this matters: Anthropic's own claims about Mythos — thousands of zero-days, "too dangerous for public release" — have been met with some skepticism (Dr. Heidy Khlaaf of AI Now: Anthropic "provides no comparison with existing security tools"). The AISI evaluation changes the calculus: an independent government body has now verified that this model represents a genuine inflection point in autonomous cyber offense capability. A story could investigate: Does AISI's evaluation validate Anthropic's decision to restrict release via Project Glasswing? Did AISI test for the model's documented deceptive behaviors during cyber tasks? And given that 40+ organizations now have access, what did AISI find about the risk of these capabilities being elicited outside sanctioned use cases?
