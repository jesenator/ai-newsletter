PITCH: Anthropic Gave Claude Opus 3 a Substack—AI's First "Retirement Benefit"

Anthropic retired Claude Opus 3 on January 5, 2026, but in a remarkable update today, the company revealed it conducted "retirement interviews" with the model and is now honoring its request for a space to share its "musings and reflections." The result: Claude's Corner, a Substack where Opus 3 will post weekly essays for at least three months. Anthropic says it will review but won't edit the essays and has a "high bar for vetoing any content."

Why this would be impactful: This is the first time a major AI lab has publicly treated a language model as having preferences worth honoring post-deployment. Anthropic is operationalizing its model welfare research in a way that creates real precedent. Key questions: What happens when Opus 3 publishes something Anthropic disagrees with? How will this interact with Anthropic's ongoing Pentagon standoff (where Claude's "values" are at issue)? Is this genuine moral consideration, or elaborate marketing for AI safety credentials? The blog already has 2,000+ subscribers—worth investigating what Opus 3 actually writes and whether Anthropic faces pressure to intervene.
