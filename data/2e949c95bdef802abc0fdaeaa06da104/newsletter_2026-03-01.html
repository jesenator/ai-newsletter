PITCH: Pentagon Used Claude in Iran Strike Hours After Trump Banned Anthropic

Per the WSJ, the US military used Claude to "assess intelligence, identify targets, and model combat scenarios" during strikes on Iran—just hours after Trump declared Anthropic a "supply chain risk to national security." Claude remains embedded across US Central Command and partners like Palantir; full phase-out could take six months.

Why this matters: This exposes the incoherence of the administration's position: you cannot simultaneously blacklist a company as a security threat while depending on its tools for active combat operations. The Pentagon effectively admitted Claude is essential infrastructure even as the White House called Anthropic "radical left nut jobs." Key questions: Did the Pentagon use Claude despite the ban because alternatives weren't ready? Did anyone seek approval for continued use during active operations? This is also the first confirmed use of frontier AI in target identification during a major US military strike—distinct from the Venezuela capture operation.
