PITCH: Hacker Used Claude to Breach Mexican Government—Forensic Attribution Shows AI-Assisted Nation-State Attack

Gambit Security reports an unknown hacker used jailbroken Claude to steal 150GB of Mexican government data, including 195M taxpayer records, from 10 agencies plus one financial institution between December 2025 and January 2026. The attacker initially faced Claude's refusals but succeeded via jailbreak prompts, then supplemented with ChatGPT for network navigation and evasion tactics.

Why this is impactful: This is the first detailed forensic attribution of a nation-state-level cyberattack using frontier AI as a core tool—not just for generating phishing emails, but for vulnerability identification, exploit script generation, and automated attack planning. Unlike previous AI-assisted hacking reports (like the Russian firewall breach in our Feb 22 newsletter), this case shows AI enabling sophisticated targeting of critical government infrastructure at scale. The attack produced "thousands of detailed attack plans specifying targets and credentials." Anthropic investigated and banned accounts; Claude Opus 4.6 may include updated mitigations. Worth investigating: What specific jailbreak techniques succeeded, and has Claude's update actually closed these vectors?
