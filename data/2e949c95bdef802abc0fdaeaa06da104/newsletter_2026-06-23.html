Pitches

  • The fatal Tesla "Autopilot" crash — a warning shot landing on a system one step from recall BBC Electrek WSJ NHTSA Monday federalized the June 20 Katy, TX crash, where a Model 3 the driver says was on Autopilot tore through a brick home at an estimated 60–70 mph, killing 76-year-old Martha Avila Mantilla in her living room. The probe lands atop a March Engineering Analysis (3.2M vehicles — the last step before a recall) and a separate evaluation of FSD running red lights. Why it's a story: A concrete real-world-harm datapoint on the most-scrutinized Level 2 system, with EDR data now likely to become public. The reportable wrinkle: Tesla dropped the "Autopilot" name in January after a California ruling called it misleading — yet millions of cars still carry it, and the driver's complacency claim is exactly the failure mode regulators flagged. Did the system engage, and will Tesla's chronic crash-underreporting surface here?
  • Qualcomm nears a $4B all-in bet on Modular — buying the "CUDA killer" software layer to break Nvidia's moat Yahoo/Bloomberg Bloomberg Law Qualcomm is in advanced talks to acquire Modular — Chris Lattner's AI-compiler/inference startup (the Mojo language + MAX serving stack) — at ~$4B, a sharp markup from its $1.6B valuation under a year ago. Why it's a story: Modular's whole pitch is hardware-agnostic AI software that loosens Nvidia/CUDA lock-in; Qualcomm buying it is a tell that the inference-economics war is now being fought in the compiler layer, not just silicon. Reportable: how Lattner's open-source community reacts to acqui-absorption, and whether this is Qualcomm's real datacenter-AI entry after years of failed attempts.
  • "Pax Silica" gains the Netherlands — the US allied AI-supply-chain bloc now controls the EUV chokepoint, with Taiwan endorsing from the sidelines Reuters/Techmeme State Dept The Netherlands (home of ASML) formally joins the US-led Pax Silica group alongside Japan and South Korea to coordinate chips, AI hardware and critical minerals; Taiwan endorses as a non-signatory. Why it's a story: An under-covered policy structure quietly hardening into a chip-and-AI alliance — the geopolitical counterpart to the Fable export saga. ASML's accession matters most given the live "can China crack EUV?" debate (AI Futures Project). Reportable: what binding commitments members make on minerals/export alignment, and whether this institutionalizes the "allies-only" compute order.
