Pitch of the Day

curl Maintainer Just Punctured the "Mythos Is Dangerously Good At Cyber" Story That Triggered the White House Prior-Restraint Regime

Daniel Stenberg published a methodical post-mortem today (daniel.haxx.se) on the first Mythos scan of curl — the result Anthropic invoked in April when it announced Mythos was so capable at finding zero-days it would only "trickle out" access via Glasswing. Mythos reported five "confirmed" vulnerabilities in 178K LOC. After review by curl's security team: three were false positives (documented API behavior), one was "just a bug," and one is a real but severity-low CVE shipping with curl 8.21.0 in late June.

Why this is the story: the Mythos cyber narrative is the load-bearing premise for (a) the White House's veto of Anthropic expanding Mythos access from ~50 to ~120 customers, (b) Hassett's FDA-style prior-restraint trial balloons, (c) the Glasswing program structure, and (d) Palisade's framing this week. Stenberg — operator of the most-fuzzed C codebase on Earth, running 20B installs — is reporting Mythos found fewer real bugs than AISLE, Zeropath, or Codex Security have over the past 8–10 months. A reporter could (1) ask Anthropic for the aggregate true-positive rate across all Glasswing partners, not curated demos; (2) FOIA OSTP/NSC for what evidence the WH actually saw before the Mythos veto; (3) compare to today's NYT/Google TIG zero-day disclosure (real harm exists — but from unspecified models, not Mythos); (4) press whether the prior-restraint regime is being built on a marketing claim that doesn't survive contact with the maintainer of curl.
