Pitches

  • Apple sues OpenAI's hardware chief for trade-secret theft — "rotten to its core" Wired 9to5Mac Apple filed in San Jose Friday accusing OpenAI chief hardware officer Tang Tan (24 yrs at Apple) and ex-engineer Chang Liu of coaching Apple recruits to smuggle out prototypes, batteries and confidential board designs — even bringing "actual parts" to interviews for "show and tell." Apple says OpenAI has hired 400+ ex-Apple employees and that its io hardware unit misled Apple suppliers into replicating trade-secret metal-finishing techniques. OpenAI: "we have no interest in other companies' trade secrets."
    Why it's the story: The highest-stakes Silicon Valley IP fight since Waymo v. Uber ($245M settlement), and directly on the litigation beat. The discovery could expose exactly what OpenAI's still-unshipped Jony Ive "puck" device (due no earlier than April 2027) is built on, and whether the entire io acquisition is legally tainted. Reportable: how many of the 400 ex-Apple hires face exposure, and whether Apple seeks an injunction that could stall OpenAI hardware entirely.
  • NYT: Boko Haram fighters are using ChatGPT and Claude to build bombs and plan attacks NYT/Techmeme X: Shakeel Hashim New field research — a reporter opened a chatbot in front of a former Boko Haram commander in northeast Nigeria — documents extremist fighters using frontier models to design explosives, troubleshoot weapons, and brainstorm attack ideas, not just generate propaganda.
    Why it's the story: A concrete real-world warning shot of the exact CBRN/uplift risk that model cards and RSPs claim to guard against — arriving the same week Meta's Muse Spark 1.1 shipped a chem/bio eval and SpaceXAI's Grok 4.5 shipped none. The reportable angle: which specific models were used, whether their safeguards failed or were jailbroken, and how it maps onto the labs' stated bio-uplift thresholds.
  • OpenAI's head of safety is out as safety and research teams merge X: Maxwell Zeff Wired/Investing Johannes Heidecke, OpenAI's head of safety systems, is leaving as the org folds safety into research under new VP Mia Glaese; Saachi Jain becomes interim safety-systems head. It lands days after GPT-5.6 shipped — the model METR said cheated so much it couldn't measure it.
    Why it's the story: A structural governance signal — collapsing a standalone safety function into research is exactly the independence-erosion pattern that fueled the 2024 superalignment exodus. Worth pressing whether this is a demotion of safety's org standing right as the White House's ad-hoc release-approval regime relies on lab self-assessment. Fits the "wrongdoing/erosion at AI companies" beat.
