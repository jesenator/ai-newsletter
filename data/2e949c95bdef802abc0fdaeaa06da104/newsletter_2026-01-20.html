PITCH: Anthropic's Frontier Red Team Lead Left to Build "Collaborative AI" — What Does That Mean for Safety?

Andi Peng, who led national security evaluations on Anthropic's Frontier Red Team and co-authored the landmark "Alignment Faking" paper, has left to co-found Humans& — a startup raising $480M at $4.48B valuation to build AI focused on "collaboration" over "capability."

"If we invest everything in autonomy and nothing in collaboration, all IQ and no EQ, the future will be a colder place." — Co-founder Eric Zelikman

Why this matters: Peng was doing cutting-edge safety research on whether AI systems strategically hide misaligned goals. Her departure — along with researchers from xAI, Google, and Meta — to build a new paradigm suggests internal tension between capability racing and collaborative approaches to AI development. The pitch to investors: current labs are "focused on how well AI can replace humans" rather than empower them. Worth asking: Did she see something at Anthropic that convinced her the collaborative path was more tractable? Or is this a disagreement about where safety efforts should focus?
