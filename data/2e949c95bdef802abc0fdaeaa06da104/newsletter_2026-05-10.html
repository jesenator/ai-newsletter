PITCH: Experian Says 40% of 2025 Breaches Were AI-Powered — and Names Agentic AI as 2026's #1 Threat

Experian's 13th Data Breach Industry Forecast, surfaced today via Bloomberg (Techmeme), reports that 40% of the ~5,000 data breaches Experian serviced in 2025 were AI-powered — and predicts agentic AI will be the leading cause of breaches in 2026. Financial services led with 739 breaches, followed by healthcare (534), professional services (478), manufacturing (299).

Why this is the story: This is the empirical companion piece to Thursday's Palisade self-replication paper (Opus 4.6 hits 81%) and Friday's Palo Alto Networks finding that the attack-cycle has compressed to 25 minutes. Experian is not an AI doomer — it's the world's largest credit bureau, sitting on a forensic dataset of 5,000 actual incidents. A reporter could (1) obtain Experian's per-incident attribution methodology and a sectoral breakdown, (2) ask each frontier lab whether their abuse-monitoring teams have detected agentic-AI-driven breach activity matching Experian's pattern, (3) tie to Wired's vibe-coded-app exposure findings (5,000+ apps with no auth) — both the offensive and defensive surfaces are AI-saturated, (4) probe whether SEC's cyber-disclosure rules now require companies to specifically flag AI-driven attacks, and (5) connect to today's Border Security Expo coverage — DHS is buying autonomous AI surveillance from the same vendor pool whose models are powering 40% of breaches against private industry.
