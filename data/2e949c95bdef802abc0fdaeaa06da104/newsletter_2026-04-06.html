Pitch: OpenAI's CFO Was Sidelined From Financial Meetings — While the Company Pushes for an IPO at $852B

  • The Information Fortune Sam Altman has excluded CFO Sarah Friar from key financial meetings, including one where "her absence was notable and awkward." Friar has reported to Fidji Simo (CEO of Applications), not Altman, since August 2025. She's reportedly skeptical of the aggressive late-2026 IPO timeline. This lands alongside a devastating New Yorker profile based on 100+ interviews and never-before-seen Ilya Sutskever memos alleging Altman's "consistent pattern of lying."
  • Why this story matters: OpenAI just closed the largest private funding round in history ($122B at $852B valuation) and is sprinting toward an IPO. But its CFO — the person responsible for financial disclosures, audit readiness, and investor communications — is being cut out of financial conversations by the CEO. This is extraordinary corporate governance. For any company heading into a public listing, the CFO is the SEC's primary point of contact. Marginalizing her raises serious questions about whether Altman wants unchallenged control over financial narratives. The timing with the New Yorker piece — which reveals Sutskever's 70-page secret memos alleging Altman deceived the board about safety protocols — creates a pattern: the person entrusted with the most consequential AI company keeps sidelining internal checks. A story could investigate: What specific financial discussions was Friar excluded from? Is the board aware? And do OpenAI's investors — who just committed $122B — know their CFO is being marginalized?
