Pitch: Global Finance Leaders Scramble Over Mythos — While Anthropic Quietly Expands Access to UK Banks

At the IMF spring meetings in Washington this week, finance ministers, central bankers, and top bank CEOs held crisis discussions about Claude Mythos. Canadian Finance Minister Champagne: "Certainly it is serious enough to warrant the attention of all the finance ministers." Bank of England Governor Bailey: "We are having to look very carefully now at what this latest AI development could mean for the risk of cyber crime." ECB President Lagarde: "I don't think there is a governance framework that is there to actually mind those things." Meanwhile, Anthropic's UK head Pip White told Bloomberg TV the company would expand Mythos access to UK financial institutions "in the next week." A financial industry source told the BBC that "another prominent US AI company could soon release a similarly powerful model but without the same safeguards." (BBC, Guardian)

Why this matters: This is the first time a single AI model has commanded attention at a major international financial summit. The juxtaposition is sharp: while world leaders warn Mythos is an "unknown unknown" for financial stability, Anthropic is simultaneously expanding access to more organizations. The BBC report that another AI company may release something similar without safeguards is especially alarming — this is likely a reference to OpenAI's GPT-5.4-Cyber, already in limited release. A story could investigate: Which UK banks are getting access first? What happens if Mythos finds vulnerabilities in UK financial systems — who gets told, and when? And does Anthropic's rapid expansion of Mythos access (from 40+ organizations to now UK banks) undermine the "too dangerous for public release" framing?
