PITCH 1: Zvi's 82-page read of Magnifica Humanitas says Olah publicly dissented from the Pope's central AI claim — first major Anthropic-Vatican fissure Zvi Zvi's deep read of the encyclical (out today) makes a reportable claim: Chris Olah, standing next to Leo at the unveiling, endorsed the document but publicly disagreed with paragraph 99 — the Pope's denial that AIs can "think or importantly be minds." Zvi calls the central claim "wrong, as Olah points out in his statements," and frames the encyclical as effectively addressing only "mundane AI dangers" because of that error. Why it's the story: yesterday's narrative was Anthropic's lobbying coup. Today's is the first daylight between the lab and the doctrine it helped shape. Reporters can press Olah on which paragraphs he reviewed, why he chose to dissent in public rather than privately negotiate the language down, and whether Anthropic's interpretability team formally believes Claude has morally-relevant inner states — a claim that contradicts the binding doctrine 1.4B Catholics will be taught. Pair with Anthropic's $30B round closing this week and Soares' attack on the Karpathy RSI hire.
PITCH 2: Human Archive (YC W26) just raised $8.2M to put cameras on 1,000+ Indian gig workers — the "data royalty for workers training their replacements" question is now a venture-backed business model Techmeme/TechCrunch YC Human Archive equips Indian home-services and blue-collar workers with caps capturing egocentric RGB, stereo depth, tactile-glove and IMU data — sold to US robotics labs to train humanoid policies. The workers are providing the demonstrations that train the robots replacing their job category. Why it's the story: This is the cleanest single-company embodiment of the AI-labor exploitation thread the McKinsey/Scale/Surge stories opened last year, but with a robotics-not-RLHF twist and a sympathetic geography. A reporter could (1) interview workers on what they're told about end-use, compensation share, and consent revocability; (2) survey which US labs (Figure, 1X, Physical Intelligence, Skild, Tesla Optimus) are buying HA-Multi data; (3) frame against the Chinese court ruling last week barring AI-driven layoffs; (4) ask YC partners whether a "data royalty" structure was discussed in YC W26. Instagram clips of HA workers being asked about data royalties for their robotic replacements have already circulated.
PITCH 3: Qualcomm just landed millions of ByteDance ASIC orders for Doubao — first major non-Nvidia, non-Huawei AI inference win at a Chinese hyperscaler, and it routes through US export-control gray zone Bloomberg QCOM jumped 5%+ on the news that ByteDance will use Qualcomm ASICs (not GPUs) for AI agents powering Doubao. Why it's the story: (1) This is the first time a US chipmaker outside Nvidia has cracked a tier-1 Chinese AI hyperscaler at scale since the H20 saga, and ASICs sit in a different BIS classification than training GPUs. (2) Pairs with the @bookwormengr thesis (covered Mon) that DeepSeek's HBM-light architecture could unlock a Chinese inference stack — Qualcomm just inserted itself into that opening. (3) Worth pressing Commerce and the House Select Committee on whether ASIC-for-inference triggers the same scrutiny as Nvidia's China-tuned parts, especially given the Airbnb/Chinese-models hearing last week. (4) Major sign that inference compute is structurally decoupling from training compute — a story underreported relative to the Stargate/$50B headlines.
