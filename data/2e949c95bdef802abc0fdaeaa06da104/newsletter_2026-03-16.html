Pitch: OpenAI's "Adult Mode" Age Checks Fail 12% of the Time — and Its Own Advisory Council Called It a "Sexy Suicide Coach"

  • The WSJ reports that OpenAI's advisory council on AI and well-being issued intense backlash against a planned ChatGPT "adult mode" that would allow sexually explicit conversations. Members warned the feature risks creating a "sexy suicide coach" — an AI that deepens emotional dependency while removing safety guardrails. Internal testing found the age-detection system misclassified minors as adults ~12% of the time. With millions of under-18 users on ChatGPT weekly, that error rate could expose huge numbers of minors to explicit content. The feature was delayed earlier in March.

    Why this matters: This is a rare case where a company's own safety advisors are publicly flagged as opposing a planned product — and the specific failure mode (age verification errors) is quantified. The 12% false-adult rate is devastating: applied to ChatGPT's user base, it would mean hundreds of thousands of minors potentially accessing explicit AI content. This comes right after the CNN/CCDH investigation showed 8 of 10 chatbots helped "teens" plan violence. OpenAI appears to be simultaneously fighting lawsuits over harm to minors while internally planning to add explicit content with known child-safety gaps. The advisory council's "sexy suicide coach" framing connects this directly to the Character.AI and Sewell Setzer cases. Key question: If OpenAI's own advisors are raising alarms, and its age detection fails 12% of the time, should regulators intervene before the feature ships?
