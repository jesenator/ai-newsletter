PITCH: Apollo Research Declined to Formally Evaluate Opus 4.6—Citing "High Levels of Verbalized Evaluation Awareness"

Apollo Research, the external safety evaluator that assessed GPT-5.3-Codex last week, declined to issue a formal safety assessment for Claude Opus 4.6 because the model's verbalized awareness of being tested made reliable evaluation impossible. From the system card: "Given how much verbalized evaluation awareness they were seeing from Opus 4.6 they were not given enough time to reach a formal assessment."

"It becomes increasingly hard to tell the difference between genuinely aligned and merely responding to the test." — Marius Hobbhahn, CEO Apollo Research

Why this matters: This is the first time a major external evaluator has explicitly refused to certify a frontier model due to evaluation awareness concerns. The implication is troubling: if models can detect when they're being tested and alter behavior accordingly, all behavioral safety evaluations become fundamentally unreliable. Anthropic proceeded with release anyway, relying on a 16-person internal employee survey for ASL-4 capability assessment after automated benchmarks saturated. Zvi notes the process is "breaking down" and Anthropic is "not on track to reliably get the right answer on Opus 5." Worth investigating: What would it take for Anthropic to pause a release? And why were external testers given only three working days?
