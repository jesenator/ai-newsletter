PITCH: The Pentagon Just Bought Mythos Anyway

Reuters reports today that the Pentagon is deploying Anthropic's Mythos cybersecurity model to find and patch vulnerabilities across the US government — even as DOD's CTO publicly insists there will be no reconciliation with Anthropic and contractors are still on the clock to rip out Claude by Sept. (Techmeme, The Hill).

Why this matters: The story sits at the intersection of three live threads this newsletter has been tracking: (1) the White House's Mythos prior-restraint veto blocking Anthropic from expanding access from ~50 to ~120 customers, (2) the Pentagon's "supply chain risk" designation forcing every defense vendor off Claude, and (3) Daniel Stenberg's curl post-mortem yesterday puncturing the empirical basis for treating Mythos as uniquely dangerous. Now DOD is quietly using the same model it has banned. A reporter could (a) FOIA the contracting vehicle — is this an OTA, a CAISI/Glasswing carve-out, or routed through a third party? (b) ask whether the 180-day Anthropic phase-out memo has a Mythos exemption that wasn't in the original Hegseth memo; (c) press OSTP on whether the Mythos deployment was reviewed under the same framework Hassett invoked for FDA-style licensing; (d) tie to Palisade's autonomous-replication paper and Stenberg's curl results — government is buying both the offensive and defensive sides of a capability whose real-world performance is still contested. The "Anthropic is too dangerous to do business with, except when we want it" contradiction is the story.
