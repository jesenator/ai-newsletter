PITCH: 71% of Americans Oppose AI Data Centers — Now Bigger Than Anti-Nuclear Opposition, and the AI Industry's $100M Political Bet Is Hitting the Wall

Gallup, out today: 71% of Americans oppose AI data center construction in their area, 48% strongly, with environmental concerns (water, electricity) cited more than nuclear-plant opposition has ever scored (Gallup, via WaPo). Opposition is bipartisan but intense among Democrats. Pair with Transformer's Wednesday scoop that Rep. Val Hoyle (OR-04) reversed her disavowal of Leading the Future four times in 24 hours, and Blue Rose Research's finding that AI is rising in voter salience faster than any tracked issue (Transformer).

Why it's the story: The AI industry built a $100M-plus political operation (Leading the Future, Build American AI, Think Big PAC) on the premise that AI policy could be moved with crypto-style spending. Today's Gallup number is the empirical proof the premise is wrong: the public has hardened against the most visible artifact of the AI buildout — data centers — faster than against nuclear power. A reporter could (1) map Gallup's state-level breakdowns against the 50+ pending data center siting fights tracked by Data Center Watch; (2) ask the hyperscalers (Meta's $125–145B capex, ByteDance's $30B+, OpenAI/Oracle Stargate) which projects are now politically at risk; (3) tie to Hoyle's pirouette and Jesse Jackson Jr.'s IL-02 loss as the first electoral evidence that AI-industry money has become a net liability; (4) connect to Wired's same-day Meta reporting that internal morale is collapsing partly over AI-tracking software and the "draft" of 1,000 engineers — the industry is losing its workforce and its electorate simultaneously.
