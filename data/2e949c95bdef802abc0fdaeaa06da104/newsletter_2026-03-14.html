PITCH: Wired Just Showed Exactly How Claude Generates War Plans Inside the Pentagon

A Wired investigation reviewed Palantir software demos, Pentagon records, and public documentation to paint the clearest picture yet of how AI chatbots—including Anthropic's Claude—are being used inside the US military. The findings: Claude is integrated into Palantir's Maven system, which identifies potential targets, "nominates" them for bombardment, and recommends which bombers and munitions to assign. An "AI Asset Tasking Recommender" proposes strike assignments. Maven is deployed "across the entire department," including CENTCOM, which oversees the Iran war.

Why this matters: This is the first detailed, evidence-based reporting on what Claude actually does inside the military—and it's exactly the kind of use Anthropic said it wanted restrictions on. Anthropic demanded prohibitions on "fully autonomous weapons" and "domestic mass surveillance." But the Wired investigation shows Claude is already embedded in a targeting pipeline that nominates sites for bombing. The key ambiguity: does Claude make recommendations that humans rubber-stamp, or does it merely analyze intelligence while humans make targeting decisions independently? Anthropic and Palantir both declined to comment. This investigation lands at the worst possible moment for both Anthropic (fighting the Pentagon in court) and the Pentagon (arguing Anthropic's restrictions are unnecessary). It also raises the question: if Anthropic's own model is already in a target-nomination pipeline, what exactly were the "red lines" it was trying to draw?
