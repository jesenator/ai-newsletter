PITCH: GSA's New "Any Lawful Use" Mandate Would Codify What Destroyed Anthropic

The FT reports that a draft GSA guidance would require AI companies selling to civilian agencies to allow "any lawful" use by the government—the exact phrase Anthropic refused and was blacklisted for. This would effectively institutionalize the Pentagon's position as federal policy, preventing any AI company from imposing ethical restrictions on government use.

Why this matters: If formalized, this rule would preemptively strip AI companies of the ability to negotiate the kinds of restrictions Anthropic demanded—prohibitions on mass surveillance, autonomous weapons, etc. It would set a precedent that accepting government contracts means surrendering all usage discretion. The timing is notable: this comes as Anthropic prepares to sue the Pentagon over its supply chain risk designation. Worth investigating: Did the administration draft this specifically to prevent future Anthropic-style negotiations? How would this affect companies like Microsoft or Google who embed Claude in commercial products? Would this effectively force AI companies to choose between government business and ethical constraints?
