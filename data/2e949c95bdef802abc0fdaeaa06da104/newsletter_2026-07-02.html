Pitches

  • Anthropic is designing its own AI chip — and talking to Samsung to build it The Information Anthropic has begun early-stage work on a custom AI server chip and held preliminary talks with Samsung as a manufacturing partner.
    Why it's the story: The safety-first lab that just flexed pricing power over backer Amazon (June 29) is now moving to vertically integrate silicon — the clearest sign yet that compute scarcity is pushing every frontier lab toward its own chips. It reshapes the Nvidia/Broadcom/TSMC dependency map and Anthropic's Amazon/Google alliances. Reportable: whether this is inference or training silicon, the design partner (Broadcom? Marvell?), and how it squares with Anthropic's Trainium commitments to AWS.
  • OpenAI's twin Washington plays: Altman pitches a "US-led international forum," floats a 5% government stake FT (Altman op-ed) Quartz CNBC Same day: Altman proposes a US-led forum to set AI standards, assess capabilities/risks, and make models available to allies; separately OpenAI reportedly offered the Trump administration a 5% stake (~$42.6B) to seed an "AI wealth fund" and ease political blowback.
    Why it's the story: These are coordinated moves to lock in state patronage as the government already gates GPT-5.6 customer-by-customer. A 5% equity offer to the sitting administration is an extraordinary governance/conflict question — is it a sovereign-wealth idea or regulatory insurance? Pairs directly with the Trump-stock-purchase story below. Reportable: whether the White House is entertaining it, and how the "forum" differs from the CAISI/IVO ecosystem others are converging on.
  • SCOTUS killed the independent agency — and it may reroute AI oversight to private "verification organizations" Transformer (Andrew Freedman) Monday's ruling overturning Humphrey's Executor lets presidents fire agency heads at will. Fathom's Freedman argues the fix is to split AI governance: keep the science at CAISI plus a competitive ecosystem of accredited independent verification organizations (IVOs), leave enforcement to elected bodies. Connecticut and Virginia have already passed IVO bills; the bipartisan Great American AI Act names them.
    Why it's the story: This is the under-covered structural debate about who audits frontier models after the courts gutted agency independence — and it's the concrete alternative to the opaque case-by-case licensing regime that's dominated the Fable/GPT-5.6 saga. An explainer on IVOs (and how Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind have each quietly converged on independent verification) advances literacy on the emerging governance consensus.
  • Trump bought up to $5M each of Nvidia, Broadcom, Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, Apple — the same day he unveiled his AI action plan NYT Filings show the July 23 purchases coincided with the rollout of the administration's AI policy blueprint.
    Why it's the story: A clean conflict-of-interest datapoint sitting directly atop the policy that governs the companies purchased — and it lands the same day OpenAI floats handing the government an equity stake. Reportable: timing relative to specific policy provisions that moved those stocks, and whether ethics rules were triggered.
  • Cloudflare gives AI crawlers a September 15 ultimatum NBC News AI companies must differentiate their bots into search, training, and agents by Sept 15 or face blocking.
    Why it's the story: A private infrastructure company is unilaterally setting the terms of the AI-scraping fight while ~400 local papers and the NYT litigate it in court. It's a de facto enforcement layer for the copyright wars — reportable on how the labs respond and whether it becomes leverage in licensing negotiations.
