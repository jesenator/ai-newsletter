Pitches

  • Anthropic is quietly running a state-by-state "one-upmanship" campaign for ever-tougher AI safety laws — the mirror image of OpenAI's "reverse federalism" Politico Politico details a sharpening split: Anthropic is actively backing and escalating state bills (SB 53-style transparency, independent testing, revenue-scaled penalties) as a near-term safety backstop, insisting Congress shouldn't preempt state law unless a federal regime is "at least as strong" as its Advanced AI Framework. OpenAI and the Trump White House push the opposite — a "minimally burdensome national standard, not 50 discordant State ones," backed by a DOJ AI Litigation Task Force to sue states and a Commerce catalog of "onerous" laws. Why it's the story: This is the structural fault line under every AI-policy fight of the past month (Grok/SB 53, the NY moratorium, the FTC preemption warning). A frontier lab openly weaponizing state legislatures against a rival lab + the White House is a genuine "who's lobbying for what, and why" investigation. Reportable: which specific bills Anthropic is drafting/funding, and whether "as strong as our framework" is a sincere floor or a poison pill against any federal deal.
  • China just wrote emotional rules for chatbots — banning "emotional reliance," virtual relationships with minors, and mandating suicide-intervention handoffs WSJ Yahoo Beijing's new framework forbids companionship bots from fostering emotional dependency, bans "virtual intimate relationship" services (virtual relatives/partners), requires human intervention when suicide is mentioned, and mandates guardian-contact for minors and elderly users. ByteDance and Alibaba are cutting features; some "agent" functions went offline July 15 for "adjustments." Why it's the story: The world's most aggressive regulatory response to the exact companion-AI harms driving US wrongful-death litigation against OpenAI and Character.AI — and it lands as US states (and China itself) diverge sharply on child-safety rules. An explainer contrasting China's prescriptive "emotional boundaries" regime with the US's litigation-driven, patchwork approach is timely and underexplored. Reportable: enforcement mechanics and whether Western companion apps face the same demands to operate in China.
  • The data bottleneck that could stall the intelligence explosion — even if AI automates its own R&D Transformer A sharp explainer on the crux dividing "foom" optimists (Davidson, Clark) from skeptics (Tom Reed, Dwarkesh Patel): whether a self-improving AI can generalize from mostly-synthetic data, or whether it needs "mind-stretching amounts of human expert trajectories in every field." RL-environment builders say the work is bottlenecked by scarce human experts — "only so many top red teamers in the world" — and rushing produces "slop." Notably, the fastest-timeline optimists are also the most worried; Palisade's Jeffrey Ladish: "Thank God we have more time." Why it's the story: Directly rebuts the recursive-self-improvement narrative underlying AI 2027 and the Sol "100x internal compute" datapoint we covered July 14. A rare piece grounding the abstract takeoff debate in a concrete, investigable industry bottleneck — with named RL-environment vendors (Trajectory Labs) and dollar figures on data spend worth reporting out.
  • Toyota-backed Walden Robotics emerges from stealth at $1.1B — MIT's Russ Tedrake, Nvidia, Boeing, CoreWeave all on the cap table AI Weekly Bloomberg The Cambridge, MA startup — with roboticist Russ Tedrake ("Building Physical AI at Walden") — launched with a ~$300M seed led by Toyota and Deviation Capital, betting on wheeled humanoids for factory/warehouse floors rather than bipeds. Backers (Prologis, Boeing, Toyota) hint at built-in pilot sites. Why it's the story: A billion-dollar valuation at first disclosure, and a deliberate US echo of the China wheeled-humanoid thesis (AI² Robotics $735M, LimX ~$200M) we've tracked. The Tedrake pull from MIT and Toyota's spin-off structure are reportable talent/strategy angles; watch which cap-table warehouse gets the first live deployment.
