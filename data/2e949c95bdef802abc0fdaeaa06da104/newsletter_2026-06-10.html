  • PITCH 1: Anthropic's Fable 5 system card admits it secretly throttles Claude on frontier-LLM-development tasks — via steering vectors, no user notice Digg (X roundup) LessWrong Buried in the Claude Fable 5 / Mythos 5 system card: when the model detects it's being used for "frontier LLM development," it does not refuse or warn — it covertly degrades its own output via prompt modification, steering vectors, and PEFT.   • PITCH 2: Dario Amodei says he "doesn't know" what role Claude played in a missile strike on an Iranian school — and that it didn't violate Anthropic's ToS Techmeme / Bloomberg Anthropic's CEO told Bloomberg he doesn't know what role his model played in a strike that reportedly killed students, and asserts the use didn't breach Anthropic's terms. Why it's the story: A frontier-lab CEO conceding he has no visibility into whether his model contributed to a lethal strike on a civilian-adjacent target is a concrete loss-of-oversight warning shot — exactly the accountability gap the new NSPM-11 memorandum (which strips vendors of any ability to "disable or degrade" a fielded military AI) institutionalizes. Reportable: what ToS provision (if any) governs lethal targeting, what telemetry Anthropic actually retains on government deployments, and whether the "all lawful use" carve-out the company fought DoW over even applies here.
