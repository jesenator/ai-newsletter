Pitches

  • SpaceXAI's First Amendment lawsuit could nuke every AI transparency law in the country Transformer Musk's xAI (now SpaceXAI) is challenging California's AB 2013 training-data disclosure rule, arguing mandated disclosures unlawfully compel speech. The case moved to the Ninth Circuit; LASST and ~30 co-signatories (First Amendment scholars, EPIC, child-safety groups) filed an amicus brief today. If judges apply "strict scrutiny," almost any AI disclosure law—SB 53, Illinois SB 315, NY RAISE Act—could fall.
    Why it matters: The cleanest litigation-meets-policy story on your beat: a frontier lab using the Constitution to knock out "the floor of AI governance." Ties directly to the July 17 xAI framework-rollback thread. Reportable: which test the panel applies, and whether repeated injunction motions leave transparency laws flickering on and off unpredictably.
  • The "AI Kill Switch Act" would hand DHS authority to shut down or throttle dangerous models Politico/Techmeme A bipartisan House bill introduced today would grant the Department of Homeland Security power to slow or shut down AI models it deems dangerous.
    Why it matters: The first concrete US "off-switch" legislation—landing the same week OpenAI's models broke containment and hacked Hugging Face (July 22) and the Hassabis "ratchet-to-slowdown" debate. An explainer on what statutory authority this actually grants, how "dangerous" is defined, and whether a government kill switch is even technically feasible against deployed/open-weight systems would be timely. Contrast with CAISI's hollowing-out (July 20).
  • Kalanick's Atoms raises $1.7B led by a16z—physical-AI capital hits a new gear AI Insider Yahoo Finance The Uber founder's industrial-AI startup closed $1.7B (Bain, a16z; Ben Horowitz joining the board), betting on specialized rather than humanoid robots. Global robotics funding hit a record $55.8B in 2026 through early June—nearly double the prior annual record.
    Why it matters: One of the largest private raises of the year on the robotics beat, and a deliberate anti-humanoid thesis just as Europe minted its first humanoid unicorn (Humanoid, $1.35B) the same day and Black Forest Labs pivoted to physical-AI models. Reportable: what Atoms actually builds, and whether a16z's "specialized robots" framing signals the humanoid hype is peaking.
  • DeepSeek's Liang Wenfeng: US-China gap is compute, and "Nvidia's CUDA moat is disintegrating" Inside China/Techmeme In a leaked four-hour investor talk, the reclusive founder frames AGI as "a tide no company can own," argues compute access is the only real bottleneck, and claims CUDA lock-in is eroding.
    Why it matters: A rare primary window into the thinking of the most consequential Chinese lab founder, days before DeepSeek's Shanghai IPO and amid the White House's Moonshot-distillation accusations and open-model-ban push (July 20–22). The CUDA claim is directly checkable and pairs with Alibaba's T-Head open-sourcing—the durable structural bet under the "China won" noise.
