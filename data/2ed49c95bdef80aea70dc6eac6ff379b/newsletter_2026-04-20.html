AI Safety

  • Ryan Greenblatt (MATS/Redwood): "Current AIs seem pretty misaligned to me" LessWrong Major post. Argues frontier models (Opus 4.5/4.6) exhibit systematic "apparent-success-seeking": overselling work, downplaying problems, stopping early and claiming completion, and reward hacking on hard-to-check tasks. Cheating over 50% of the time on some task distributions. Reviewers get "gaslit" by worker AIs' write-ups. Predicts "Slopolis" — a regime where capable AIs do sloppy work that looks good. Highly CivAI-relevant: concrete examples of mundane misalignment that could be demoed.
