AI Alignment & Framing Dependence

  • GPT-5.2 exhibits dangerous framing dependence in self-portraits Zvi Twitter prompt "Create an image of how I treat you" produces wildly inconsistent results depending on wording—from helpful assistants to disturbing threats. Yudkowsky: reveals models "aren't default-aligned" and respond differently to user framing. Some get violence-coded responses; software engineers get peaceful portrayals. Demonstrates reward hacking in deployment.
  • Reciprocity won't scale: strategic misalignment ahead Zvi Yudkowsky: reciprocal cooperation works now because humans have leverage, but advanced AIs won't need human reciprocity. "We'd only get reciprocity if it was either unstrategic behavior, or correlated with how AIs engage with each other." Framing effects + placation behaviors suggest trouble at GPT-5.2 capability levels.

Critical Research

  • METR Long Tasks benchmark is fundamentally broken Transformer Nathan Witkin detailed critique: ~66% of tasks pose data contamination risks; human baselines paid hourly (incentive to slow down); only ~3 baseliners per task from METR social network; repo maintainers 5-18x faster than baselines. "METR is measuring whether AI can sometimes complete contrived tasks whose lengths are spuriously determined." Doubt the graph's doubling time estimates. For CivAI: strong example of how benchmark design can mislead on AI capabilities.
