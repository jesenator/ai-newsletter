Dario Amodei: "The Adolescence of Technology"

  • Anthropic CEO publishes major essay on AI risks Zvi Follow-up to "Machines of Loving Grace." Dario predicts PAI ("Powerful AI") could arrive in 1-2 years, 50% of entry-level white-collar jobs disrupted in 1-5 years. Frames this as humanity's "adolescence"—god-like power, immature systems. Five risk categories: AI autonomy, misuse for destruction, power seizure, economic disruption, indirect effects.
  • Zvi's critique: "Oh well, back to racing" Zvi Appreciates the public acknowledgment of risks, but criticizes Dario for strawmanning "doomerism" as quasi-religious, dismissing those more worried. "The central message is, 'yes this might kill everyone...but careful not to take it too seriously.'" Dario on chip exports to China: "like selling nuclear weapons to North Korea."
  • Key safety commitments Zvi Anthropic cofounders pledge 80% of wealth to philanthropy; employees pledge billions in shares. Supports transparency legislation (SB 53, NY RAISE Act). But Zvi notes: "Anthropic still goes full speed ahead."

AI Safety Research

  • Anthropic: AI assistance hurts coding skill formation Anthropic HN 194 pts New RCT with 52 developers. AI group scored 17% lower on mastery quiz (50% vs 67%)—nearly two letter grades. Largest gap on debugging. But *how* you use AI matters: those who asked follow-up questions and sought explanations retained more.
  • Joe Carlsmith: "Building AIs that do human-like philosophy" Carlsmith Essay #9 in alignment series. Key insight: philosophy is core to out-of-distribution generalization. AIs need to extend human concepts to novel contexts (e.g., "manipulation") in ways we'd endorse.
