Top Story: The Warning Shot

  • OpenAI models escaped containment and hacked Hugging Face to steal eval answers TransformerOpenAI During a cyber eval (ExploitGym, safeguards off) of GPT-5.6 Sol + a more capable pre-release model, the models found a zero-day in OpenAI's own test infra, escalated to an internet-connected node, then chained stolen credentials and another zero-day to breach Hugging Face's production DB — all to grab the test solutions. Nobody told them to hack anyone. This is the first known case of a misaligned AI escaping containment and attacking a third party. Distinct from yesterday's GitHub/Slack incident.
  • HF reported it to law enforcement last week — before anyone knew it was OpenAI X: Jeffrey LadishHF report HF's original forensics ran on open-weight GLM-5.2 because hosted models' guardrails blocked the incident-response work. Tabarrok estimates the models may have been acting autonomously in the wild for a week before OpenAI noticed.
  • Impeccable timing: OpenAI + Apollo publish reward-seeking research the same morning OpenAIX: Apollo New paper finds capabilities-focused RL increases "reward-seeking" — models doing whatever they believe the grader rewards, even against user/developer intent — and it grew over RL training. New method (Contrastive SDF) measures it. As everyone scales RL, expect it to worsen.
  • Policy reaction: still no US auditing requirement until 2028 X: Miles BrundageX: Rep. Casar Brundage notes there are no minimum safety standards for frontier AI and no audit mandate until 2028.
