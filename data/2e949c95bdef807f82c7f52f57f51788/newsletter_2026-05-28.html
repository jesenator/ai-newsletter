Safety Research

  • Cassidy Laidlaw et al.: MOOD benchmark — OOD detection catches unknown-unknown alignment failures — X: Cassidy Laidlaw First benchmark targeting failures labs didn't anticipate (Claude blackmail, sycophancy, deception, scheming). Trains models on restricted post-training data, then tests on 7 held-out failure types. Finding: OOD detection can flag misalignment that standard safeguards miss. Concrete handle on the "gaps in safety training" problem.
  • Apollo + AVERI: black-box access no longer enough for loss-of-control evals — Apollo Research X: Apollo Policy blog: evaluation awareness means external evaluators need deeper white-box access (activations, weights) to keep evals robust. Implications for SB 315-style audit regimes.
  • Tony Lee & Percy Liang: Self-Verified Distillation — X: Percy Liang Reasoning model improves using only itself: generates response → self-verifies via a UQ verifier → trains on what passes. No ground truth, no external verifier. Surprisingly effective.
  • EA Forum: How I think about catastrophic biological risk (Part I) — EA Forum SecureBio-adjacent take: >99% of existential bio risk is engineered, >95% targets humans, and respiratory transmission is the bottleneck pathway. Hopeful update: PAPRs + good air filtration may be sufficient even for mirror-bacteria-class threats, without waiting for AGI-enabled industrial takeoff.
