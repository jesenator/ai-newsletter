Interesting Research

  • Duplicate 3 layers in a 24B LLM → logical deduction jumps .22→.76 HN 181 pts GitHub Replicating David Ng's RYS method on consumer AMD GPUs. Transformers appear to have discrete "reasoning circuits" — contiguous blocks of 3-4 layers that act as indivisible cognitive units. Duplicate the right block = model runs reasoning twice. No training, no weight changes. Different duplication patterns create different cognitive "modes" from the same model. Shift by one layer and the effect disappears. Tools in the repo to find circuits in any GGUF model.
  • ICML desk-rejected 497 papers for LLM-generated reviews ICML Blog HN 137 pts ~2% of submissions rejected because their reciprocal reviewers used LLMs after explicitly agreeing not to. Detection method: watermarked PDFs with hidden LLM instructions from a dictionary of 170K phrases — sampled two per paper, probability of random match <1 in 10 billion. 506 reviewers caught, 51 used LLMs in >50% of reviews. Every flag manually verified. Clever prompt-injection-as-detection.
