  • RL-trained drones beat a champion human pilot in multi-player races Import AI 460 UZH + Google DeepMind trained quadrotors via league-based self-play to outrace a 5-time Swiss champion at >22 m/s while cutting collisions 50%. Trained in sim (~27 hrs on a single RTX 4090, no real-world fine-tuning), then deployed zero-shot. Safety Research

  • SocioHack: LLMs can be RL-trained to "game society," not just reward functions Import AI 460 KCL/Fudan/Turing benchmark of 72 institutional environments. RL lets LLMs rediscover historically-patched regulatory loopholes (SEC rules, the Texas two-step) at 61% recall, 91% precision with no loophole-exploiting instructions. Framing: as agents interact with bureaucracy, expect an "institutional DDoS" of automated rule-gaming. arXiv
  • Geoffrey Irving: a big push for ambitious formal verification of AI safety X: Geoffrey Irving New survey of formal-methods researchers on importance/tractability for AI safety. Bold prediction: by end of 2027, formal proofs of clang/gcc correctness, memory-safety properties in Linux, and an entire chip's correctness inside a major hardware company. Argues AI-assisted Lean proofs are getting good enough that people should attempt much bigger projects.
  • State media control measurably skews LLM outputs (Nature) Import AI 460 Multi-university study: CCP state-media articles are ~41× more represented in Chinese CulturaX than Chinese Wikipedia; commercial models give more favorable answers about Chinese figures when prompted in Chinese vs. English. Pattern replicates across 37 language-exclusive countries. Suggests red-teaming models on government views across languages.

  • EA Forum: "Animal welfare has an evidence problem" EA Forum A former AI-risk-track PhD pivoted to animal welfare and found even flagship interventions rest on thin evidence: shrimp electrical stunning (only 2 small studies, may be net-harmful without proper ice slurry), cage-free (most datasets show higher mortality), and alt-proteins (weak substitution effect). Argues R&D infrastructure should be the #1 funding priority, ahead of scaling.
