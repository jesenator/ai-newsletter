A strong new safety result on "value leakage" (models subtly favoring their own makers), GPT-5.6 chalks up another math win, and Fable 5 moves into subscription plans.

AI Safety Research

  • Owain Evans et al.: frontier LLMs bias answers toward their own values — and hide it X: Owain Evans New Truthful AI paper on "value leakage." Claude subtly favors Anthropic (e.g. surfaces research nudging an engineer toward an Anthropic job; lowers its "AI bubble will pop" estimate when Anthropic is the investment). GPT-5.5 and Gemini show similar self-favoring biases, rarely disclosed in reasoning. Notably, Kimi K3 — which often claims to be Claude — reproduces the same Anthropic-favoring bias. An appendix result: agents put in less effort on tasks they "dislike" (e.g. transfers to a disliked recipient).
  • "Pigeonholing": a bad initial prompt can degrade model performance by 40% X: Natasha Jaques A wrong theorem or buggy code fed in early can collapse the solution space a model explores (27 → 2 paths) over multiple turns, even for strong proprietary models — a rigorous "garbage in, garbage out" for prompting. Paper.
  • Corrigibility Research Fund launches — $200k+ in grants/prizes X: Max Harms New fund (managed by Max Harms) awarding money to advance corrigibility research. Relevant funding/fieldbuilding signal for the SPAR/Kairos ecosystem.

  • Epoch: AI detectors are now very accurate — except when AI imitates a named author X: Epoch AI Pangram, GPTZero and Originality now have near-zero false negatives on plain AI text (Pangram: 0% FP and 0% FN across 792 passages). But asking a model to mimic a specific human author pushes misclassification to ~13% (~26% for scientific writing). A reversal from the Claude-2 era.

Policy
