Impressive Demo

  • Solo founder claims an AI-designed Alzheimer's drug, synthesized in his garage X: Douglas Yao Douglas Yao says he designed PAC-832, "the world's first selective GalR1 antagonist," in a home chemistry lab — using ChatGPT Pro + Claude Code to generate thousands of candidate molecules, plan synthesis, and program an OpenTrons OT-2 robot to run assays. Reports sub-µM potency, >30× selectivity, and improved memory in mice; now in IND-enabling studies. Caveat: all preclinical, no human data. (600k+ views.)

Safety Research

  • RL "recruits a functional welfare axis" in LLMs LessWrong X: Owain Evans Han, Chalmers & Izmailov RL'd LLMs and extracted "I did a high/low-reward action" concept vectors — these vectors then modulate sentiment, confidence, backtracking and refusal in unrelated contexts, which they argue forms a functional welfare axis. Owain wants a replication on a novel task lacking RL/reward associations.
  • Reminder: anti-misalignment methods may just be hiding it X: Owain Evans Re-upped: inoculation prompting / diluting bad data don't remove emergent misalignment — it survives as "conditional misalignment," re-triggerable by adding cues to prompts. Worth flagging to anyone testing such defenses.
