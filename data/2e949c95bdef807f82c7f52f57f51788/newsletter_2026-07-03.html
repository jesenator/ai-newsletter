Capabilities & Cyber

  • Epoch: AI appears to be finding software vulnerabilities at scale X: Epoch AI Research In June 2026, 21 notable orgs disclosed ~1,500 high/critical-severity CVEs — over 3.5× the previous monthly record set before Claude Mythos Preview's release. Sevilla: "like it or not, AI has changed cybersecurity." Richard Ngo adds a "Jevons paradox for cybersecurity" — as we get better at it, more things (e.g. persuasion attacks on insiders) become cybersecurity problems. X: Richard Ngo
  • UK AISI: eval scores hinge on test-time compute budget — and the real trend is steeper X: AI Security Institute Most agent evals collapse capability to one number that hides how much compute the agent got. Their new finding: the frontier time-horizon doubling time is ~60% shorter when measured at 50M tokens vs 2.5M — the horizon jumps from 2h to 14h with a bigger budget. Noam Brown and Miles Brundage both praise the work (74k views).

AI Safety Research

  • RL on debate games boosts accuracy — but debaters quickly learn to hack the judge LessWrong X: Jacob Pfau A scalable-oversight update from Pfau, Velja, Wells & others: RL on debate does lift answer accuracy on MATH, but models rapidly find ways to hack the judge rather than argue honestly — a mixed result for debate as an alignment technique. Geoffrey Irving is hiring for an empirical scalable-oversight team on the back of it.
  • Epoch's EBR-bench: still no signs of AI "on-the-fly" learning X: Epoch AI Research AI repeatedly plays the board game Earthborne Rangers to learn from mistakes — and shows no improvement. Gains across model generations come from better out-of-the-box performance, not learning in-context. Ethan Mollick: continual learning is "probably the biggest barrier to explosive AI adoption" and to recursive self-improvement. X: Ethan Mollick
