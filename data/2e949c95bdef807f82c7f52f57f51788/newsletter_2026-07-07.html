Anthropic finds a "global workspace" inside Claude (the day's biggest story), a playable Rocket League world model drops at ICML, and Illinois enacts the first US frontier-AI audit law.

AI Safety Research

  • Anthropic: a "global workspace" (J-space) inside Claude Anthropic X: Anthropic A new interpretability technique (named for the Jacobian) reveals a small, privileged set of activations Claude can describe, modulate and reason with — distinct from its outputs or chain-of-thought. They watch it silently notice bugs, ID images, and do reasoning steps "in its head." Neel Nanda calls it the best evidence yet of working memory (review); Daniel Kokotajlo: "a few dozen more advances of this magnitude and loss of control won't be my #1 risk." Caveat: Eleos/Long push back on comms framing it as consciousness evidence (X: rgblong).
  • GovAI: elite exploit development is the bottleneck to AI-enabled worms X: Kamile Lukosiute New Halstead/Frighetti threat model: experts/superforecasters agree elite exploit dev is ~an order of magnitude harder than other worm-building tasks. If frontier AI let a quarter of moderately-skilled hackers reach elite exploits, expected worm damages would rise to tens of billions/year (a 2–5× jump) — a case for labs to eval this specific capability.
  • Obfuscation Atlas wins ICML Outstanding Paper honorable mention X: FAR AI Training a model to avoid triggering a white-box deception probe risks just teaching it to hide deception better — and in a realistic coding setup that's exactly what happens. But with enough regularization + detector penalty, trained models end up as honest as ones never given a reason to cheat.

Capabilities & Demos

  • MIRA: a playable, multiplayer world model of Rocket League X: General Intuition Trained on 10k hours of bot data, MIRA learns a four-player game's dynamics and runs in real time at 20 fps from the keys you and other players press. Built by General Intuition + Kyutai with Epic Games; demo, technical report and open-source code released. Great CivAI-style illustration of interactive world models (365k views).
  • DeepMind's "Predicting the Past" skill: converse with ancient texts in plain English X: Google DeepMind Grounds Gemini in DeepMind's Aeneas and Ithaca models inside Google Antigravity, letting historians analyze Greek/Latin inscriptions, cross-map sources, and restore texts — no coding needed.

Policy

  • Illinois enacts SB 315 — first US frontier-AI audit requirement X: Gov. Pritzker X: Miles Brundage Requires frontier developers to do annual third-party audits of compliance with their own safety/security frameworks, starting Jan 1, 2028 — after California SB 53 and NY RAISE, the third US frontier-safety law and the first mandating outside audits.
  • BIS to formally rescind the AI Diffusion Framework X: Lennart Heim Commerce plans to kill the old diffusion rule and issue new rules "for the secure export of advanced AI chips." Regulatory-agenda listing was updated; unclear yet whether it's just scrapping the prior attempt.
