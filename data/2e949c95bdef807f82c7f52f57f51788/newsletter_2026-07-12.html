Sunday, July 12, 2026
Quiet Sunday. Terry Tao vibe-codes a fleet of math visualizations, a widely-shared "political will is the bottleneck" argument lands on LessWrong, and ~200 march in SF to stop the AI race.

Coding & Demos

  • Terry Tao ports 25-year-old math applets — and builds new ones — with coding agents in hours Terence Tao HN (154 pts) Migrated his old Java applets to JavaScript with only one minor bug across ~two dozen apps — and the agent found two bugs in his original 1999 code. Then vibe-coded new interactive visualizers (a Minkowski-space "Inkscape," a Gilbreath-conjecture tool) he'd abandoned decades ago. A concrete, credible-source illustration of agentic coding for interactive demos — squarely in your CivAI wheelhouse.
  • Kyutai releases MuScriptor — best open multi-instrument music transcription model X: Kyutai Labs Feed it any recording (pop, classical, metal, jazz) and it transcribes individual instruments to MIDI. Sander Dieleman: "automatic music transcription just got a serious upgrade." Minor but a clean open-model win.

AI Safety & Fieldbuilding

  • Charbel-Raphaël Segerie: "The current bottleneck is political will, not research" LessWrong X: CRSegerie Argues risk understanding is already adequate and concrete governance tools (red lines, an IAEA-like body) exist — the scarce resource is coordination and generalist capacity, not more technical papers. Calls much "safety research" procrastination. His "most important intellectual contribution of the year" (186 likes); directly relevant to Kairos's fieldbuilding/generalist focus.
  • ~200 march in SF to "Stop the AI Race" X: Rob Miles PauseAI Protesters marched from Anthropic to OpenAI to xAI demanding a conditional pause — billed as the largest US AI-safety demonstration to date. Local Bay Area fieldbuilding context.
  • Update — Zvi engages Plan A at length Zvi A ~23-section walk through objections to AI-2040's Plan A (he doesn't endorse it, but wants it taken seriously). Meanwhile critics keep firing: Adithya Baradwaj attacks the "superhuman persuasiveness as unbounded scalar" assumption; Joshua Saxe asks why radiologists/programmers still have jobs.

Worth Reading

  • Nature study of 41M papers: AI boosts individual careers but flattens scientific discovery IEEE Spectrum AI-using scientists publish ~3× more papers and get ~5× more citations — but AI-heavy research clusters around the same data-rich problems and covers less topical ground. "We are digging the same hole deeper and deeper." A tension worth keeping in mind re: automated research.

World
