AI Safety & Policy

  • Joe Carlsmith: "On restraining AI development for the sake of safety" Joe Carlsmith New essay (10th in his alignment series). Argues a wiser civilization would employ significant capability restraint as we approach transformative AI. Key tension: idealized restraint is clearly good, but feasibility in an international competitive context is the hard part. Worried about algorithmic progress being much harder to restrain than compute. Concerned restraint regimes tend to centralize power. Notes AI's compute dependence gives leverage. Speaks for himself, not Anthropic.
  • Transformer: Six states copying Oregon's chatbot bill — with industry carve-outs Transformer CO, HI, AZ, GA, NE, ID all introduced bills structurally similar to Oregon SB 1546 but with weaker provisions: carve-outs for chatbots embedded in other services (exempts Meta/Google), no private right of action, and "actual knowledge" loopholes for minor detection. Google backed bills in HI, NE, AZ. Colorado hearing postponed, amendments possible. Critics: "tech lobbyists supply full bill text."
  • 80K Hours: Meta's $16B scam economy — lessons for AI regulation 80K Hours Leaked docs show Meta earned ~$16B/yr from scam ads, involved in 1/3 of all US fraud. Anti-fraud team capped at 0.15% revenue impact. Conclusion: we should regulate AI companies like banks — with embedded supervisors, not voluntary commitments. Directly relevant to AI governance trust questions.
