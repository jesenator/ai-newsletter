AI Safety Research

  • Forethought: train AIs to be risk-averse in resources Forethought If resources have diminishing marginal utility for an AI, a misaligned-but-risk-averse model prefers a high chance of small guaranteed payments over a low chance of a successful rebellion — so we could "pay it not to rebel" for as little as ~10¢/day rather than half the universe. Argues it's a broad, easy-to-reward target that preserves usefulness if aligned.
  • DeepMind: invert the Bellman equation to recover an agent's world model from its value function X: Jonathan Richens RL agents implicitly model latent variables they were never trained to optimize for — a potential interpretability handle for reading what an agent "believes" straight out of its values.
  • MIRI: catching pause-treaty violations via network taps — and defending against side-channels MIRI Naci Cankaya proposes watching for traffic "fingerprints" between chips and users to verify chips aren't used to break an AI pause, plus retrofitted defenses against data being smuggled out through side-channels.
