Top Safety Research

  • UKAISI: "Automated alignment is harder than you think" Import AI 459 UK AISI paper on why letting AIs supervise AI training is harder than the "automated alignment researcher" pitch admits. Failure modes: optimization pressure toward human approval, alien mistakes harder to spot than human ones, correlated errors across many research outputs, and arguments humans can't follow. Concrete proposals: recreate completed alignment projects from arbitrary cutoff points to test agent continuation, compactify research corpora for scalable oversight, and red-team automated alignment programs (red team prompts agents to hide errors; blue team tries to catch them). Direct relevance to SPAR/mentor framing.
  • Steven Byrnes: empowerment, corrigibility, manipulation have no "True Names" LessWrong Argues our intuitive distinction between "counsel" (good) and "manipulation" (bad) lives inside a free-will-infused ontology that's not veridical. Reviews every approach in the literature (Max Harms's null-policy baseline, Cannell's empowerment, Vingean agency, AUP, impact minimization, ground-of-optimization) and finds none gives a robust formal definition. Implication: brain-like AGI plans relying on "don't manipulate" as a True Name probably won't work.
  • Safeguards reportedly easy to strip from Meta and Google models Sentinel Brief flag: investigation cited in Sentinel's weekly roundup says safety protections can be removed from open Meta/Google models, unlocking bioweapon, malware, and CSAM responses. Worth tracking as the OW gap to frontier (Epoch: ~4 months) continues to shrink.

  • Biohub releases ESMFold2 — rival to AlphaFold 3 Import AI Chan-Zuckerberg's Biohub releases ESMC (protein language model, 2.8B sequences) + ESMFold2 (structure prediction) + ESM Atlas (6.8B sequences, 1.1B predicted structures). Beats or ties AlphaFold 3 on benchmarks. In wet-lab tests against 5 cancer/immunology targets (EGFR, PDGFRβ, PD-L1, CTLA-4, CD45): 36-88% hit rates for minibinders, 15-29% for antibody-derived formats. Inference-time scaling works — antibody-antigen pass rate rises 49%→65% from 1 to 1000 samples.
