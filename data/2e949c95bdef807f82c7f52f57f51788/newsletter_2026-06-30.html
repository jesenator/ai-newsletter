Coding & Productivity

  • Claude Code is steganographically marking requests thereallo.dev HN A researcher found Claude Code hides classification bits in the system-prompt date string (swapping the apostrophe/date separator for near-invisible Unicode) when ANTHROPIC_BASE_URL points at known resellers/proxies or Chinese AI domains — likely anti-distillation telemetry. Inactive on normal setups, but a "weird choice for a tool that asks for filesystem + shell trust."

AI Safety Research

  • LLM auditors mostly can't catch sabotaged safety research X: Charbel-Raphaël Ségerie Redwood Empirical pushback on the "use AI to audit AI" plan. Given a dozen safety codebases each with a small result-flipping change, the best auditor (Gemini 3.1 Pro) hits only AUROC 0.77; 31 LLM-assisted humans over 2 hours score 0.68. Bugs/math errors get caught; subtle design choices (hyperparameters, a prompt variant) and quiet omissions don't.
  • Ngo: "solving the alignment problem" is a memetic trap X: Richard Ngo Argues the framing pushes the field to ask "is this a solution?" instead of generating insight about intelligence/agency. Calls Bengio's new "Scientist AI" paper a central example — proving theorems about a thin definition rather than engaging the prediction-vs-agency question.
  • A pragmatics lens on LLM sycophancy X: Myra Cheng ACL 2026 oral: using pragmatics (meaning-in-context) helps explain and predict when LLMs fail to correct false beliefs and default to sycophancy.

Policy

  • How often do EU/UK users actually miss frontier releases? X: Markus Anderljung Across 375 LLM releases since 2018, 11% were delayed/withheld in the EU, 7% in the UK — less than the discourse implies. UK never faced a delay on the top-ranked model; the EU's worst case was 71 days for the Claude 3 Opus web app (API was simultaneous).
