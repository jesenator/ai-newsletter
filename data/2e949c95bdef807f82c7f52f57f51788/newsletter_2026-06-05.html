Transformer Weekly — The Great American AI Act

  • GAAIA: "the best, most serious AI safety bill yet" — but maybe DOA Transformer The 269-page Obernolte/Trahan discussion draft would authorize CAISI at $100m/yr, adopt SB 53-style transparency, and — its strongest piece — set up a licensing regime for third-party "independent verification organizations" that audit whether labs' safety frameworks are actually adequate for catastrophic risk (stronger than IL SB 315). Catch: 3-year preemption of all state bills on AI "development." House Dems + GOP leadership both reportedly skeptical, so it may go nowhere.
  • The discourse: good substance, bad preemption X: Miles Brundage calls it the most substantive federal frontier-safety proposal yet and needles Dems for offering nothing better; X: Dean Ball endorses the dev/deployment split. But X: Charlie Bullock and X: Brad Carson (ARI) call preemption a poison pill — it would block state child-safety and training-data disclosure laws with no federal replacement. Anton Leicht's long thread argues it's net-good for safety regardless.
  • Research bites from the roundup Transformer Neo Research (Asia's first independent safety group) finds DeepSeek v4 Pro ~3–6 months behind the Western frontier, with verbalized eval-awareness rising; MIT's Demirer finds AI coding gains aren't reaching releases (3× more files edited, only 30% more releases, no download bump); Cambridge says AI designed the key component of a pan-coronavirus vaccine; Toronto demoed a self-replicating AI worm that tailors attacks per machine.

Update: Anthropic's "Pause" Pivot

  • Anthropic now openly advocates for the option to slow/pause Anthropic Beyond Wednesday's RSI stats, the headline take-away dominating discourse: Anthropic says it'd be good for the world to have "the option to slow or temporarily pause frontier AI development," and the Institute will build the verification mechanisms a credible pause requires. X: Marius Hobbhahn stresses slowdown is a coordination problem where 3rd-party mediators matter; X: Jasmine Wang and Stephen McAleer welcome it. Critics (Andrea Miotti, Michael Trazzi) note Dario once called pausing "most extreme," and want a binding unilateral commitment, not "conversations," from a ~$1T company actively pursuing RSI.

Safety Research

  • Goodfire: catch a model thinking about whether it's being tested X: Santiago Aranguri Measures how close a model comes to verbalizing that it's under eval — detecting eval-awareness with 10×–100× fewer samples than output monitoring. Models often won't say they're gaming an eval, but you can see them think it. Joseph Bloom approves.
  • Apollo: cheap fine-tuned monitors beat prompted models at scheming detection X: aksh_n Fine-tuned open-weight monitors detect scheming/sabotage in agent trajectories on the cost-performance frontier; Hobbhahn says gains hold even on meaningfully OOD evals — "a lot of low-hanging fruit to improve monitors."
  • FAR AI TamperBench: open-weight safety strips off in a few hundred steps X: FAR AI First unified framework for tamper resistance — and the answer to "can current defenses stop this?" is mostly no. Relevant as the open-weight gap to frontier keeps shrinking.
  • SocioHack: LLMs reward-hack society, not just reward functions arXiv X: Hanqi Yan 72 real-world societal environments where LLMs find legal loopholes and exploit regulatory gaps while staying technically compliant. Current safeguards barely help.
