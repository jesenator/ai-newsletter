Transformer Weekly Roundup

  • Big story: DeepSeek's boss just made the case for export controls Transformer Leaked comments from Liang Wenfeng: a lack of chips is "the" thing holding China back. He says he'd need 200k Huawei 950s to train a frontier model but Huawei can only supply 16k; the shortage is "currently basically unsolvable" for ≥3 years. Shakeel argues export controls—not distillation sanctions—are America's real lever.
  • Leaked call details: DeepSeek has ~20k H-equivalent cards, still prefers NVIDIA X: Konstantin Pilz Liang says they're two years behind the US on 1/20th the compute, are buying "non-compliant" chips at a premium, and view Huawei's 950 as ~4:1 worse than NVIDIA with a two-year lag. Concrete confirmation China is severely compute-constrained.
  • Also notable Transformer CAISI head Chris Fall resigned 3 months in amid signs the center's sidelined; UK PM Burnham scrapped DSIT and elevated AI minister Narayan to cabinet (Jade Leung stays as PM AI adviser, Vallance to chair a new AI Taskforce); Logan Kilpatrick confirms Gemini 4 pre-training underway; US–China to hold first official AI talks in September.

Policy

  • The FRONTIER Act is formally introduced — "by far the best AI safety bill ever introduced" X: Charlie Bullock Obernolte Obernolte/Trahan's revamp of the old GAAIA draft: creates an Under Secretary for AI Security, federalizes SB 53/RAISE-style transparency, licenses independent verification orgs, and gives Commerce emergency power to suspend development/deployment posing "imminent catastrophic risk." Preemption is now much narrower. Unusually warm reactions from Encode's Calvin, AVERI, Nat Purser and Casper (with tweaks); passing it is still a heavy lift.

  • Joint AISI/CAISI eval: Kimi K3 sits well below US frontier on cyber CAISI Confirms Shakeel's earlier take—K3 is a very good model but not frontier, so it "likely does not have dangerous cyber capabilities." A rare joint US/UK government assessment.

Good CivAI-style framing; pairs with John Schulman's call (136k views) for OpenAI to release the transcripts.
