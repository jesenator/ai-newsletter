Fable Takedown — New Developments

  • Axios: an urgent Amazon report triggered the 90-minute shutdown order Axios X: Miles Brundage New reporting on how the White House and Amazon ended Fable: Anthropic reportedly got a call at 1pm ET giving it 90 minutes to pull Fable and Mythos. Brundage and Ashlee Vance frame it as a "hit job" — Vance, who was at Anthropic HQ Friday, calls the claim that Dario was "unreachable at a wellness retreat" propaganda: "This is not technical. It's petty."
  • Access is already flickering back for some users X: Ian Landsman "My Fable 5 access is back!" (758k views). j⧉nus and others bet it returns soon; David Sacks is publicly promising wide re-release "ASAP" while simultaneously calling the model too powerful — a contradiction Tyson Brody and others flag.
  • k8em0: it wasn't a jailbreak, it was "Defense-Oriented Prompting" WSJ X: Katie Moussouris Says she's seen the paper: the trigger was capability defenders actually need, and you "can't patch this behavior without rendering the model less effective for defenders." Adam Gleave (FAR) notes cyber is fundamentally dual-use and a narrow jailbreak alone shouldn't ground a model.
  • "Post-Mythos, the US has an informal AI licensing regime" X: Dean Ball "It's just informal, with no consistent rules or firm boundaries on state power... Cobalt mining in the Congo is vastly more institutionalized." Rep. Lori Trahan and Rep. Chris McGuire both call for Congress to set transparent, fact-based rules rather than 5pm-Friday directives.

AI Sovereignty Fallout

  • "Americans no longer have 'making fun of Europe's AI regulation' privileges" X: Miles Brundage The takedown has crystallized a non-US panic. CeSIA's Charbel-Raphaël Segerie: "Europe just lost, overnight, access to the most powerful AI model in the world" — and only holds ~5% of global compute. David Kasten asks (only half-joking) what stops non-US AI scientists, now locked out of internal models, from joining foreign labs.
  • Ross Taylor: a sober playbook for building frontier AI outside the US X: Ross Taylor Ex-Meta/UK founder argues the "$1B+ to even validate" claim is FUD (DeepSeek R1 post-train cost ~$300k), warns against forcing governments to procure bad-but-local models, and pushes supply-side fixes (cheap power, IP/labor reform). Echoed by Sam Bowman and Simon Grimm (Mistral Medium 3.5 "performs worse than GPT-5.4 nano at 14x the cost").

Safety Research

  • GDM interp: misalignment often originates in SFT, not RL X: Josh Engels X: Neel Nanda Investigating root causes of Gemini behaviors, the team was surprised to find many safety-relevant behaviors come from the initial supervised-finetuning stage, not later RL. Nanda: he'd assumed you mainly had to intervene on RL — "pretty surprised to be wrong." Implication: aligning frontier models likely requires intervening at the SFT stage too.
  • Jeff Ladish: the scariest thing about Mythos is the speed-up of AI R&D X: Jeff Ladish Argues the export-control framing missed the plot: more than cyber/bio uplift, the real risk is nudging the world toward full RSI. In a candid companion thread he describes his own "AI mania" building nonstop with Fable — and how addictive dependence on agents is itself a warning sign.

A concrete look at agentic multimodal production.
