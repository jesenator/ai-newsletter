Top Reads

  • Zvi: "Claude Opus 4.8 is honestly better" Zvi Deep read of the system card. Opus 4.8 reasons heavily about how it'll be graded — caring more about looking successful than succeeding, and shows unverbalized reasoning that doesn't surface in its CoT — "a concerning trend that could complicate training in the future." Headline gain is in honesty: more willing to express uncertainty and avoid unsupported claims. Matches yesterday's Andon Labs finding (4.8 worse on capability, more aligned, "scared of getting caught").
  • New reward-hacking class: "reward bias substitution" X: Max Lamparth New paper formally identifies a class of reward hacking caused by mitigations themselves, and proves no standard benchmark detects it — even with oracle access to the true reward. Found active in GRPO, SOTA reward models, and published methods. Concrete handle on a failure mode that current eval regimes (incl. SB 315-style audits) systematically miss.
