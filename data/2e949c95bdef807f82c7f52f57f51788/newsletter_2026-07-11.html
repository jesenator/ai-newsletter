GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra reportedly cracks a 50-year-old math conjecture in under an hour, Thinking Machines drops its founding manifesto, and the Plan A debate keeps escalating (now with a Vitalik essay and a plagiarism accusation).

Impressive Capability

  • GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra produces a proof of the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture HN (488 pts) X: __eknight__ One day after Ultra went GA, OpenAI says it solved the 50-year-old graph theory conjecture using 64 subagents in under an hour — proof is barely 2 pages (prompt + PDF posted). Jaime Sevilla flagged it; the math community is now scrutinizing the proof's validity. A striking frontier-math signal if it holds up.

AI Company Vision

  • Thinking Machines publishes its manifesto: "The Future Worth Building Is Human" Thinky X: Mira Murati First worldview essay from Murati/Schulman's lab: values don't average out, so the good future has many AIs shaped by the people they serve — classical-liberal, anti-power-concentration framing (Hayek/Polanyi cites). Miles Brundage's thread: it operationalizes "AI augments rather than substitutes for humans," which rivals only vaguely aspire to.

Plan A Debate (Update)

  • Vitalik Buterin: the two camps hold "totally incompatible worldviews" X: Vitalik Buterin A widely-shared take (225k views): detractors implicitly assume the ASI transition goes fine by default, which makes zero sense if superintelligence is real. Proposes pre-agreeing on concrete "serious shit" triggers (super-pandemics, >25% unemployment, slaughterbots) that flip both sides toward a slowdown. Praises Plan A's less-power-concentrating "kinds" of pause buttons.
  • Hendrycks accuses AI Futures of plagiarizing "Mutually Assured AI Malfunction" X: Dan Hendrycks Says Plan A's "Mutually Assured Compute Destruction" section doesn't cite his/Schmidt/Wang's earlier MAIM concept despite him flagging it as feedback pre-release: "would constitute plagiarism in any other context."
  • AI Futures posts "suggestions for further work" + a community-notes site appears AI Futures Project Outlines follow-up research (Plan S variants, covert-project modeling, robot cap-and-trade) and teases prizes. Separately, Common Notes lets people vote on AI-generated fact-checks of the AI 2040 text.

Safety & Verification Research

  • MIRI: a way to distinguish AI training from inference on GPUs — enabling a verifiable pause X: Robi Rahman / MIRI Presented at ICML's TAIG workshop: a method to tell training workloads from other GPU work that's privacy-preserving, zero-overhead, and adversarially robust — a key primitive for enforcing an AI pause while still allowing inference. MIRI also won a TAIG best-paper for bit-exact inference verification.
