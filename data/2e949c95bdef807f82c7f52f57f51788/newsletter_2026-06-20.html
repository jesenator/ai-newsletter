Saturday, June 20, 2026
A quiet news day dominated by Fable echoes. The real new items: a second Nobel-tier scientist defects to Anthropic, a DeepMind paper showing diffusion models can stay CoT-monitorable, and a stark warning that China likely already has full read access to the top labs' internal repos.

Talent Moves

  • John Jumper (AlphaFold lead, 2024 Nobel laureate) leaves DeepMind for Anthropic X: John Jumper Reuters After ~9 years, the AlphaFold team lead is joining Anthropic (after a break). Third high-profile defection in three days — following Noam Shazeer→OpenAI and Arthur Conmy→Anthropic. Ethan Perez/others note the talent-gravity shift toward Anthropic.

AI Safety Research

  • DeepMind: diffusion LLMs can stay CoT-monitorable X: Josh Engels arXiv Text diffusion models do many forward passes before emitting text, threatening chain-of-thought monitoring. Auditing DiffusionGemma, the team finds the intermediate states are interpretable, recovering most CoT-monitoring benefits. Neel Nanda: would love similar transparency audits for any latent-reasoning architecture.
  • Wei Dai: AI philosophical competence is a neglected crux Forethought New podcast on ensuring AI can do real philosophy. Andreas Stuhlmüller flags the open question: how much will AI help with high-level strategy/philosophy vs. only tasks with short feedback loops?

Security

  • "China has full access to all of OpenAI & Anthropic's GitHub/Slack/docs today" X: Miles Brundage Brundage broadly endorses Will Depue's blunt claim, calling it a "super serious/bad situation." Depue: wouldn't be surprised to see plausibly-deniable stolen architecture methods in Chinese OSS models. Brundage's caveat: this is also why chip-export controls still matter — hard to run even a stolen model at scale without compute.

Capabilities

  • Recursive's automated AI-research system hits SOTA on three ML-systems benchmarks X: Jeff Clune An autonomous research system reportedly topped benchmarks spanning training algorithms, optimization loops, and GPU-kernel efficiency. Framed as qualitatively different from prior approaches for hitting three levels of the stack at once — another data point on automating AI R&D.

Discourse & Fable Fallout

  • Helen Toner's clearest take on "what Anthropic's deal is" X: Helen Toner A widely-shared analogy: there are dangerous monsters in the forest, others are rousing them anyway for treasure, so Anthropic's bet is to go furthest fastest while spending heavily on containment and sending back warnings. "I understand how they got there… [but] it's not hard to see why this makes people wonder if you're crazy or lying or both."
  • Update: Trump on whether Anthropic is a national-security threat X: Hannah / Axios "Well, not now, but a week ago maybe… seems like a nice guy, smart guy. But… it's tremendous liability. People get put in prison immediately for that." — a notable softening since the takedown, with a veiled threat attached.
  • Hobbhahn: the takedown incentivizes labs to be more secretive about powerful models X: Marius Hobbhahn "I think this would be very bad" — hopes for a solution that doesn't keep the world in the dark about future frontier capabilities.
