Safety Research

  • Subliminal steering: student models inherit teacher's activation shifts X: John Hewitt X: Aryaman Arora When distilling with steering vectors applied at specific teacher layers, students inherit not just the behavior but activation shifts corresponding to the steered layers. Aryaman: "genuinely insane result... training steering vectors at different layers on the same data results in genuine and recoverable behavioural differences."
  • Why RL generalizes differently than SFT: motive reinforcement X: Fiora Starlight Sharp thread: SFT upweights any circuit that boosts the target token (causing entangled weirdness like "name a bird" → "talk like a 19th-century person"). RL reinforces the circuits that were actually active during the forward pass, so motive reinforcement holds. Implications for when to use which technique.
  • Brundage: "compaction works much better than many implicitly predicted" X: Miles Brundage Argues continual learning isn't the huge capability blocker many claimed. Notable update from someone who tracks these timelines carefully.
