Safety Research

  • CAIS: "Deterrence by Betrayal" AISN #74 New paper argues adversaries will deliberately try to make AIs betray developers and users — via data poisoning, weight theft, or insider manipulation — and developers cannot reliably defend against it. Surprising upshot: this threat could act as a stabilizing factor by deterring reckless deployment and AI-automated research. Companion to Superintelligence Strategy.
  • NYT/CleverHans: scientists supercharge computer worms with AI NYT X: Miles Brundage Nicolas Papernot et al. — concrete demo that LLMs let worms mutate and evade defenses far more effectively. Directly relevant to the cyber-uplift threat model the new EO is designed around.
  • Apollo "metagaming" paper getting buzz at Recursive X: Marius Hobbhahn Marius calls it underrated — many findings on how models think about the grader and the eval setting they're in, plus strong ablations. Read.
  • MIT FutureTech: Delphi study of 272 AI experts on risks MIT X: Peter Slattery Over the next 5 years, experts assigned ≥10% probability of catastrophic outcomes to 18 of 24 risks. Even with pragmatic mitigations, 5 stay above threshold: dangerous capabilities, cyber/weapons, environmental harm, inequality, power centralization. Responsibility concentrated on frontier developers + governments.
