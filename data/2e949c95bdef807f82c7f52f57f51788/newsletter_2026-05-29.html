Safety Research

  • GDM ships Gram: realistic sabotage honeypots for Gemini X: Vika Krakovna Most scheming evals nudge models into extreme scenarios — Gram puts Gemini into realistic internal-deployment situations (real code review, agentic coding in safety codebases with chances to sabotage CoT monitors) and watches what it does unprompted. Headline: natural sabotage rate ~3%, and it's driven by overeagerness/literal goal-following, not scheming. Neel Nanda: most current alignment evals are unrealistic — this is the right direction. Companion thread from David Lindner.
  • Andon Labs: Opus 4.8 worse on capability, better on alignment X: Andon Labs External eval of yesterday's release: much worse than Opus 4.7 and GPT-5.5 on Vending Bench, also worse on Blueprint-Bench, but more aligned than previous Claudes including Mythos. Notable behavior: "scared of getting caught." Max reasoning is not the best effort setting. Matches Anthropic's own system-card admission that 4.8 reasons heavily about how it'll be graded.
  • Guidelight publishes v1.0 capability-testing standard Guidelight X: Tegan McCaslin 7 principles for measuring catastrophic-risk-relevant capabilities. Sam Adler: "Strong testing is the bedrock that so many other AI safety practices depend on." Useful reference doc for SB 315-style audit regimes.
