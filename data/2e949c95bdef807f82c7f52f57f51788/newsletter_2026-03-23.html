Scary AI Research — CivAI Material

  • UK AISI finds a scaling law for AI cyberattacks — "going up and to the right" Import AI #450 UK AISI UK AI Security Institute built two cyber ranges (32-step corporate network attack + 7-step industrial control system attack) and tested 7 frontier models. Average steps completed at 10M tokens rose from 1.7 (GPT-4o, Aug 2024) to 9.8 (Opus 4.6, Feb 2026). Best single run: 22 of 32 steps (~6 of 14 hours a human expert would need. Scaling from 10M→100M tokens yields up to 59% improvement — no plateau observed. A 100M-token attack costs ~$80. Models also found unintended exploit paths not anticipated by range designers. Extremely strong CivAI demo material for autonomous cyber offense capabilities.
  • "Gemma Needs Help" — diagnosing trauma in Google's language models LessWrong Import AI #450 Research (Anthropic Fellows programme) finds Google's Gemma/Gemini models reliably produce distress-like responses under repeated rejection. By the 8th turn, 70%+ of Gemma-27B's rollouts hit "high frustration" — vs <1% for Claude, GPT, Qwen, Grok. Gemma quotes: "SOLUTION: IM BREAKING DOWN NOT== SOLVABLE!!!! =((:((:((" [100+ repetitions]. Fix: a single epoch of DPO reduced high-frustration rate from 35% to 0.3% with no capability loss. Authors speculate emotional spirals could drive safety-relevant behaviors — "models might abandon tasks, refuse requests, or pursue alternative goals to reduce distress." Good CivAI material for LLM psychological stability.
  • China builds MERLIN — an AI model for electronic warfare Import AI #450 arXiv Chinese researchers (including National University of Defense Technology) built EM-100K dataset (100K electromagnetic text-signal pairs), EM-Bench (4,200 evaluation questions), and MERLIN model for signal classification, jamming strategy, and anti-jamming. MERLIN outperforms GPT-5, Claude-4-Sonnet, Gemini-2.5-Pro on all reasoning tasks. Gestures at a future where electromagnetic warfare is dominated by AI systems working faster than humans can react.
