Wednesday, July 15, 2026

A quieter day: Transformer's deep dive on whether data will bottleneck the intelligence explosion, Scott Alexander defends Plan A's chip rules, and Concordia's annual China AI-safety report drops.

Transformer Feature

  • Could a data bottleneck slow the superintelligence race? Transformer Careful survey of the optimist-vs-skeptic split on recursive self-improvement. Optimists (Davidson) think synthetic data + RL environments crack sample efficiency fast; skeptics (Reed, Dwarkesh) argue AI R&D and real-world skills need "mind-stretching amounts of human expert trajectories" that don't yet exist. Notably, those most worried about fast takeoff are relieved by bottlenecks — Kokotajlo compared longer timelines to "a better prognosis from my doctor."

AI Safety & Governance

  • Concordia AI's "State of AI Safety in China" 2026 report is out X: kelmgren The yearly must-read from a key institution in China's safety ecosystem — useful reference for Kairos's fieldbuilding view of the global landscape.
  • DeepMind: AI-for-science faces a "validation bottleneck" X: GoogleDeepMind New essay argues agents can propose hypotheses and design experiments, but real-world testing is the hard part; lays out four priorities for policymakers and funders (echoes the Transformer piece above).
  • New report: "Underwriting the AI Agent Economy" X: Rajiv Dattani FT-covered blueprint for a private-governance stack (incident data, standards, audits, cat modeling) using insurance markets as an underrated AI-governance lever. Stellar author list; Casper: "insurers probably need to be ready for billions of agents soon."
  • Update — Demis's governance plan draws "too little, too late" X: Nate Soares Following yesterday's essay, Soares and Aaron Scher argue a partially-voluntary standards body has no plan for what to do once a model is found dangerous, and that "experts disagree" glosses over a 5%-vs-≥50% catastrophe split. Sam Altman and Satya Nadella both praised the piece.

Policy Opinion

  • Scott Alexander: "AI Chip Regulation Is Not A Dystopian Surveillance State" ACX Point-by-point defense of Plan A's chip rules: they roughly mirror how controlled substances (Adderall/Xanax) are already regulated, would raise chip prices "a few percent," and wouldn't touch your laptop. Notes the Trump admin quietly enacted KYC-style chip controls this January — and that "you already live in a global panopticon" via bank KYC and chat monitoring.
