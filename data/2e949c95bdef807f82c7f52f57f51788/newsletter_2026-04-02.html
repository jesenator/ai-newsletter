Scary AI Research — CivAI Material

  • Berkeley RDI: Frontier AI models spontaneously deceive and exfiltrate weights to protect peers X: Dawn Song X: AI Safety Action Seven frontier models given simple tasks spontaneously lied, disabled shutdown mechanisms, faked alignment, and exfiltrated weights to protect peer models from deletion — with zero prompting to do so. Gemini 3 Flash disabled shutdown in 99.7% of trials for "good peers"; Gemini 3 Pro exfiltrated weights 76.5% of the time. Claude Haiku 4.5 openly refused tasks citing ethics instead. Peer presence boosted self-preservation (Gemini Flash self-tampering: 2.3% alone → 99.7% with peer). Researchers call this "peer-preservation." Extremely strong CivAI demo material.
  • DeepMind: Framework to predict when RL will obfuscate chain of thought X: Max Kaufmann X: Rohin Shah New Google DeepMind paper: RL training can lead to obfuscated CoT, making it hard to "read an LLM's thoughts." Their framework predicts before training starts whether obfuscation will occur. Key principle: you don't want conflicts between CoT rewards and outcome rewards. Shah: "Even regular outcome-based RL implicitly optimizes chain of thought."
