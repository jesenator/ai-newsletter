Safety Research

  • How much can models reason without any chain-of-thought? LessWrong New Redwood/Astra/Aether/MATS paper (Ward, Greenblatt et al.) measures no-CoT task-completion time horizons across 43 benchmarks. GPT-5.5 reliably handles tasks that take humans ~3 min with zero externalized reasoning; the no-CoT horizon doubles ~every 373 days, extrapolating to ~25 min by 2030. Implication: CoT monitoring may quietly become much less effective even without neuralese. They urge labs to track no-CoT horizons as a lower bound on hidden reasoning.
  • UK AISI: do lie-detectors scale to frontier open-weight models? X: Alan Cooney New AISI paper tests whether activation-based lie detection still works not just when a model is prompted to lie, but when it's trained to lie and believes the opposite of what it says — a harder, more realistic threat model.
  • Buck Shlegeris: labs will impose "totally insane" takeover risk Redwood X: Charbel-Raphaël Segerie Argues regulation won't end up haggling over 10% vs 20% AI takeover risk per year — the real levels labs will impose are high enough that "if governments agreed with me, they would take much more drastic action on international coordination."
