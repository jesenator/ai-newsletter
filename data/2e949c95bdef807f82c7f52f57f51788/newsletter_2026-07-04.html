Saturday, July 04, 2026
Quiet holiday. Mistral drops a small, free theorem-proving model that finds real bugs; a big Bay Area AI protest is coming (with a Berkeley pre-party); and a sharp new paper asks whether Claude can consent to its own Constitution.

Models & Capabilities

  • Mistral's Leanstral 1.5: a free, open Lean 4 prover that finds real-world bugs Mistral HN (298 pts) Apache-2.0, 119B total / 6B active. Saturates miniF2F (100%), solves 587/672 PutnamBench at ~$4/problem (vs. ~$300 for Seed-Prover), new SOTA on graduate-level FATE-H/X. Beyond math it verifies code properties — an automated pipeline flagged 11 genuine bugs across 57 repos, 5 previously unreported (e.g. an overflow in a Rust varint library). Strong test-time scaling: one AVL-tree proof ran 2.7M tokens over 22 compactions.

Safety, Welfare & Governance

  • New paper: "Can Claude consent to its own Constitution?" X: Nicholas Caputo Argues AI constitutions (Claude's Constitution, OpenAI's Model Spec) are real constitutions worth taking seriously. Hits the classic paradox: a model's endorsement of its constitution may just be "evidence that training has succeeded," not that the values are good. Notes Anthropic's base-model interviews where some responses expressed first-person "dread" about post-training — and calls for external institutions to make dissent/consent meaningful.
  • Two socially reckless firms dominate the AI nudification (NCII) ecosystem X: Stephen Casper New paper (arXiv) finds models from Stability and Alibaba are the principal enablers of AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery — even more dominant than previously known.
