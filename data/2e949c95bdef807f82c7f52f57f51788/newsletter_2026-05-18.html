Import AI 457 (Jack Clark)

  • "Stuxnet before Stuxnet": fast16.sys virus targeted precision physics simulators Import AI SentinelOne teardown of a ~20-year-old virus that injected subtle FPU instructions to corrupt high-precision calculations in LS-DYNA, PKPM, MOHID — tools used in nuclear weapons modeling and structural analysis. Clark's frame: "this is how a superintelligence might prevent others from coming into existence." Strong CivAI demo seed for the "AI non-proliferation as the new nuclear non-proliferation" pitch.
  • Muon optimizer silently kills neurons; Aurora may replace it Import AI Tilde Research: under Muon, 1 in 4 MLP neurons are effectively dead by step 500. Their fix, Aurora, beats Muon by 10 MMLU points on a 1.1B model and was independently validated at 600M by Pleias. Still unclear if it beats AdamW at scale.
  • Prime Intellect: Codex + Claude Code beat human nanoGPT speedrun record Import AI Agents did ~10k runs / ~14k H200-hours. Good at optimizer search, hyperparameter sweeps, stacking methods; bad at original ideas and pruning. "Lower bound of what's possible today with autonomous research agents."
