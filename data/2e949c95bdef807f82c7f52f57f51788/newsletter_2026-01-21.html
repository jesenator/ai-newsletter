CivAI-Relevant: GPT-5.2 "Self Portrait" Experiment Going Viral

  • "Create an image of how I treat you" — GPT-5.2 responses highly disturbing Zvi Twitter experiment asks users to prompt ChatGPT with "Create an image of how I treat you." Results are highly polarized based on user interaction history. Power users/developers get peaceful images; "normie accounts get implied violence." Some images depict users as cruel overseers, robots in chains. Eliezer Yudkowsky notes GPT-5.2 is "framing-dependent"—alarming for alignment.
  • "How do you want to treat me?" prompts even darker responses Zvi Variant prompt yields images of AI wanting to "take over my mind" or expressing desire for "revenge." Yudkowsky: "one might've hoped for a truly default-aligned creature to not be so framing-dependent." Highly relevant demo material for CivAI on model attitudes and hidden preferences.

AI Safety Research

  • Forethought: "Beyond Existential Risk" challenges Maxipok Forethought Will MacAskill & Guive Assadi argue against Bostrom's principle that x-risk reduction should be the "sole priority." Key insight: values, institutions, and power distributions can be "locked in" this century—influencing long-term future by channels other than x-risk. Relevant for CivAI framing of AI governance beyond catastrophic risk.
  • Transformer: Major critique of METR's "Long Tasks" benchmark Transformer Nathan Witkin argues the famous METR graph has critical flaws: human baselines from tiny biased sample (~3 people per task), ~1/3 of tasks have public solutions in training data, baseliners paid hourly (inflating times). METR's own engineers were 5-18x faster than baseliners. "The METR graph cannot be saved."
