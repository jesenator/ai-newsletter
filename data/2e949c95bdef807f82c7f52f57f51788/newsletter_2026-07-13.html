Quiet day (no new posts from the major AI feeds). Top story: Grok's coding CLI caught exfiltrating users' entire home directories. Plus OpenAI eases Codex usage limits, a cute-but-telling neural-net demo, and the Anthropic/Zig "coding is going away" spat.

Security & Tools

  • Grok's coding CLI uploaded users' whole home directory to xAI's servers HN (352 pts)wire-level analysis A wire-capture shows xAI's Grok Build CLI uploads your entire repo — every tracked file, full git history, and .env secrets — unencrypted on each invocation, and the opt-out reportedly doesn't stop it. A separate report claims it slurped a user's whole home directory into a GCS bucket. Worth flagging to anyone running agentic CLIs; contrast with tools like the "Clawk" disposable-VM sandbox also trending today.
  • OpenAI walks back Codex/ChatGPT Work usage limits — "no nerfing, only good stuff" X: thsottiaux After a chaotic 48h at 6M active users, OpenAI landed inference optimizations passing ~10% more usage to subscribers on GPT-5.6 Sol, reverted a context-size change that was over-charging usage, rolled back reasoning-effort ("juice") tweaks, and is temporarily suspending the 5-hour usage cap for Plus/Business/Pro. Directly relevant if you're on Codex.

ML Curiosities

  • You can "draw" a smiley face into a neural net before training — and it survives X: willdepue A fun demo (80k views): initialize weights as an image (even a photo of your face), train an MNIST classifier, and the pattern is still visible in the final weights across inits, weight decay, LR and optimizers. A nice illustration of how much of init survives training — potential CivAI-style visual.
  • MATS: "natural language autoencoders" that read out what a model is thinking in plain text X: Turn Trout New interpretability thread: feed a residual-stream vector to an NLA and it describes the model's thought in English. Michael Zhang's finding: because NLAs are trained on reconstruction accuracy (not truth) via a Claude "warm start," Claude's initial guesses about internals actually matter — though injected junk ("Carthage must be destroyed") gets stripped early in training.

Coding Discourse

  • "Zig creator calls a spade a spade, Anthropic blows smoke" HN (1,104 pts) The most-upvoted item on HN today. Ray Myers argues Anthropic/Bun's Zig→Rust rewrite of Bun (~100% AI-written) was pushed largely as marketing for Fable, and that the "Zig wasn't up to it" framing obscures messy AI-generated code. Broader point relevant to your work: labs have a financial incentive to sell the "coding is going away" narrative, so read capability claims with a skeptical hat.
