CivAI-Relevant: "How AI Is Learning to Think in Secret"

  • LessWrong curated post on Thinkish, Neuralese, and the end of readable reasoning LessWrong Excellent explainer by Nicholas Andresen. Key insight: Chain-of-Thought was "cosmically lucky"—an accident where smarter-by-thinking-longer happened to produce readable reasoning. Now facing pressures toward opacity: "Thinkish" (compressed pseudo-English) and "Neuralese" (internal vectors). OpenAI's "Monitoring Monitorability" paper finds current models are "fairly, not perfectly" monitorable (G-mean² of 0.65-0.82). Highly relevant demo material for CivAI on why AI transparency is fragile.
  • Key quote on the coordination problem LessWrong "Right now, the [monitorability] tax is small. Frontier labs can pay it and remain competitive...But suppose Neuralese cracks the scaling problem. If thinking-in-vectors proves substantially faster, cheaper, more capable...the tax explodes."
