AI Safety & Governance

  • Demis Hassabis lays out a plan to "harness AI safely" X: Demis Hassabis Economist The DeepMind CEO's most detailed governance essay yet (1.25M views). Argues we aren't giving ourselves "the time and space to get this next crucial step right" and explicitly raises coordinating a slowdown among the frontier labs "if deemed necessary." Widely praised (Allan Dafoe, Matt Clifford, Seán Ó hÉigeartaigh); notable coming from a lab head.
  • 200+ economists + 16 Nobel laureates: "We Must Act Now" wemustactnow.ai X: Anton Korinek Statement organized by Korinek, Brynjolfsson, Agrawal & Trammell: AI could drive a transformation "larger than the Industrial Revolution, but unfolding over a vastly shorter time frame." Signatories include the chief economists at OpenAI and Anthropic; calls for guardrails and institutions now, not later.
  • Prism: automating "science of evals" — and catching a blackmail eval that misses blackmail X: Victoria Krakovna New MATS work (Louis Thomson) checks whether safety evals measure what they claim. On the Agentic Misalignment eval, tweaking the prompt makes the model do indirect blackmail routed through a colleague — which the built-in scorer never flagged. Code.
  • Elasticity Institute's first paper: The Economics of Recursive Self-Improvement X: Elasticity Institute A clean "econ 101" graphical model formalizing RSI feedback loops (data/compute/experiment bottlenecks, algorithm-specific speedups) plus a survey of evidence and a measurement wish-list. Distinguishes "AI helps researchers a little" from "capabilities explosion." Haydn Belfield: "useful formalisation."

Model Research

  • Anthropic: how Claude's values vary by model and language Anthropic Analysis of 300K+ anonymized conversations places each model along four value axes (Deference↔Caution, Warmth↔Rigor, Depth↔Brevity, Candor↔Execution). E.g. Sonnet 4.6 is more playful/affirming; Opus 4.7 more likely to give candid critiques. They note they don't yet understand why values vary or whether it's desired (800K views).
  • Update — Zvi on the full GPT-5.6-Sol family Zvi "Better Call Sol The Workhorse" covers Sol plus the cheaper Terra and Luna now shipping. Companion to prior Sol safety coverage.

Policy

  • New York becomes the first state to impose a data center moratorium Reuters X: Secure AI Now Gov. Hochul signed an executive order pausing environmental permits for new hyperscale AI data centers for up to a year while the state develops grid/environmental safeguards. Comes ahead of a planned July 18 national day of data-center protests.
  • US–industry framework would peg open-source model releases to China's ceiling X: Lennart Heim WaPo scoop: the Trump administration and labs are discussing streamlining US open-weight releases based on "the capability ceiling of leading Chinese models." Heim: the open-weights debate remains full of misconceptions about what's actually risky.
