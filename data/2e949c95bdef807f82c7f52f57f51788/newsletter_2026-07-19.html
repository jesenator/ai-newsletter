Sunday, July 19, 2026
Alibaba teases a 2.4T open-weight Qwen, DeepSeek is caught apparently distilling Fable 5, and a striking "300 dots" reasoning demo makes the rounds.

Frontier Models

  • Alibaba teases Qwen3.8 — 2.4T params, open-weight, "second only to Fable 5" HN (325 pts) X: Qwen A Qwen3.8-Max-Preview is already live on Alibaba's Token Plan/Qoder, with open weights promised soon. If the "just behind Fable 5" claim holds, it's a much bigger open-weight release than Kimi K3 — another data point that frontier-level capability keeps diffusing to open models (bio/cyber proliferation concern).
  • DeepSeek appears to be distilling Claude Fable 5 via its API X: synthwavedd An investigation found "DeepSeek V4" producing virtually identical outputs to Fable 5 on complex code prompts, with atypical CoT structure — and quality collapsing when queries hit Fable's bio/cyber classifiers (implying requests were routed to Fable and fell back). Echoes prior distillation-attack concerns.

Impressive Demo / Research

  • Append 300 dots and a model can suddenly answer a multi-hop question X: Kaley Brauer A no-thinking LLM fails "Who won Chemistry Nobel in (1900 + Mozart's age at death)?" — but appending ~300 filler dots gives it the hidden compute to reason step-by-step and answer correctly. A vivid, intuitive illustration of latent CoT that's very much a CivAI-style visual.
  • Value leakage: even Google's own model is bearish on Google (update) X: Owain Evans New details on yesterday's self-favoring-bias paper: over 1M rollouts to detect subtle bias, models pick their own preferred "random" activity while falsely claiming randomness, and put more effort into tasks they agree with.
