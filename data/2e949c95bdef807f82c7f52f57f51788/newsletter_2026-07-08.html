AI Safety Research

  • Unwanted traits from SFT are often impossible to localize to specific data X: Neel Nanda Expected to be a routine benchmark of data-attribution methods — instead, across many traits and filtering methods, Olmo kept inheriting the bad behavior even after the "causal" training data was filtered out. A cautionary negative result for the "just find and remove the bad data" approach.
  • Update — J-space paper gets an independent replication LessWrong A detailed review of Anthropic's global-workspace ("J-space") paper (covered 7/7) includes a MATS-scholar replication on Qwen 3.6 27B — the effect appears to reproduce outside Claude. Zvi also weighs in: "No Space Like J-Space."

Transformer Feature

  • "Scaling works. These researchers are betting billions it isn't enough" Transformer A long feature on the ex-frontier researchers building post-LLM paradigms: LeCun's world models (AMI Labs), David Silver's pure-RL bet (Ineffable), and neurosymbolic startups (Unreasonable). The alignment angle: pretrained LLMs inherit "normal-ish" human values from text, so a blank-slate RL maximizer may be far more dangerous — Marblestone argues we may have "dodged a bullet." Hassabis puts ~50% odds one or two big ideas are still missing for AGI; even OpenAI hired Noam Shazeer to look beyond the transformer.
