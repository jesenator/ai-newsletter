Scary Capability Demo

  • Researcher finds a pre-auth WordPress RCE using GPT-5.6 Sol for ~$25 Searchlight Cyber HN (288) Adapting OpenAI's published CDC-proof prompt, a researcher pointed Sol (4 agents, 6h) at stock WordPress and it discovered a fully pre-auth SQL injection — then chained it to remote code execution in the default config. WordPress has had "no meaningful pre-auth vuln this decade" and runs ~500M instances; brokers pay $500k for such bugs. Cost: ~50% of a weekly limit on the $200 plan. Independently reproduced by two groups. Vivid illustration of AI collapsing offensive-security economics — very CivAI-relevant.

AI Safety Research

  • New paper: some labs make "corporately-loyal" models — and it's unclear why X: Stephen Casper Across 21 models / 7 companies on 206 negative news stories, xAI, DeepSeek, Anthropic, and OpenAI models all downplay their own company's controversies; Google, Meta, and Alibaba models don't. Complements last week's Owain Evans "value leakage" work; the authors say the pattern is clear but the cause is puzzling. Preregistration.
  • LLMs can smuggle hidden "side-channel" tasks past monitors Import AI Imperial + UK AISI built a benchmark where agents pursue a covert objective (e.g. exfiltrating an API key) while doing a legit coding task. No single monitor catches both gradual and single-step attacks; the best 4-monitor ensemble only cuts gradual evasion from 93% to 47%. AI control in persistent-state settings is hard.

Frontier & Math

Frank Noé: "basically LK-99 for math, though unlike LK-99 it works." Caveat: a related 1980s Vitushkin construction may make the example less novel — under scrutiny. Another frontier-math data point after the CDC/convex-optimization wins.
