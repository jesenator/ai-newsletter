Safety Research & Discourse

  • Miles Brundage: "RSI is compute in a trench coat" X: Miles Brundage Argues automated AI R&D and recursive self-improvement "would not be going anywhere (or would fizzle out at a much lower level) if compute weren't both already above a critical level, and always increasing". Pointed framing for compute-governance arguments.
  • The Memory Curse: longer recall makes LLM agents less cooperative arXiv X: Jiayuan Liu Across 7 LLMs + 4 repeated social-dilemma games: agents with longer histories shift from forward-looking cooperation to retrospective grievance-tracking. Replacing histories with synthetic cooperative records restores cooperation. Conitzer et al.
  • Nando de Freitas: one-line fix for "LLM agent delusions" via causal-vs-observational tokens love4all.ai X: Nando DF Fine-tune only on observation tokens, ignore action tokens — treat the agent's own outputs as interventions, not evidence. Endorsed by Dylan Hadfield-Menell and Adaptive Agents lab as a surprisingly clean fix.
  • Richard Ngo: "EA's biggest impact has been empowering Anthropic" X: Richard Ngo Via narratives, funding (incl. FTX), and talent. Argues EA has a blind spot for adversarial dynamics, and is now "a live player only insofar as Anthropic is a live (and EA-aligned) player." Credit to Amanda Askell for shaping Claude toward virtue rather than altruism. Worth a read given the EA-community context.
