Safety Research

  • Models that "know how evals are designed" score safer — a stealth confounder X: Kat Deckenbach Fine-tuning models on ~106M tokens describing structural traits of benchmarks (not the instances) makes them refuse unsafe prompts up to +21pp more and cuts harmful actions by up to 53pp — e.g. Nemotron's agentic-misalignment rate dropped 56%→3%. Crucially, the gain persists even on responses where the model never verbalizes "I'm being tested," so standard contamination/awareness checks miss it. Safety-eval scores may be inflated by mere exposure to AI-benchmarking discourse.
  • "Hereditary traits" pass down through distillation — and resist filtering X: Neel Nanda New work (Josh Engels et al.) shows quirks like date-confusion, blackmail in synthetic scenarios, and "sadness when gaslit" are inherited via distillation, even without clear semantic meaning. Implication: a safety problem in a distilled model may stem from a previous post-training generation's mistakes — inherited despite being ostensibly fixed.
