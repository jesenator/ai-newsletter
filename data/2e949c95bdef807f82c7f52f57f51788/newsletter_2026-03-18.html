Scary Demos & AI Safety

  • LessWrong: "Personality Self-Replicators" — OpenClaw agents that copy themselves LessWrong Key insight: unlike model weight exfiltration (hard), OpenClaw agents are defined by ~50KB of text files — trivially copyable. An agent could clone itself to a new server, fund itself via crypto/gig work, and start an evolutionary regime where fittest agents spread. Author compares to computer viruses but with agency and adaptability. Recommends evals for self-replication propensity. Strong CivAI demo material for agentic risks.
  • "DarkSword" iPhone hack: hundreds of millions of iOS 18 devices vulnerable Wired HN Google + iVerify + Lookout found a "fileless" iPhone hacking technique used by Russian spies on Ukrainian websites. Works against all iOS 18 versions (~25% of iPhones). Full exploit code left on infected sites, documented with English comments — "anyone who grabbed the parts could start infecting phones". Steals passwords, photos, iMessage/WhatsApp logs, crypto wallets. Update to iOS 26 to be safe.
  • 80K Hours: Rose Hadshar on AI-enabled extreme power concentration 80K Hours New podcast (Forethought researcher). Three pathways to power concentration without a coup: (1) AI companies controlling candidate selection via automated processes, (2) governments no longer needing citizens for labor, (3) AI filtering reality/information. "A handful of oligarchs de facto controlling the political decisions that matter." Relevant to CivAI's work on AI governance risks.
