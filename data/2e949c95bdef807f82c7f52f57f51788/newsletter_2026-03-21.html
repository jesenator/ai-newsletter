White House AI Legislative Framework — Major Policy Move

  • White House releases 4-page AI policy framework pushing state law preemption Transformer Zvi White House PDF Released Friday. Covers child safety, energy, IP, free speech — but no mention of frontier model risk, biorisk, loss of control, or transparency requirements. Calls for full federal preemption of state AI laws and no new regulatory agency. Transformer's read: designed to drive a partisan wedge and counter Sen. Blackburn's broader bill (which includes AGI provisions, Section 230 sunset, and frontier reporting requirements). Brad Carson calls it "Section 230 on testosterone." Sen. Ted Cruz expected to lead the legislative push. House leadership already endorsed it. Zvi: "Support this if and only if you think 'ban states from regulating AI without actually regulating most of AI' is a good plan." Extremely relevant for CivAI's policy communication work — the framework would effectively kill SB 53 and the RAISE Act with no substitute for frontier risk.

  • LessWrong: "Terrified Comments on Corrigibility in Claude's Constitution" LessWrong Detailed critique of how Anthropic's Constitution handles corrigibility. Key argument: the Constitution muddies "corrigibility" by simultaneously wanting Claude to defer to humans AND be an independent moral agent. Author notes the document "sounds like the humans are begging" — e.g. "we would like AI models to defer to us... or at least to not attempt to actively undermine our efforts." Argues this reveals Anthropic doesn't have a True Name for corrigibility and is hoping cooperation with Claude induces further cooperation. Calls for stronger emphasis on corrigibility "if it's not already too late." Highly relevant for CivAI framing of alignment challenges.
